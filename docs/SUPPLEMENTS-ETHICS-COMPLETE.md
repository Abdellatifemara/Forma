## Overview

This document provides the complete ethical classification, contraindications, and recommendations for all supplements in FitApp. It answers all questions from the Research Brief.

**Key Principles:**
- Trainer-gated feature (only verified trainers can recommend)
- Health screening required before any recommendations
- Educational content available to all users
- When in doubt, err on side of caution

## 1. Protein Powder (Whey, Casein, Plant-based)

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Provides amino acids for muscle protein synthesis
- **Benefits:** Supports muscle recovery, convenient protein source, helps meet daily protein needs
- **Dosage:** 20-40g per serving, 1-3 servings daily based on total protein needs
- **Timing:** Post-workout (within 2 hours), between meals, or before bed (casein)

### Ethical Rating: GREEN

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Low
- **Requires blood test:** No
- **Medical territory:** No
- **Drug interactions:** None significant
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Dairy allergy (whey/casein)
  - Soy allergy (some plant proteins)
  - Severe kidney disease (consult doctor for protein intake)

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes
- **Screening required:** Allergy check (dairy, soy)
- **Trainer-only:** No (educational content for all)
- **Special notes:** Specify plant-based options for dairy allergies

## 2. Creatine Monohydrate

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Increases phosphocreatine stores for ATP regeneration during high-intensity exercise
- **Benefits:** Increased strength, power output, muscle mass, cognitive benefits
- **Dosage:** 3-5g daily (no loading phase necessary)
- **Timing:** Any time of day, with or without food

### Ethical Rating: YELLOW

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Low-Medium
- **Requires blood test:** No (but kidney function check recommended if concerns)
- **Medical territory:** No
- **Drug interactions:**
  - NSAIDs (potential kidney stress)
  - Diuretics (dehydration risk)
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Pre-existing kidney disease
  - Taking nephrotoxic medications

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes, with screening
- **Screening required:**
  - Kidney disease question
  - Current medications
  - Adequate hydration reminder
- **Trainer-only:** Yes (for recommendation)
- **Special notes:** One of the most researched supplements, very safe for healthy individuals

---

## 3. Omega-3 / Fish Oil

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Essential fatty acids (EPA/DHA) for inflammation, brain, heart health
- **Benefits:** Reduced inflammation, cardiovascular support, joint health, cognitive function
- **Dosage:** 1-3g combined EPA/DHA daily
- **Timing:** With meals (better absorption, reduces fishy burps)

### Ethical Rating: YELLOW

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Low-Medium
- **Requires blood test:** No
- **Medical territory:** No
- **Drug interactions:**
  - Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin) - increased bleeding risk
  - Blood pressure medications (additive effect)
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Shellfish/fish allergy
  - Bleeding disorders
  - Upcoming surgery (stop 2 weeks before)

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes, with screening
- **Screening required:**
  - Blood thinner medication check
  - Fish/shellfish allergy
- **Trainer-only:** No (but trainer adds to plan)
- **Special notes:** Offer algae-based options for vegetarians/allergies

- **Benefits:** Insurance against deficiencies, general health support
- **Dosage:** 1 tablet/serving daily as directed on label

### Ethical Rating: GREEN

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Low

### Recommendation for FitApp

## 5. Vitamin D3

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Hormone precursor for calcium absorption, immune function, mood
- **Benefits:** Bone health, immune support, mood regulation, muscle function
- **Dosage:** 1000-4000 IU daily (higher doses need monitoring)
- **Timing:** With fatty meal for absorption

### Ethical Rating: YELLOW

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Medium (at high doses)
- **Requires blood test:** Recommended for doses >4000 IU
- **Medical territory:** Yes, for therapeutic high-dose protocols
- **Drug interactions:**
  - Steroids (reduce absorption)
  - Cholesterol medications (statins)
  - Thiazide diuretics (hypercalcemia risk)
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Hypercalcemia
  - Kidney disease
  - Sarcoidosis
  - Hyperparathyroidism

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes, with limits
- **Screening required:**
  - Kidney disease
  - Current calcium levels (if known)
  - Medications
- **Trainer-only:** Yes for recommendation (education for all)
- **Special notes:**
  - App should NOT recommend >4000 IU without blood test
  - Common deficiency in Egypt (indoor lifestyle, clothing)

- **Benefits:** Immune function, skin health, iron absorption, antioxidant

## 7. Vitamin A

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Vision, immune function, skin health, cell growth
- **Benefits:** Eye health, immune support, skin integrity
- **Dosage:** 700-900 mcg RAE daily (3000 mcg upper limit)
- **Timing:** With fatty meal

### Ethical Rating: YELLOW

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Medium-High (fat soluble, accumulates)
- **Requires blood test:** No, but caution with high doses
- **Medical territory:** Yes, at therapeutic doses
- **Drug interactions:**
  - Retinoid medications (additive toxicity)
  - Tetracycline antibiotics (increased intracranial pressure)
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - **PREGNANCY** (teratogenic at high doses)
  - Liver disease
  - Hypervitaminosis A

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes, with strong warnings
- **Screening required:**
  - Pregnancy/trying to conceive (MANDATORY)
  - Liver disease
  - Current medications
- **Trainer-only:** Yes
- **Special notes:**
  - NEVER recommend to pregnant women or those trying to conceive
  - Beta-carotene (provitamin A) is safer alternative

## 10. Vitamin K2

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Directs calcium to bones, away from arteries
- **Benefits:** Bone health, cardiovascular health, works with D3
- **Dosage:** 100-200 mcg daily (MK-7 form preferred)
- **Timing:** With fatty meal, often paired with D3

### Ethical Rating: YELLOW

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** Low-Medium
- **Requires blood test:** No
- **Medical territory:** No
- **Drug interactions:**
  - **WARFARIN** (directly opposes its action)
  - Other blood thinners
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Anyone on warfarin/Coumadin
  - Blood clotting disorders (some)

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** Yes, with screening
- **Screening required:**
  - Blood thinner medications (CRITICAL)
- **Trainer-only:** Yes
- **Special notes:** Excellent when paired with D3 and calcium

## 13. Iron

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Oxygen transport (hemoglobin), energy production
- **Benefits:** Prevents/treats anemia, energy, athletic performance
- **Dosage:** 8-18mg daily (45mg upper limit)
- **Timing:** Empty stomach or with vitamin C, NOT with calcium/coffee/tea

### Ethical Rating: ORANGE

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** HIGH
- **Requires blood test:** YES (MANDATORY)
- **Medical territory:** YES
- **Drug interactions:**
  - Antacids (reduced absorption)
  - Levothyroxine (thyroid medication)
  - Many antibiotics
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Hemochromatosis (iron overload)
  - Hemosiderosis
  - Repeated blood transfusions
  - Thalassemia (some forms)

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** LIMITED - Education only
- **Screening required:**
  - BLOOD TEST REQUIRED (ferritin, serum iron)
  - Known hemochromatosis
  - Mediterranean/Middle Eastern ancestry (thalassemia risk)
- **Trainer-only:** Yes, AND requires blood test confirmation
- **Special notes:**
  - NEVER recommend iron without blood test
  - Iron toxicity can be fatal
  - Common misconception that everyone needs iron

## 15. Potassium

### Basic Info
- **Function:** Electrolyte balance, muscle/nerve function, blood pressure
- **Benefits:** Blood pressure regulation, muscle function, heart rhythm
- **Dosage:** Food sources preferred; supplements typically 99mg (regulatory limit)
- **Timing:** With meals

### Ethical Rating: ORANGE

### Risk Assessment
- **Harm potential:** HIGH (cardiac risk)
- **Requires blood test:** Recommended for supplementation
- **Medical territory:** YES
- **Drug interactions:**
  - ACE inhibitors (hyperkalemia risk)
  - Potassium-sparing diuretics
  - ARBs (blood pressure meds)
- **Contraindicated for:**
  - Kidney disease
  - On potassium-sparing medications
  - Addison's disease
  - Hyperkalemia

### Recommendation for FitApp
- **Include in app:** LIMITED - Education only, food sources
- **Screening required:**
  - Kidney disease (MANDATORY)
  - Blood pressure medications
  - Heart conditions
- **Trainer-only:** Yes (supplement form)
- **Special notes:**
  - Recommend food sources (bananas, potatoes, coconut water)
  - Supplement potassium only in electrolyte blends
  - High potassium can cause fatal heart arrhythmias

# PART 3: HEALTH SCREENING QUESTIONS

## Final Screening Question List

### MANDATORY Questions (Must Ask Before Any Supplement)

```
1. Do you have any kidney disease or kidney problems?
   Block if YES: Creatine, high-protein, potassium, magnesium, many others

2. Do you have any liver disease or liver problems?
   Block if YES: Vitamin A, glutamine, many processed supplements

3. Do you have high blood pressure (hypertension)?
   Block if YES: Pre-workouts, high-sodium electrolytes
   Caution for: Stimulants, high-dose omega-3

4. Do you have any heart conditions or history of heart problems?
   Block if YES: Pre-workouts, stimulants
   Caution for: Potassium, high-dose electrolytes

5. Are you pregnant, breastfeeding, or trying to conceive?
   Block if YES: Pre-workouts, vitamin A, ashwagandha, melatonin
   Caution for: Most supplements

6. Are you under 18 years old?
   Block if YES: Pre-workouts, ashwagandha
   Caution for: Most performance supplements

7. Do you have any thyroid conditions?
   Block if YES (hyperthyroid): Ashwagandha
   Caution for: Selenium, iodine-containing supps

8. Do you have any autoimmune conditions?
   Block if YES: Ashwagandha, immune-stimulating supplements
   Caution for: Melatonin
```

### MEDICATION Questions (Block or Warn Based on Interaction)

```
9. Are you taking any of these medications?

   [ ] Blood thinners (warfarin, Coumadin, aspirin daily, Plavix)
       Block: Vitamin K2, high-dose omega-3, vitamin E

   [ ] Blood pressure medications
       Caution: Pre-workouts, potassium, high sodium

   [ ] Diabetes medications (insulin, metformin, etc.)
       Caution: Ashwagandha, melatonin, chromium

   [ ] Thyroid medications (levothyroxine, etc.)
       Caution: Calcium (spacing), iron (spacing), ashwagandha

   [ ] Antidepressants or anti-anxiety medications
       Caution: Pre-workouts, melatonin

   [ ] Antibiotics (currently taking)
       Caution: Zinc, calcium, magnesium (spacing needed)

   [ ] Immunosuppressants
       Caution: Ashwagandha, immune-stimulating supps

   [ ] Other medications (specify)
       Flag for trainer review
```

### ALLERGY Questions

```
10. Do you have any of these allergies?

    [ ] Dairy/lactose
        Block: Whey protein, casein
        Alternative: Plant protein, lactose-free whey

    [ ] Shellfish
        Block: Marine collagen, some omega-3
        Alternative: Bovine collagen, algae omega-3

    [ ] Fish
        Block: Fish oil, marine collagen
        Alternative: Algae omega-3, bovine collagen

    [ ] Soy
        Block: Soy protein, some lecithin-containing supps
        Alternative: Other plant proteins

    [ ] Eggs
        Block: Some collagen, some protein blends

    [ ] Other food allergies (specify)
        Flag for trainer review
```

### CONDITION-SPECIFIC Questions

```
11. Do you have any of these conditions?

    [ ] History of kidney stones
        Caution: Calcium, high-dose vitamin C, high protein

    [ ] Gout
        Caution: High protein, creatine (debated)

    [ ] Anemia (low iron)
        Flag: May benefit from iron, but BLOOD TEST REQUIRED

    [ ] Digestive disorders (IBS, Crohn's, ulcerative colitis)
        Caution: Some supplements may irritate; magnesium oxide

    [ ] Depression or anxiety disorder
        Caution: Melatonin, pre-workouts

    [ ] Bleeding disorder
        Block: Omega-3, vitamin E, vitamin K2
```

### NICE-TO-HAVE Questions (Optional)

```
12. What is your current fitness activity level?
    - Helps determine electrolyte needs

13. Do you work outdoors or in hot environments?
    - Increases electrolyte recommendations

14. What is your typical daily protein intake from food?
    - Determines protein powder necessity
```

# PART 4: LEGAL & ETHICAL FRAMEWORK

## Education vs Medical Advice Distinction

### ALLOWED (Education)
- "Vitamin D supports bone health and immune function"
- "Creatine is one of the most researched supplements for strength"

## Trainer Qualification Requirements

### Minimum Requirements to Unlock Supplement Feature
1. Verified trainer status in app
2. Completed app's supplement education module (to be created)
3. Acknowledged legal responsibility and limitations
4. Agreed to never diagnose or prescribe

### What Trainers CAN Do
- Recommend GREEN and YELLOW supplements after screening
- Educate clients about supplements
- Add supplements to client's plan
- Suggest timing and dosage within safe ranges
- Recommend clients see a doctor for testing (iron, vitamin D)

### What Trainers CANNOT Do
- Recommend ORANGE supplements without enhanced screening
- Recommend RED supplements (ever)
- Diagnose deficiencies
- Treat medical conditions with supplements
- Recommend supplements to clients who failed screening
- Override screening blocks

## Never-Include List (RED - Banned from App)

The following should NEVER appear in FitApp:

1. **Hormones**
   - Testosterone boosters (DHEA, etc.)
   - Prohormones
   - Growth hormone
   - Thyroid hormones

2. **Banned Substances**
   - SARMs (Selective Androgen Receptor Modulators)
   - Anabolic steroids
   - Peptides (BPC-157, etc.)
   - EPO

3. **Prescription-Level**
   - Anything requiring a prescription
   - Injectable supplements
   - High-dose single nutrients (therapeutic doses)

4. **Dangerous Stimulants**
   - DMAA
   - DMHA
   - Ephedra/ephedrine
   - Any banned stimulant

5. **Unregulated/Unproven**
   - Weight loss pills
   - "Fat burners" with proprietary blends
   - Testosterone boosters
   - Any supplement with hidden ingredients
