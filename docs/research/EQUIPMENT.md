## Understanding Equipment Selection

|--------|------------------|--------|
| **Budget** | What can I realistically afford? | Determines equipment quality and variety |
| **Space** | How much room do I have? | Limits equipment size and quantity |

## 1. BODYWEIGHT EXERCISES (No Equipment Required)

### Push Muscles (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)

#### Push-Up Variations

**1. Standard Push-Up (Beginner)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Pectoralis major, anterior deltoid |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Triceps, serratus anterior, core |
| **Setup** | Hands shoulder-width apart, body straight from head to heels |
| **Execution** | Lower chest to floor, elbows 45° from body, push back up |
| **Tempo** | 2 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 10-20 reps |
| **Progressions** | Incline (easier) → Standard → Decline (harder) |

**Technique Cues:**
- Keep core engaged (no sagging hips)
- Look slightly ahead, not down
- Full range of motion - chest touches or nearly touches floor
- Fully lock out arms at top

**Common Mistakes:**
- Flared elbows (causes shoulder strain)
- Sagging lower back
- Partial range of motion
- Head dropping forward

---

**2. Wide Push-Up (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Outer pectoralis major |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Anterior deltoid, triceps |
| **Setup** | Hands 1.5x shoulder width apart |
| **Execution** | Lower with control, elbows track toward hands |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-15 reps |

**Key Difference:** Greater chest stretch, more outer chest emphasis, reduced tricep involvement.

---

**3. Diamond Push-Up (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Triceps, inner chest |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Anterior deltoid |
| **Setup** | Hands together forming diamond shape (thumbs and index fingers touching) |
| **Execution** | Lower chest toward hands, keep elbows close to body |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-12 reps |

**Note:** Higher tricep activation than any other push-up variation.

---

**4. Decline Push-Up (Advanced)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Upper chest (clavicular head), anterior deltoid |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Triceps, serratus anterior |
| **Setup** | Feet elevated on chair/bench/step (12-24 inches) |
| **Execution** | Standard push-up movement with elevated feet |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-15 reps |
| **Progression** | Higher elevation = harder |

---

**5. Pike Push-Up (Intermediate-Advanced)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Triceps, upper chest, trapezius |
| **Setup** | Hips high, body forms inverted V, hands shoulder-width |
| **Execution** | Lower head toward floor between hands, push back up |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 6-12 reps |
| **Progression** | Standard → Elevated feet pike → Wall handstand push-ups |

---

**6. Hindu Push-Up (Advanced)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Chest, shoulders, triceps |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Core, hip flexors, hamstrings |
| **Setup** | Start in downward dog position |
| **Execution** | Dive forward, chest swoops near ground, push up into upward dog, return |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-12 reps |

**Benefits:** Full body movement, great for mobility and strength simultaneously.

---

**7. Archer Push-Up (Expert)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Chest, triceps (unilateral focus) |
| **Setup** | Wide hand placement, one arm straight to side |
| **Execution** | Lower toward working arm, other arm stays extended |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 4-8 reps per side |
| **Progression** | Leads to one-arm push-ups |

---

**8. Pseudo Planche Push-Up (Expert)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Anterior deltoid, upper chest |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Triceps, biceps (isometric), core |
| **Setup** | Hands by hips (fingers pointing forward or out), lean forward |
| **Execution** | Push-up while maintaining forward lean |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 5-10 reps |

### Pull Muscles (Back, Biceps)

**1. Inverted Row (Beginner)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Latissimus dorsi, rhomboids, rear deltoid |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Biceps, core |
| **Setup** | Lie under bar/table edge, grip overhand, body straight |
| **Execution** | Pull chest to bar, squeeze shoulder blades, lower with control |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-15 reps |
| **Progressions** | Elevated body (easier) → Feet on floor → Feet elevated |

**Technique Cues:**
- Keep body rigid like a plank
- Initiate pull with shoulder blade retraction
- Chest reaches bar/edge at top
- Control the negative (lowering)

---

**2. Pull-Up (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Latissimus dorsi |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Biceps, rear deltoid, rhomboids, core |
| **Setup** | Overhand grip, slightly wider than shoulders, dead hang |
| **Execution** | Pull chin over bar, lower with control to full extension |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 5-12 reps |

**Technique Cues:**
- Start from dead hang (arms fully extended)
- Pull elbows down and back (not out)
- Chin clearly over bar at top
- Full extension at bottom (no partial reps)
- Avoid kipping/swinging

**Common Mistakes:**
- Partial range of motion
- Excessive kipping
- Not reaching full extension
- Pulling with biceps first (should initiate with lats)

---

**3. Chin-Up (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Biceps brachii, latissimus dorsi |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Rear deltoid, rhomboids |
| **Setup** | Underhand (supinated) grip, shoulder-width apart |
| **Execution** | Pull chin over bar, lower with control |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 6-12 reps |

**Key Difference:** ~20% more bicep activation than pull-ups, generally easier for beginners.

---

**4. Wide-Grip Pull-Up (Advanced)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Latissimus dorsi (width emphasis) |
| **Setup** | Hands 1.5x shoulder width |
| **Execution** | Pull until chin over bar |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 5-10 reps |

---

**5. Close-Grip Pull-Up (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Latissimus dorsi (lower lats), biceps |
| **Setup** | Hands 6-12 inches apart, neutral or underhand grip |
| **Execution** | Pull chin over bar |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 6-12 reps |

---

**6. L-Sit Pull-Up (Advanced)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Lats, biceps, core, hip flexors |
| **Setup** | Standard pull-up grip, raise legs to 90° (L position) |
| **Execution** | Maintain L position throughout pull-up |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 5-8 reps |

---

**7. Archer Pull-Up (Expert)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Unilateral lat focus |
| **Setup** | Wide grip |
| **Execution** | Pull toward one hand while other arm straightens |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 3-6 per side |
| **Progression** | Leads to one-arm pull-ups |

---

**8. Muscle-Up (Expert)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Lats, chest, triceps |
| **Setup** | False grip (wrists over bar) recommended |
| **Execution** | Explosive pull-up transitioning to dip position |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 3-5 reps |
| **Prerequisites** | 15+ pull-ups, 20+ dips |

### Lower Body Exercises

#### Squat Variations

**1. Bodyweight Squat (Beginner)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hamstrings, core, adductors |
| **Setup** | Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out (15-30°) |
| **Execution** | Sit back and down, knees track over toes, thighs to parallel or below |
| **Depth** | Hip crease below knee level ("ass to grass" if mobility allows) |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 15-25 reps |

**Technique Cues:**
- Keep chest up, back neutral
- Weight in mid-foot to heels
- Knees push out, track over toes
- Full depth if mobility allows
- Drive through heels to stand

**Common Mistakes:**
- Knees caving inward
- Heels rising
- Excessive forward lean
- Partial range of motion

---

**2. Jump Squat (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Calves, hamstrings, core |
| **Setup** | Standard squat stance |
| **Execution** | Squat down, explode up, land softly |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 10-15 reps |

**Landing:** Soft knees, absorb impact, reset before next rep.

---

**3. Bulgarian Split Squat (Intermediate)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hamstrings, core, hip flexors |
| **Setup** | Rear foot elevated on bench/chair (laces down or ball of foot) |
| **Execution** | Lower until front thigh parallel, drive through front heel |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 8-12 per leg |

**Stance:** Front foot 2-3 feet ahead of bench, torso slightly forward.

---

**4. Pistol Squat (Expert)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes (single leg) |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hip flexors, core, calves |
| **Setup** | Stand on one leg, other leg extended forward |
| **Execution** | Squat to full depth, stand back up without touching floor |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 3-8 per leg |

**Prerequisites:** Good ankle mobility, hip flexibility, quad strength.

**Progressions:**
1. Assisted pistol (holding support)
2. Box pistol (sit to box)
3. Negative pistol (lower only)
4. Full pistol squat

### Core Exercises

#### Plank Variations

**1. Standard Plank (Beginner)**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Rectus abdominis, transverse abdominis |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Obliques, erector spinae, shoulders |
| **Setup** | Forearms on ground, body straight, on toes |
| **Execution** | Hold position, maintain neutral spine |
| **Target** | 3 sets of 30-60 seconds |

**Technique Cues:**
- Elbows under shoulders
- Squeeze glutes
- Tuck pelvis slightly (posterior tilt)
- Don't let hips sag or pike

## 2. BASIC HOME EQUIPMENT (Under $100)

### Resistance Bands

#### Types of Resistance Bands

| Type | Price | Resistance | Best Uses |
|------|-------|------------|-----------|
| **Loop Bands (Mini)** | $8-15 | 5-50 lbs | Glute activation, warm-ups, mobility |
| **Loop Bands (Large/Monster)** | $15-40 | 15-150 lbs | Pull-up assistance, heavy resistance, stretching |
| **Tube Bands with Handles** | $20-40 | 5-50 lbs | Full body strength, cable exercise alternatives |
| **Therapy/Flat Bands** | $10-20 | Very light | Rehabilitation, physical therapy |
| **Fabric Bands** | $15-25 | Light-medium | Glute work, non-slip option |

#### Band Color Chart (Typical)

| Color | Typical Resistance | Best For |
|-------|-------------------|----------|
| Yellow | 2-5 lbs | Rehabilitation, warm-ups |
| Red | 5-15 lbs | Beginners, upper body isolation |
| Green | 15-25 lbs | Intermediate, general training |
| Blue | 25-40 lbs | Advanced, compound movements |
| Black | 40-60 lbs | Pull-up assistance, heavy work |
| Purple/Silver | 60-100+ lbs | Very heavy resistance |

#### Top Exercises with Resistance Bands

| Exercise | Band Type | Primary Muscles | Sets x Reps |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Banded squat | Loop (mini) | Glutes, quads | 3x12-15 |
| Banded glute bridge | Loop (mini) | Glutes | 3x15-20 |
| Pull-apart | Tube/loop | Rear delts, rhomboids | 3x15-20 |
| Face pull | Tube with handles | Rear delts, rotator cuff | 3x15-20 |
| Banded push-up | Large loop | Chest, triceps | 3x10-15 |
| Assisted pull-up | Large loop | Lats, biceps | 3x8-12 |
| Bicep curl | Tube with handles | Biceps | 3x12-15 |
| Tricep pushdown | Door anchor + tube | Triceps | 3x12-15 |
| Pallof press | Tube/loop | Core anti-rotation | 3x10-12 per side |
| Lat pulldown | Door anchor + tube | Lats | 3x12-15 |

#### Band Training Tips

1. **Accommodating Resistance:** Bands get harder as they stretch - great for power development
2. **Anchor Points:** Door anchor, pole, sturdy furniture, squat rack
3. **Combine with Weights:** Add bands to barbell exercises for variable resistance
4. **Check for Damage:** Inspect bands before use - small tears can cause snapping
5. **Start Light:** Band resistance feels different than weights

**Exercise:** Ab Rollout

| Aspect | Details |

### DIY Sandbag

**Materials:**
- Duffel bag or army bag
- Heavy-duty garbage bags
- Duct tape
- Sand or pea gravel

**Instructions:**
1. Double-bag the sand in garbage bags
2. Fill to desired weight (start 20-40 lbs)
3. Seal with duct tape
4. Place in duffel bag
5. Use for squats, carries, cleans, etc.

**Weight Reference:**
- Sand: ~100 lbs per cubic foot
- Pea gravel: ~96 lbs per cubic foot

---

### DIY TRX Alternative

**Materials:**
- 15-20 ft of climbing rope or cargo straps
- PVC pipe or wooden dowel for handles
- Eye bolt or tree anchor

**Instructions:**
1. Create handles from PVC (4-6 inches)
2. Thread rope through handles
3. Tie secure knots
4. Anchor to door frame, tree, or beam
5. Test with low body weight first

## 11. SAFETY GUIDELINES

### General Safety Rules

1. **Inspect equipment before each use**
2. **Use proper form over heavy weight**
3. **Have a spotter for heavy lifts**
4. **Use safety pins/bars in rack**
5. **Keep floor clear of obstacles**
6. **Stay hydrated**
7. **Warm up before training**
8. **Know your limits**
9. **Don't train to failure alone**
10. **Keep emergency contact nearby**

### Equipment-Specific Safety

| Equipment | Key Safety Points |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Barbells** | Collar plates, use rack safety bars, don't train to failure alone |
| **Dumbbells** | Don't drop on feet, clear floor space, control negative |
| **Pull-up bar** | Check mounting regularly, don't swing excessively |
| **Bench** | Check stability, use spotters for heavy bench |
| **Resistance bands** | Check for tears, anchor securely, don't release under tension |
| **Kettlebells** | Clear swing path, control the bell, wear proper footwear |
| **Cardio machines** | Use safety clip, start slow, know emergency stop |

### When to Seek Professional Help

- Sharp pain during exercise
- Joint swelling or instability
- Difficulty with basic movements
- Dizziness or shortness of breath
- Any injury that doesn't improve in 2-3 days

### First Aid Kit for Home Gym

| Item | Use |
|------|-----|
| Ice packs | Acute injuries |
| Compression bandages | Sprains, strains |
| Athletic tape | Joint support |
| Antiseptic wipes | Cuts, scrapes |
| Pain relief gel | Muscle soreness |
| Blister bandages | Hand protection |
