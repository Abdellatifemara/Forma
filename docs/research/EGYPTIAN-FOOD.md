## Understanding Egyptian Cuisine

### Nutritional Characteristics of Egyptian Food

Egyptian cuisine has evolved over thousands of years, blending influences from Mediterranean, Middle Eastern, and African culinary traditions. Understanding its nutritional profile is essential for effective meal planning.

**Key Nutritional Features:**

| Characteristic | Description | Fitness Implications |
|----------------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Legume-Based** | Heavy reliance on fava beans, lentils, chickpeas | Excellent plant protein, high fiber |
| **Carb-Dense** | Rice, bread, and pasta are staples | Good for energy, needs portion control |
| **Vegetable-Rich** | Abundant vegetables in most dishes | High micronutrient density |
| **Moderate Fat** | Traditionally uses ghee, butter, oils | Calorie-dense, control portions |
| **Herb-Heavy** | Cumin, coriander, dill, parsley | Antioxidants, flavor without calories |
| **Seasonal** | Fresh, local produce preferred | Better nutrient content |

### Traditional Meal Structure

| Meal | Arabic Name | Typical Components | Timing |
|------|-------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Breakfast** | فطار (Fatar) | Ful, eggs, cheese, bread, tea | 7-9 AM |
| **Lunch** | غداء (Ghada) | Main meal - meat/fish with rice/bread | 2-4 PM |
| **Dinner** | عشاء (Asha) | Lighter fare - salads, leftovers | 8-10 PM |
| **Snacks** | سناك (Snak) | Fruits, nuts, tea with biscuits | Between meals |
