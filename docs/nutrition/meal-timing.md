# Meal Timing & Frequency

## Meal Frequency Options

| Pattern | Meals/Day | Best For |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| 3 Meals | 3 | Traditional, simple |
| 3 + 2 Snacks | 5 | Muscle gain, steady energy |
| Intermittent Fasting | 2-3 | Fat loss, busy schedules |
| 6 Small Meals | 6 | Bodybuilders, high calories |

## Pre-Workout Nutrition

### Timing Options
| Time Before | What to Eat | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 2-3 hours | Full meal | Rice + chicken + vegetables |
| 1-2 hours | Moderate meal | Oats + banana + protein |
| 30-60 min | Light snack | Banana, dates, bread |
| Fasted | Nothing | Morning cardio (fat loss) |

### Pre-Workout Meal Composition
- Carbs: 0.5-1g/kg bodyweight
- Protein: 20-30g
- Fat: Low (slows digestion)
- Fiber: Low (avoid bloating)

### Egyptian Pre-Workout Options
| Option | Carbs | Protein | Time Before |
|--------|-------|---------|-------------|
| Baladi bread + eggs | 40g | 20g | 2 hours |
| Oats + milk + banana | 50g | 15g | 1.5 hours |
| Dates (5) + protein shake | 35g | 25g | 1 hour |
| Banana | 27g | 1g | 30 min |

## Post-Workout Nutrition

### The Anabolic Window
- Not as critical as once thought
- Aim to eat within 2 hours
- More important if fasted training
- Total daily intake matters most

### Post-Workout Goals
1. Replenish glycogen (carbs)
2. Stimulate muscle protein synthesis (protein)
3. Rehydrate (water)

### Post-Workout Recommendations
| Macro | Amount | Why |
|-------|--------|-----|
| Protein | 25-40g | Muscle repair |
| Carbs | 0.5-1g/kg | Glycogen refill |
| Fat | Moderate | Not critical timing |

### Egyptian Post-Workout Options
| Option | Protein | Carbs | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|-------|
| Grilled chicken + rice | 35g | 45g | Classic |
| Ful + bread + eggs | 25g | 55g | Budget |
| Protein shake + banana | 30g | 30g | Convenient |
| Fish + potato | 30g | 40g | Lean |

## Intermittent Fasting

### Common Protocols
| Protocol | Fasting | Eating | Description |
|----------|---------|--------|-------------|
| 16:8 | 16 hours | 8 hours | Most popular |
| 18:6 | 18 hours | 6 hours | More aggressive |
| 20:4 | 20 hours | 4 hours | One meal + snack |
| 5:2 | 2 days/week | 5 days | 500-600 cal on fast days |

### 16:8 Example Schedule
| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 8:00 AM | Wake, water, coffee (black) |
| 12:00 PM | First meal (break fast) |
| 3:00 PM | Second meal/snack |
| 7:00 PM | Last meal |
| 8:00 PM | Fasting begins |

### Benefits
- Simplified eating
- Potential fat loss
- May improve insulin sensitivity
- Fewer meals to prepare

### Drawbacks
- Can be hard at first
- May reduce workout performance
- Social eating challenges
- Not for everyone

## Ramadan Eating Strategy

### Suhoor (Pre-Dawn)
| Priority | Foods | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Slow carbs | Sustained energy |
| 2 | Protein | Fullness, muscle |
| 3 | Healthy fats | Satiety |
| 4 | Water | Hydration |

**Example Suhoor:**
- Ful medames with olive oil
- 2-3 eggs
- Whole grain bread
- Dates
- Water (500ml+)

### Iftar (Breaking Fast)
| Phase | Timing | Foods |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 1 | Immediately | Dates + water |
| 2 | After prayer | Soup, salad |
| 3 | Main meal | Protein + carbs |
| 4 | Later evening | Light snack if needed |

### Workout Timing in Ramadan
| Option | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| After Iftar (8-9 PM) | Fed, hydrated | Late, may affect sleep |
| Before Iftar (5-6 PM) | Fasted training | Low energy, dehydrated |
| After Taraweeh (11 PM+) | Fed, digested | Very late |
