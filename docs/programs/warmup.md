# Warmup Protocol Guide

## Why Warmup Matters

### Physiological Benefits
| Effect | Benefit |
|--------|---------|
| Increased blood flow | More oxygen/nutrients to muscles |
| Elevated muscle temperature | Improved contraction speed and force |
| Enhanced nerve conduction | Faster reflexes, better coordination |
| Synovial fluid distribution | Joint lubrication, reduced friction |
| Mental preparation | Focus, rehearsal of movements |

### Injury Prevention
- Cold muscles are less elastic and more prone to tears
- Warming up reduces injury risk by 50%+ (research-supported)
- Prepares joints for loaded movement
- Identifies any pain/limitation before heavy loads

**Never skip warmup.** Even if short on time, do a 5-minute minimum.

## Warmup Structure (10-15 minutes)

### Phase 1: General Warmup (3-5 min)
**Purpose:** Elevate heart rate, increase core temperature

| Option | Duration | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Light cycling | 3-5 min | Easy pace, 60-70% max HR |
| Treadmill walk/jog | 3-5 min | Slight incline optional |
| Rowing machine | 3-5 min | Low intensity |
| Jumping jacks | 2-3 min | If no equipment |
| Jump rope | 2-3 min | If no equipment |

**Target:** Light sweat, elevated heart rate, feeling warm

### Phase 2: Dynamic Mobility (5-7 min)
**Purpose:** Prepare joints and muscles for specific movements

#### Upper Body Focus
| Exercise | Reps | Target |
|----------|------|--------|
| Arm circles (small → large) | 10 each direction | Shoulder joint |
| Arm swings (horizontal) | 15 total | Chest, shoulders |
| Band pull-aparts | 15-20 | Upper back, rear delts |
| Wall slides | 10 | Scapular mobility |
| Cat-cow | 10 | Thoracic spine |
| Thread the needle | 5 each side | Thoracic rotation |

#### Lower Body Focus
| Exercise | Reps | Target |
|----------|------|--------|
| Leg swings (front-back) | 10 each leg | Hip flexors, hamstrings |
| Leg swings (side-side) | 10 each leg | Hip adductors/abductors |
| Hip circles | 10 each direction each leg | Hip joint capsule |
| Walking lunges | 8 each leg | Hip flexors, glutes |
| Bodyweight squats | 10-15 | Lower body pattern |
| Inchworms | 5 | Hamstrings, core, shoulders |

#### Full Body
| Exercise | Reps | Target |
|----------|------|--------|
| World's greatest stretch | 5 each side | Hips, thoracic, hamstrings |
| Spiderman lunges | 5 each side | Hip mobility |
| High knees | 20 total | Hip flexors, coordination |
| Butt kicks | 20 total | Quad stretch, hamstring activation |

### Phase 3: Movement-Specific Warmup
**Purpose:** Prepare nervous system for specific exercise patterns

#### Before Squats
1. Bodyweight squats x 10
2. Goblet squat (light) x 8
3. Empty bar squat x 10
4. 50% working weight x 5
5. 70% working weight x 3
6. Begin working sets

#### Before Bench Press
1. Band pull-aparts x 15
2. Push-ups x 10
3. Empty bar x 10
4. 50% working weight x 5
5. 70% working weight x 3
6. Begin working sets

#### Before Deadlifts
1. Cat-cow x 10
2. Hip hinges (bodyweight) x 10
3. RDL with empty bar x 8
4. 50% working weight x 5
5. 70% working weight x 3
6. Begin working sets

#### Before Overhead Press
1. Arm circles x 10 each
2. Band shoulder dislocates x 10
3. Empty bar press x 10
4. 50% working weight x 5
5. 70% working weight x 3
6. Begin working sets

## Key Warmup Exercises Explained

### World's Greatest Stretch
1. Step into forward lunge (right foot forward)
2. Place left hand on floor inside right foot
3. Rotate right arm up toward ceiling
4. Hold 2 seconds, return hand to floor
5. Straighten front leg, hinge to stretch hamstring
6. Return to lunge, step forward to other side
7. Repeat 5 times each side

### Inchworms
1. Stand tall, feet together
2. Hinge at hips, walk hands out to plank
3. Hold plank briefly
4. Walk feet toward hands (keeping legs straight)
5. Stand up tall
6. Repeat 5-10 times

### Wall Slides
1. Back against wall, feet 6 inches out
2. Arms in "goalpost" position against wall
3. Slowly slide arms overhead
4. Keep wrists, elbows, shoulders on wall
5. Return to goalpost position
6. Repeat 10-15 times

### Hip Circles (Standing)
1. Stand on one leg (hold support if needed)
2. Lift opposite knee to hip height
3. Circle knee outward, then back
4. 10 circles each direction
5. Switch legs

### Band Pull-Aparts
1. Hold band at shoulder width, arms extended
2. Pull band apart by squeezing shoulder blades
3. Bring band to chest level
4. Control return
5. 15-20 reps

## Common Warmup Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Skipping warmup | Injury risk, poor performance | Always do minimum 5 min |
| Static stretching before lifting | Reduces power output | Save static for after |
| Too much cardio | Fatigue before lifting | 3-5 min is enough |
| Not warming up specific muscles | Unprepared for exercise | Match warmup to workout |
| Going too heavy too fast | Injury, poor form | Progressive loading |
| Same warmup every day | Doesn't address specific needs | Customize to workout |

## Warmup for Special Populations

### Older Adults (40+)
- Longer general warmup (5-10 min)
- Extra joint mobility work
- Slower progression to working weight
- More attention to shoulders and hips

### Returning After Injury
- Focus on injured area mobility
- Very gradual loading
- Listen to pain signals
- Include rehab exercises in warmup

### Cold Weather/Early Morning
- Extended general warmup (extra 2-3 min)
- More dynamic movements
- Extra clothing until warm
- Don't rush to heavy weights

### Competition/PR Attempts
- Full warmup (15+ min)
- Multiple warmup sets with progressive loading
- Adequate rest between warmup sets
- Final warmup at 90% of target
- Mental preparation

## Signs of Adequate Warmup

✓ Light sweat
✓ Elevated heart rate
✓ Muscles feel warm and pliable
✓ Joints move freely
✓ Mentally focused
✓ No pain or restriction
✓ Ready to move well

## Signs Warmup is Insufficient

✗ Still feeling cold/stiff
✗ Joints feel "clunky"
✗ First working set feels heavy
✗ Poor mind-muscle connection
✗ Tightness in muscles

**If in doubt, do more warmup.** It's better to spend an extra 5 minutes warming up than to get injured.
