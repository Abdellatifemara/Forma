# Complete Calisthenics Skills Database

## Overview

Calisthenics skills represent the pinnacle of bodyweight training - combining strength, balance, flexibility, and body control. This database covers progressions from beginner to advanced skills.

### Benefits of Calisthenics Skills
- Relative strength development
- Body awareness and control
- Impressive physical abilities
- Minimal equipment needed
- Functional strength
- Joint health through full ranges of motion

### Training Principles
- **Progressions**: Master each step before advancing
- **Consistency**: Regular practice over time
- **Patience**: Advanced skills take months/years
- **Quality**: Form over reps
- **Recovery**: Skills require neural recovery

## Push-Up Progressions

### CS001 - Wall Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 1)
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands on wall at chest height
  - Body straight, lean into wall
  - Push back to start
  - Progress by standing further from wall

### CS002 - Incline Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 2)
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Wall push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands on elevated surface (bench, box)
  - Body straight from head to heels
  - Lower chest to surface
  - Lower surface = harder

### CS003 - Knee Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 3)
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Incline push-ups 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Knees on floor, body straight from knees to head
  - Lower chest to floor
  - Full range of motion

### CS004 - Standard Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 4)
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps, core
- **Prerequisite**: Knee push-ups 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands shoulder-width, body straight
  - Lower until chest nearly touches
  - Press back to full lockout

### CS005 - Diamond Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Triceps, inner chest
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands together forming diamond
  - Elbows track along body
  - Lower chest to hands
  - Harder on triceps

### CS006 - Wide Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest (emphasis on stretch)
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands wider than shoulder-width
  - Lower with control
  - Feel stretch at bottom

### CS007 - Archer Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps (unilateral)
- **Prerequisite**: Diamond push-ups 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide hand position
  - Shift toward one arm while straightening other
  - One arm does most work
  - Alternate sides

### CS008 - Decline Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Upper chest, shoulders
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet elevated on box/bench
  - Body straight from head to heels
  - Lower head toward floor

### CS009 - Pike Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hips high in pike position
  - Lower head toward floor
  - Press back up
  - Progression toward handstand push-up

### CS010 - Pseudo Planche Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, shoulders, planche prep
- **Prerequisite**: Diamond push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands by waist, fingers pointing out or back
  - Lean forward, keeping straight body
  - Push-up with forward lean
  - Builds planche strength

### CS011 - One-Arm Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps, core
- **Prerequisite**: Archer push-ups 3x10 each
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet wide for balance
  - One hand on floor, other behind back
  - Lower and press with single arm
  - Keep body as straight as possible

### CS012 - Clapping Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Explosive pushing power
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x25
- **Form Cues**:
  - Explosive push-up
  - Hands leave ground at top
  - Clap and land softly
  - Immediately into next rep

### CS013 - Superman Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body explosive power
- **Prerequisite**: Clapping push-ups 3x10
- **Form Cues**:
  - Explosive push-up
  - Entire body leaves ground
  - Arms and legs extend (superman position)
  - Land and immediately continue

## Pull-Up Progressions

### CS014 - Dead Hang
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 1)
- **Primary**: Grip, shoulder stability
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hang from bar, arms straight
  - Shoulders active (not shrugging to ears)
  - Hold for time
- **Target**: 30-60 seconds

### CS015 - Scapular Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 2)
- **Primary**: Scapular muscles
- **Prerequisite**: Dead hang 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - From dead hang, pull scapulae down and back
  - No elbow bend, just shoulder blade movement
  - Returns to dead hang
- **Target**: 3x15

### CS016 - Negative Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 3)
- **Primary**: Lats, biceps (eccentric)
- **Prerequisite**: Scapular pull-ups 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Jump or step to top position
  - Lower as slowly as possible (5+ seconds)
  - Full extension at bottom
- **Target**: 3x5 (5 second negatives)

### CS017 - Assisted Pull-Up (Band)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner (Level 4)
- **Primary**: Lats, biceps
- **Prerequisite**: Negative pull-ups 3x5
- **Form Cues**:
  - Band looped over bar, foot in band
  - Perform full pull-up with band assistance
  - Progress to thinner bands

### CS018 - Standard Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats, biceps
- **Prerequisite**: Assisted pull-ups 3x8 (light band)
- **Form Cues**:
  - Overhand grip, shoulder-width
  - Pull chin over bar
  - Full extension at bottom
  - No kipping

### CS019 - Chin-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Biceps, lats
- **Prerequisite**: Same as pull-up
- **Form Cues**:
  - Underhand (supinated) grip
  - Pull chin over bar
  - Often easier than pull-ups

### CS020 - Wide Grip Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats (width emphasis)
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x8
- **Form Cues**:
  - Grip wider than shoulder-width
  - Pull to chest
  - Harder due to longer moment arm

### CS021 - Close Grip Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats, biceps
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x8
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands close together
  - Pull chin over bar
  - Different lat emphasis

### CS022 - L-Sit Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x10, L-sit 15 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold L-sit position (legs horizontal)
  - Perform pull-ups maintaining L
  - Intense core demand

### CS023 - Archer Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats (unilateral emphasis)
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x12
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide grip
  - Pull toward one hand, other arm straightens
  - Alternate sides
  - Progression toward one-arm

### CS024 - Typewriter Pull-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, grip
- **Prerequisite**: Archer pull-ups 3x6 each
- **Form Cues**:
  - Pull to one side
  - At top, shift across bar to other side
  - Lower from new side
  - Continuous motion

### CS025 - Muscle-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, triceps, transition strength
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x15, dips 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Explosive pull-up
  - Transition hands over bar
  - Press to top of dip
  - Can be strict or with swing

### CS026 - One-Arm Pull-Up (Progression)
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Lat, bicep (unilateral)
- **Prerequisite**: Archer pull-ups 3x8 each
- **Progressions**:
  1. Assisted with other arm on wrist
  2. Assisted with other arm on forearm
  3. Assisted with other arm on bicep
  4. Negative one-arm (slow descent)
  5. Full one-arm pull-up

## Dip Progressions

### CS027 - Bench Dip
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Triceps
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands on bench behind you
  - Lower body by bending elbows
  - Keep back close to bench
  - Feet flat or extended

### CS028 - Assisted Dip (Band/Machine)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Bench dips 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Band under knees or use assisted machine
  - Lower until shoulders below elbows
  - Press to lockout

### CS029 - Parallel Bar Dip
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps, front delts
- **Prerequisite**: Assisted dips 3x10
- **Form Cues**:
  - Support on parallel bars
  - Lower until shoulders below elbows
  - Press to full lockout
  - Slight forward lean for chest

### CS030 - Ring Dip
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps, stabilizers
- **Prerequisite**: Parallel dips 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Rings add instability
  - Turn rings out at top (RTO)
  - Full depth and lockout
  - Much harder than bar dips

### CS031 - Korean Dip
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Triceps, shoulders
- **Prerequisite**: Dips 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Bar behind you (like reverse dip)
  - Lower body between bar and back
  - Press up
  - Extreme shoulder position

### CS032 - Weighted Dip
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Dips 3x15
- **Form Cues**:
  - Add weight via belt or vest
  - Same form as regular dips
  - Progressive overload

## Core Progressions

### CS033 - Hollow Body Hold
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core (anti-extension)
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, lower back pressed down
  - Arms overhead, legs straight
  - Lift arms, head, and legs slightly
  - Maintain position (banana shape)
- **Target**: 60 seconds

### CS034 - Hollow Body Rock
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core
- **Prerequisite**: Hollow hold 60 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hollow position
  - Rock forward and back
  - Maintain shape throughout

### CS035 - Tuck L-Sit
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - On parallettes or floor
  - Lift body, knees tucked to chest
  - Hold position
- **Target**: 30 seconds

### CS036 - L-Sit (Full)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Tuck L-sit 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Legs extended horizontally
  - Arms straight, hands by hips
  - Hold position
- **Target**: 15-30 seconds

### CS037 - V-Sit
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors
- **Prerequisite**: L-sit 20 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Legs higher than L-sit (V shape)
  - Requires more compression flexibility
  - Hold position

### CS038 - Dragon Flag
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core (eccentric/concentric)
- **Prerequisite**: Hollow hold 60 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on bench, grip behind head
  - Lift body straight from shoulders
  - Lower slowly, maintaining straight line
  - Don't let hips bend

### CS039 - Front Lever Tuck
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Prerequisite**: Pull-ups 3x10
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hang from bar, pull to inverted hang
  - Lower to horizontal with knees tucked
  - Hold position parallel to ground

### CS040 - Front Lever (Full)
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Prerequisite**: Tuck front lever 15 sec
- **Progressions**:
  1. Tuck (knees tucked)
  2. Advanced tuck (knees away from chest)
  3. One leg extended
  4. Straddle
  5. Full front lever

### CS041 - Back Lever Tuck
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: Inverted hang 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - From inverted hang, lower back-first
  - Tuck knees, body horizontal
  - Arms straight

### CS042 - Back Lever (Full)
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Shoulders, biceps, core
- **Prerequisite**: Tuck back lever 15 sec
- **Progressions**: Same as front lever

## Handstand Progressions

### CS043 - Wall Plank
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank with feet on wall
  - Walk feet up, hands closer to wall
  - Build toward vertical
- **Target**: 60 seconds

### CS044 - Chest-to-Wall Handstand
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: Wall plank 60 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face wall, kick up to handstand
  - Chest and nose toward wall
  - Body straight, core engaged
  - Builds alignment
- **Target**: 60 seconds

### CS045 - Back-to-Wall Handstand
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, balance
- **Prerequisite**: Chest-to-wall 60 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away from wall, kick up
  - Heels touch wall for balance
  - Practice pulling feet away
  - Learn to find balance

### CS046 - Freestanding Handstand
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, balance, core
- **Prerequisite**: Back-to-wall 60 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Kick up away from wall
  - Stack body: wrists, shoulders, hips, feet
  - Use fingers for balance
  - Core engaged, squeeze glutes
- **Target**: 30-60 seconds

### CS047 - Handstand Walk
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, balance
- **Prerequisite**: Freestanding handstand 15 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - From handstand, shift and step hands
  - Keep body stacked
  - Small steps, maintain balance

### CS048 - Handstand Push-Up (Wall)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Freestanding handstand 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Chest to wall handstand
  - Lower head toward floor
  - Press back up
  - Full range to floor = hardest

### CS049 - Freestanding Handstand Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Shoulders, triceps, balance
- **Prerequisite**: Wall HSPU 3x8
- **Form Cues**:
  - Freestanding handstand
  - Lower and press while balancing
  - Extreme difficulty

### CS050 - One-Arm Handstand (Progression)
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Shoulders, insane balance
- **Prerequisite**: Freestanding handstand 60 sec
- **Progressions**:
  1. Weight shifts to one arm
  2. Finger lift-offs
  3. Brief one-arm holds
  4. Full one-arm handstand

## Planche Progressions

### CS051 - Planche Lean
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Shoulders, wrists
- **Prerequisite**: Push-ups 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Push-up position, lean forward
  - Hands stay in place, shoulders move over wrists
  - Hold lean position
- **Target**: 60 seconds

### CS052 - Frog Stand
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Balance, wrists
- **Prerequisite**: Planche lean 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Squat, hands on floor, knees on elbows
  - Lean forward until feet lift
  - Balance on hands
- **Target**: 30 seconds

### CS053 - Tuck Planche
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: Frog stand 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Like frog stand but knees not on arms
  - Tuck position, no arm contact
  - Body horizontal
- **Target**: 15 seconds

### CS054 - Advanced Tuck Planche
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: Tuck planche 15 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Knees pulled away from chest
  - Back more horizontal
  - Significantly harder than tuck

### CS055 - Straddle Planche
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Prerequisite**: Advanced tuck 15 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Legs extended, spread wide
  - Body horizontal
  - Arms straight

### CS056 - Full Planche
- **Difficulty**: Elite (Years of training)
- **Primary**: Shoulders, full body
- **Prerequisite**: Straddle planche 10 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Legs together, straight
  - Body completely horizontal
  - Arms straight
  - Ultimate pressing strength

## Ring Skills

### CS057 - Ring Support Hold
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps, stabilizers
- **Prerequisite**: Dips 3x10
- **Form Cues**:
  - Support on rings, arms straight
  - Turn rings out (RTO)
  - Hold stable position
- **Target**: 30-60 seconds

### CS058 - Ring Turned Out Dip
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Prerequisite**: Ring support 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Full dip with rings turned out at top
  - Maintain ring stability throughout
  - Much harder than parallel bars

### CS059 - Ring L-Sit
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors
- **Prerequisite**: L-sit 20 sec, ring support 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - L-sit position on rings
  - Turn rings out
  - Maintain stability

### CS060 - Rings Muscle-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full upper body
- **Prerequisite**: Ring pull-ups 3x10, ring dips 3x10
- **Form Cues**:
  - False grip on rings
  - Explosive pull
  - Transition through
  - Press to support

### CS061 - Iron Cross (Progressions)
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Shoulders, chest
- **Prerequisite**: Ring support 60 sec RTO
- **Progressions**:
  1. Wide ring support
  2. Assisted iron cross (band)
  3. Iron cross negatives
  4. Iron cross holds
  5. Full iron cross
- **Notes**: Takes years to achieve

### CS062 - Back Lever (Rings)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, biceps
- **Prerequisite**: Similar to bar back lever
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same as bar but on rings
  - Rings add instability
  - Requires wrist flexibility

### CS063 - Front Lever (Rings)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Prerequisite**: Similar to bar front lever
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same progression as bar
  - Rings add instability
  - Turn rings out

## Leg/Lower Body Skills

### CS064 - Pistol Squat (Assisted)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Prerequisite**: Squats 3x20
- **Form Cues**:
  - Single leg squat holding support
  - Other leg extended forward
  - Full depth
  - Progress by reducing assistance

### CS065 - Pistol Squat (Full)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes, balance
- **Prerequisite**: Assisted pistol 3x8 each
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg
  - Squat all the way down
  - Other leg stays forward
  - Stand back up
  - Arms forward for counterbalance

### CS066 - Shrimp Squat
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Quads, hip flexors
- **Prerequisite**: Pistol squat 3x5 each
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg
  - Hold back foot behind you
  - Squat until back knee touches floor
  - Stand back up
  - Harder than pistol for most

### CS067 - Nordic Curl
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Hamstrings (eccentric)
- **Prerequisite**: Good hamstring strength
- **Form Cues**:
  - Kneel, anchor feet
  - Lower body forward with control
  - Hamstrings resist descent
  - Use hands to catch and push back up initially

### CS068 - Dragon Squat
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Quads, balance
- **Prerequisite**: Pistol squat 3x8
- **Form Cues**:
  - Single leg squat
  - Other leg wraps behind standing leg
  - Extreme balance and mobility required

## Bridges and Back Flexibility

### CS069 - Glute Bridge
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Glutes, back
- **Prerequisite**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, feet flat
  - Push hips up
  - Squeeze glutes at top

### CS070 - Bridge Hold (Full Bridge)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Back extensors, shoulders
- **Prerequisite**: Glute bridge, shoulder flexibility
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, hands by ears
  - Push up, arching back
  - Arms and legs straight
  - Look at hands
- **Target**: 30-60 seconds

### CS071 - Bridge Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Prerequisite**: Bridge 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - From lying, push to full bridge
  - Lower back down
  - Repeat

### CS072 - Wall Walk Bridge
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Back flexibility
- **Prerequisite**: Bridge 30 sec
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand facing away from wall
  - Lean back, walk hands down wall
  - Can walk back up or drop to bridge

### CS073 - Stand to Bridge to Stand
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Back, shoulders
- **Prerequisite**: Wall walk comfortable
- **Form Cues**:
  - From standing, lean back to bridge
  - Push through to standing
  - Requires significant flexibility

## Training Programs

### Beginner Skill Program (12 weeks)
**Week 1-4:**
- Push-up progression: Current level 3x max
- Pull-up progression: Negatives/band 3x max
- Dip progression: Current level 3x max
- Hollow hold: 3x max time

**Week 5-8:**
- Advance push-up one level
- Advance pull-up one level
- Advance dip one level
- L-sit progression

**Week 9-12:**
- Continue progressions
- Add wall handstand work
- Add bridge work

### Intermediate Skill Program
**Push Focus Day:**
- Pike push-ups 4x8
- Pseudo planche push-ups 3x8
- Planche lean 3x30s
- Handstand practice 10 min

**Pull Focus Day:**
- Pull-ups 5x5
- Archer pull-up negatives 3x4 each
- Front lever tuck 4x10s
- L-sit 4x max

**Repeat 2x/week each day**
