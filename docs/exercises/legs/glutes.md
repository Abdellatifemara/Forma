## Anatomy Overview

### The Three Gluteal Muscles
| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Function |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Gluteus Maximus** | Ilium, sacrum, coccyx | IT band, gluteal tuberosity of femur | Hip extension, external rotation, abduction (upper fibers) |
| **Gluteus Medius** | External surface of ilium | Greater trochanter of femur | Hip abduction, internal rotation (anterior), external rotation (posterior), pelvic stabilization |
| **Gluteus Minimus** | External surface of ilium | Greater trochanter of femur | Hip abduction, internal rotation |

### Key Points for Complete Development
1. **Gluteus maximus** - Largest muscle in the body! Primary hip extensor
2. **Gluteus medius** - Critical for hip stability, prevents knee valgus
3. **Both need training** - Most people are "glute max" dominant, neglect medius
4. **Mind-muscle connection** - Many people are "glute amnesia" - can't activate properly

### Glute Function Summary
| Function | Muscles | Exercises |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| Hip Extension | Maximus | Hip thrusts, bridges, kickbacks |
| Hip Abduction | Medius, minimus | Side-lying raises, clamshells, abduction machine |
| External Rotation | Maximus, medius (posterior) | Clamshells, band walks |
| Pelvic Stabilization | Medius, minimus | Single-leg work, Copenhagen plank |

## Hip Thrust Variations (Glute Max Primary)

### GL001 - Barbell Hip Thrust
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Barbell, bench |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Primary** | Gluteus maximus |
| **Secondary** | Hamstrings |
| **Contraindications** | Lower back issues |

**Form Cues:**
- Upper back against bench edge (at shoulder blade level)
- Feet flat, hip-width apart, knees at 90 degrees at top
- Bar in hip crease (use pad!)
- Drive through heels, squeeze glutes to lift
- Chin tucked, don't hyperextend neck
- Full hip extension at top - SQUEEZE 1-2 seconds
- Lower with control

**Common Mistakes:**
- Arching lower back → Maintain posterior pelvic tilt
- Pushing through toes → Drive through heels
- Not reaching full extension → Squeeze glutes at top
- Looking at ceiling → Keep chin tucked

## Hip Abduction (Glute Medius/Minimus)

### GL020 - Side-Lying Hip Abduction
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Bodyweight |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Gluteus medius |
| **Secondary** | Gluteus minimus |
| **Contraindications** | None |

**Form Cues:**
- Lie on side, legs straight and stacked
- Lift top leg toward ceiling (30-45 degrees)
- Keep hips stacked - don't rotate backward
- Lead with HEEL, not toe
- Control the descent

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rotating hips backward → Stack hips perpendicular to floor
- Lifting too high → 30-45 degrees is enough
- Leading with toe → Keep foot neutral or slightly heel-leading

- Back foot on bench, laces down
- Front foot 2-3 feet from bench
- Lower until front thigh parallel

## Form Guides for Key Exercises

### Hip Thrust - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Upper back against bench edge at shoulder blade level
2. **Foot Position:** Flat on floor, hip-width, knees at 90 degrees at top
3. **Bar Position:** In hip crease, use pad for comfort
4. **Drive:** Push through heels, squeeze glutes
5. **Top Position:** Full hip extension, posterior pelvic tilt
6. **Head Position:** Chin tucked, look forward (not at ceiling)
7. **Lower:** Control the descent, don't drop

### Glute Bridge - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat hip-width apart
2. **Arms:** At sides, palms down for stability
3. **Lift:** Drive through heels, lift hips toward ceiling
4. **Squeeze:** Maximum glute contraction at top
5. **Alignment:** Shoulders to knees should form straight line
6. **Lower:** Control descent, don't rest fully at bottom

### Clamshell - Detailed Form
1. **Position:** Lie on side, knees bent 90 degrees, feet together
2. **Alignment:** Keep hips stacked vertically
3. **Movement:** Rotate top knee up like a clamshell opening
4. **Feet:** Keep feet together throughout
5. **Squeeze:** Hold at top for 1-2 seconds
6. **Control:** Lower slowly

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Hyperextending back on hip thrust | Spinal stress, not more glute | Maintain posterior pelvic tilt |
| Pushing through toes | Quad dominant | Drive through heels |
| Rolling hips in clamshell | Reduces glute med activation | Keep hips stacked |
| Neglecting glute medius | Hip/knee instability | Add abduction work |
| Only doing compound movements | May not isolate glutes enough | Add isolation work |
| No mind-muscle connection | "Glute amnesia" | Activation drills in warm-up |
| Using momentum | Reduces tension | Slow, controlled reps |
