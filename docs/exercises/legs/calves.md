## Anatomy Overview

### The Calf Muscles
| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Function |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **Gastrocnemius (Medial Head)** | Medial condyle of femur | Calcaneus via Achilles tendon | Plantar flexion, knee flexion |
| **Gastrocnemius (Lateral Head)** | Lateral condyle of femur | Calcaneus via Achilles tendon | Plantar flexion, knee flexion |
| **Soleus** | Posterior tibia & fibula | Calcaneus via Achilles tendon | Plantar flexion (only) |
| **Tibialis Anterior** | Lateral tibia | First metatarsal, cuneiform | Dorsiflexion, inversion |
| **Tibialis Posterior** | Posterior tibia, fibula | Navicular, cuneiforms | Plantar flexion, inversion |

### Key Points for Complete Development
1. **Gastrocnemius** - Trained with STRAIGHT legs (crosses knee joint)
2. **Soleus** - Trained with BENT knees (only crosses ankle joint)
3. **Both muscles** needed for complete calf development
4. **Soleus is ~60%** of calf mass - don't neglect seated calf raises!

### Why Straight vs Bent Knees Matter
| Knee Position | Primary Target | Example |
|---------------|----------------|---------|
| Straight | Gastrocnemius | Standing calf raise |
| Bent (~90°) | Soleus | Seated calf raise |

When the knee is bent, the gastrocnemius is shortened and can't contract effectively, so the soleus does most of the work.

## Standing Calf Raise Variations (Gastrocnemius)

### CA001 - Standing Calf Raise Machine
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Standing calf raise machine |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Gastrocnemius |
| **Secondary** | Soleus |
| **Contraindications** | Achilles tendinitis |

**Form Cues:**
- Position shoulders under pads
- Balls of feet on platform, heels hanging off
- Legs straight but knees not locked
- Lower heels below platform for full stretch
- Rise onto toes as high as possible
- Squeeze at top for 1-2 seconds
- Control the descent (2-3 seconds)

**Common Mistakes:**
- Bouncing at bottom → Use controlled tempo
- Short range of motion → Full stretch and contraction
- Bending knees → Keep legs straight
- Going too heavy → Full ROM > heavy weight

## Form Guides for Key Exercises

### Standing Calf Raise - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Balls of feet on platform, heels hanging off
2. **Start:** Lower heels below platform for full stretch
3. **Rise:** Push through balls of feet, rise as high as possible
4. **Peak:** Squeeze calves hard at top, hold 1-2 seconds
5. **Lower:** Control descent for 2-3 seconds
6. **Knees:** Straight but not hyperextended

### Seated Calf Raise - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Knees at 90 degrees, balls of feet on platform
2. **Pad:** Rest on lower thighs, not on knee caps
3. **Stretch:** Lower heels fully for deep stretch
4. **Contract:** Rise onto toes, squeeze soleus
5. **Tempo:** Slow and controlled throughout

### Eccentric Calf Raise (Rehab Protocol)
1. **Rise:** Use both legs to rise onto toes
2. **Transfer:** Shift weight to affected leg
3. **Lower:** Slowly lower over 5-6 seconds
4. **Repeat:** Rise on both legs, lower on one
5. **Protocol:** 3 sets x 15 reps, 2x/day for 12 weeks

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Bouncing at bottom | No stretch, uses momentum | Pause briefly at bottom |
| Short ROM | Missing muscle development | Full stretch to full contraction |
| Bending knees (standing) | Reduces gastrocnemius work | Keep legs straight |
| Going too heavy | Sacrifices ROM | Prioritize full ROM |
| Only standing raises | Neglects soleus (60% of calf mass) | Add seated calf raises |
| Training infrequently | Calves recover fast | Train 2-3x/week |
| Ignoring tibialis | Muscle imbalance, shin splints | Add tibialis work |

## Calf Training Tips

### For Stubborn Calves
1. **Increase frequency** - Train 3x/week or more
2. **Higher volume** - 15-20+ sets/week
3. **Full ROM** - Stretch deeply, contract fully
4. **Slow eccentrics** - 3-5 second negatives
5. **Train both muscles** - Don't skip seated raises
6. **Pause at stretch** - 1-2 seconds at bottom
7. **Use various toe positions** - In, out, neutral

### Achilles Tendinitis Protocol
1. Eccentric calf raises (CA057, CA058)
2. 3 sets x 15 reps, 2x/day
3. 12-week protocol minimum
4. Progress slowly with load
5. Some discomfort OK, sharp pain = stop
