## Anatomy Overview

### The Oblique Muscles
| Muscle | Origin | Insertion | Function |
|--------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **External Oblique** | Lower 8 ribs | Linea alba, iliac crest, pubic tubercle | Rotation to OPPOSITE side, lateral flexion, trunk flexion |
| **Internal Oblique** | Iliac crest, inguinal ligament, thoracolumbar fascia | Lower 3 ribs, linea alba | Rotation to SAME side, lateral flexion, trunk flexion |

### Key Points
1. **External obliques** - Largest abdominal muscle, runs diagonally downward
2. **Internal obliques** - Lies beneath external, runs diagonally upward
3. **They work together** - Rotating right uses right internal + left external
4. **Four functions to train:**
   - Rotation (twisting)
   - Anti-rotation (resisting twist)
   - Lateral flexion (side bending)
   - Anti-lateral flexion (side planks)

### Oblique Function Summary
| Function | Movement | Examples |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Rotation | Twisting torso | Russian twist, woodchop |
| Anti-rotation | Resisting twist | Pallof press, bird dog |
| Lateral flexion | Side bending | Side bends, side crunches |
| Anti-lateral flexion | Resisting side bend | Side plank, suitcase carry |

### OB001 - Russian Twist
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Floor, optional weight |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Obliques (both) |
| **Secondary** | Hip flexors, rectus abdominis |
| **Contraindications** | Lower back issues |

**Form Cues:**
- Sit on floor, knees bent, heels down or elevated
- Lean back slightly (V-sit position)
- Hold weight (optional) at chest
- Rotate torso side to side
- Keep hips stable - only torso rotates

**Common Mistakes:**
- Moving hips instead of torso → Keep hips stationary
- Going too fast → Slow, controlled rotation
- Using too heavy weight → Light weight, proper form

## Anti-Rotation Exercises

### OB014 - Pallof Press
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Cable machine or band |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Primary** | Obliques (anti-rotation) |
| **Secondary** | Transverse abdominis |
| **Contraindications** | None |

**Form Cues:**
- Stand sideways to cable/band at chest height
- Hold handle at chest with both hands
- Press arms straight out in front
- Resist the rotation force
- Hold extended position, return to chest

**Why It's Effective:**
The cable/band tries to rotate you - your obliques work to RESIST that rotation. This is how they function in real life (sports, daily activities).

## Form Guides for Key Exercises

### Russian Twist - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Sit on floor, knees bent, heels down or elevated
2. **Position:** Lean back to ~45 degrees, V-sit position
3. **Arms:** Hold weight at chest or clasp hands
4. **Rotation:** Twist torso side to side
5. **Hips:** Keep stationary - only torso moves
6. **Speed:** Slow and controlled

### Pallof Press - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Stand sideways to cable/band at chest height
2. **Grip:** Both hands on handle at chest
3. **Press:** Extend arms straight in front
4. **Resist:** Hold position, resist rotation force
5. **Return:** Bring hands back to chest
6. **Tension:** Feel obliques working to stay square

### Side Plank - Detailed Form
1. **Position:** Lie on side, forearm on floor, elbow under shoulder
2. **Feet:** Stack or stagger for balance
3. **Lift:** Raise hips until body forms straight line
4. **Alignment:** Head, shoulders, hips, ankles in line
5. **Hold:** Squeeze obliques, breathe normally
6. **Don't:** Let hips sag or pike up

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Moving hips during Russian twist | Reduces oblique work | Keep hips stationary |
| Going too heavy on side bends | Can widen waist | Use moderate weight |
| Rotating too fast | Uses momentum, injury risk | Slow, controlled movement |
| Letting hips sag in side plank | Reduces effectiveness | Keep hips lifted |
| Ignoring anti-rotation work | Missing key function | Add Pallof press, carries |
| Only doing rotation exercises | Incomplete development | Add anti-rotation, lateral flexion |

### Important Note on Oblique Training
**Don't overdo oblique training!** Unlike other muscles, hypertrophied obliques can widen your waist and reduce the V-taper appearance. Focus on:
- Moderate volume (not excessive)
- Lighter weights
- Quality over quantity
- Anti-rotation and stability work
