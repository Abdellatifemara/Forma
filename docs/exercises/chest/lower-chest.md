# Lower Chest Exercises (65+ Movements)

## Anatomy
The **lower sternocostal fibers** of the pectoralis major run from the lower sternum and ribs to the upper arm.

### Attachments
- **Origin**: Lower sternum, costal cartilages of ribs 5-7, external oblique aponeurosis
- **Insertion**: Lateral lip of bicipital groove of humerus

### Functions
- **Shoulder extension**: Pulling arm down from raised position
- **Shoulder adduction**: Bringing arm toward body
- **Internal rotation**: Rotating arm inward

### Optimal Activation
- Decline angle (15-30° below horizontal)
- Dips with forward lean
- High-to-low cable/pressing movements

## Total Lower Chest: 73 Exercises

## Optimal Decline Angle Guide

| Angle | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| 15° decline | Slight lower pec emphasis |
| 30° decline | Optimal lower pec activation |
| 45° decline | Excessive, reduces ROM |
| Dips (45-60° torso lean) | Maximum lower pec emphasis |

## Dip Form for Lower Pec

| Cue | Effect |
|-----|--------|
| Lean forward 45° | Maximum lower pec activation |
| Elbows flared slightly | More pec, less triceps |
| Go deep (shoulder level) | Full pec stretch |
| Squeeze at top | Peak contraction |

## Key Training Principles

1. **Forward lean on dips**: Essential for lower pec focus
2. **Control the descent**: Especially on dips to protect shoulders
3. **Full ROM**: Deep stretch at bottom for full activation
4. **Don't go too steep**: >30° decline reduces effectiveness
5. **Blood pressure warning**: Decline positions can raise BP

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Upright on dips | Triceps dominant | Lean forward 45° |
| Too steep decline | Reduced ROM | Max 30° decline |
| Bouncing at bottom | Shoulder stress | Controlled descent |
| Cutting ROM short | Less muscle activation | Full stretch |
| Holding breath on decline | Blood pressure spike | Breathe normally |

## Programming Recommendations
| Goal | Sets/Week | Rep Range | Rest |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Strength | 6-10 | 3-6 | 3-5 min |
| Hypertrophy | 10-16 | 8-12 | 60-90s |
| Endurance | 12-20 | 15-25 | 30-45s |

**Note:** Lower pec is often trained sufficiently through flat bench and dips. Dedicated decline work is optional for most lifters.

**Optimal Weekly Volume:** 4-8 sets specifically for lower pec (in addition to flat chest work)
