# Upper Chest Exercises (60+ Movements)

## Anatomy
The upper chest (clavicular head of the pectoralis major) originates from the clavicle and requires incline angles (30-45°) for optimal activation. EMG studies show 30° incline provides best upper pec activation.

## Key Training Principles

1. **Optimal Angle:** 30-45° incline (30° slightly better for upper pec EMG)
2. **Shoulder Safety:** Keep shoulders retracted and depressed
3. **Elbow Position:** 45-75° from torso (not 90° flared)
4. **Full ROM:** Touch chest on presses, full stretch on flyes
5. **Mind-Muscle:** Focus on squeezing upper chest fibers

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Angle too steep (60°+) | Becomes shoulder press | Use 30-45° |
| Bouncing off chest | Reduces tension | Pause at bottom |
| Flared elbows | Shoulder stress | Tuck to 45-60° |
| Lifting hips | Reduces incline effect | Keep butt on bench |

## Programming Recommendations
| Goal | Sets/Week | Rep Range | Rest |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Strength | 6-10 | 3-6 | 2-3 min |
| Hypertrophy | 10-20 | 6-12 | 60-120s |
| Endurance | 12-20 | 15-25 | 30-60s |
