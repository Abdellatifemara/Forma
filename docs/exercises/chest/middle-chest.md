# Middle Chest Exercises (75+ Movements)

## Anatomy
The **sternal (middle) head** of the pectoralis major is the largest portion of the chest muscle.

### Attachments
- **Origin**: Sternum, costal cartilages of ribs 1-6
- **Insertion**: Lateral lip of bicipital groove of humerus

### Functions
- **Horizontal adduction**: Bringing arm across chest
- **Shoulder flexion**: Raising arm forward
- **Internal rotation**: Rotating arm inward

### Optimal Activation
- Flat bench angle (0°)
- Horizontal pressing and fly movements
- Arms at 45-75° from torso

## Bench Press Grip Guide

| Grip Width | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| Close (shoulder width) | More triceps, inner pec emphasis |
| Standard (1.5x shoulder) | Balanced pec/triceps activation |
| Wide (2x shoulder) | More outer pec, less ROM |

## Key Training Principles

1. **Scapular retraction**: Pinch shoulder blades together and down
2. **Elbow angle**: 45-75° from torso for shoulder safety
3. **Full ROM**: Touch chest on press, full stretch on flys
4. **Controlled eccentric**: 2-3 seconds lowering
5. **Mind-muscle**: Focus on squeezing pecs, not pushing weight

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Flared elbows (90°) | Shoulder impingement | 45-60° angle |
| Bouncing off chest | Reduced muscle tension | Pause at bottom |
| Excessive arch | Lower back strain | Moderate arch only |
| Incomplete lockout | Triceps do less work | Full extension |
| Shoulders rolling forward | Injury risk | Retract scapulae |
