# Complete Stretching Database

## Overview

Stretching improves flexibility, reduces injury risk, aids recovery, and enhances movement quality. This database covers static stretches, dynamic stretches, and PNF techniques for every major muscle group.

### Types of Stretching
- **Static**: Hold stretch 20-60 seconds, best post-workout or separate session
- **Dynamic**: Controlled movement through range, best pre-workout
- **PNF**: Contract-relax technique, most effective for flexibility gains
- **Active**: Use agonist muscle to hold stretch position

### When to Stretch
- **Pre-workout**: Dynamic stretches only (5-10 min)
- **Post-workout**: Static stretches (10-15 min)
- **Dedicated session**: Full routine 2-3x/week

## Lower Body Stretches

### Hamstrings

#### ST001 - Standing Hamstring Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Calves
- **Contraindications**: Acute hamstring injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand and place one heel on elevated surface
  - Keep leg straight, toes pointing up
  - Hinge at hips, reaching toward foot
  - Keep back flat - don't round spine
  - Feel stretch in back of thigh
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding lower back → Hinge from hips only
  - Bending knee → Keep leg straight
  - Bouncing → Hold static position

#### ST002 - Seated Forward Fold
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Lower back
- **Contraindications**: Disc herniation
- **Form Cues**:
  - Sit with legs extended, feet flexed
  - Reach forward, hinging at hips
  - Keep spine long, not rounded
  - Reach for shins, ankles, or feet depending on flexibility
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding excessively → Lead with chest
  - Locking knees hard → Slight softness OK

#### ST003 - Lying Hamstring Stretch with Strap
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Calves
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, loop strap around one foot
  - Keep opposite leg flat on ground
  - Pull leg toward ceiling, keeping it straight
  - Use strap to gently increase range
  - Lower back stays flat on floor
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending knee → Keep leg straight
  - Opposite leg lifting → Press it down
  - Pulling too aggressively → Gradual tension

#### ST004 - Standing Leg Swing (Dynamic)
- **Type**: Dynamic
- **Reps**: 10-15 per leg
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Balance issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold wall for support
  - Swing leg forward and back like pendulum
  - Keep leg straight, controlled speed
  - Gradually increase range
  - Maintain upright posture
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Swinging too fast → Control the movement
  - Excessive torso movement → Keep upper body still
  - Not using full range → Progressively go higher

#### ST005 - Supine Hamstring PNF
- **Type**: PNF (Contract-Relax)
- **Hold**: Contract 6s, relax 30s, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: None
- **Contraindications**: Recent hamstring tear
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, partner lifts leg to stretch
  - Contract hamstring against partner's resistance (6 sec)
  - Relax, partner increases stretch
  - Repeat 3 times, gaining range each time
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Contracting too hard → 50% effort is enough
  - Not fully relaxing → Complete relaxation for 30s
  - Rushing cycles → Full relax phase important

#### ST006 - Doorway Hamstring Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Calves
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie in doorway, one leg up on door frame
  - Other leg extends through doorway on floor
  - Slide closer to door for more stretch
  - Keep leg straight on wall
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Too far from door → Start closer
  - Knee bending → Straighten fully

### Quadriceps

#### ST007 - Standing Quad Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Quadriceps
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand tall, grab ankle behind you
  - Pull heel toward glute
  - Keep knees together
  - Squeeze glute for deeper stretch
  - Maintain upright posture
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knee going out to side → Keep knees together
  - Leaning forward → Stay upright
  - Lower back arching → Tuck pelvis slightly

#### ST008 - Side-Lying Quad Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Quadriceps
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on side, grab top ankle
  - Pull heel toward glute
  - Keep hips stacked, don't roll back
  - Push hip forward for more hip flexor stretch
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rolling backward → Stay on side
  - Not pushing hip forward → For full stretch, extend hip

#### ST009 - Prone Quad Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Quadriceps
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Knee problems
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie face down
  - Bend one knee, grab ankle
  - Pull heel toward glute
  - Keep hips pressed into floor
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Lifting hip → Press hip down
  - Twisting → Keep hips square

#### ST010 - Kneeling Quad Stretch (Couch Stretch)
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 60-120 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Quadriceps, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Rectus femoris
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain, patellar issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Back foot against wall, knee on floor
  - Front foot flat in lunge position
  - Squeeze glute of back leg
  - Keep torso upright
  - Extremely effective stretch
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning forward → Stay vertical
  - Not squeezing glute → Engage for deeper stretch
  - Lower back arching → Brace core

#### ST011 - Rectus Femoris Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per leg
- **Primary**: Rectus femoris (quad that crosses hip)
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Half-kneeling position
  - Grab back ankle, pull toward glute
  - Push hip forward simultaneously
  - This targets the quad that crosses the hip
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Forgetting hip extension → Must push hip forward
  - Overarching back → Keep core tight

#### ST012 - Leg Swing Side-to-Side (Dynamic)
- **Type**: Dynamic
- **Reps**: 10-15 per leg
- **Primary**: Adductors, abductors
- **Secondary**: Hip mobility
- **Contraindications**: Hip pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face wall, hands on wall for support
  - Swing leg side to side across body
  - Keep leg straight
  - Controlled tempo, increasing range
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rotating hips → Keep pelvis stable
  - Swinging too fast → Control the motion

#### ST014 - Low Lunge Hip Flexor Stretch

#### ST015 - Standing Hip Flexor Stretch

- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per side
- **Primary**: Iliopsoas
- **Secondary**: Quads
- **Contraindications**: Balance issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand in split stance
  - Tuck pelvis, squeeze back glute

- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Overarching back → Tuck pelvis
  - Not reaching → Arm overhead increases stretch

#### ST016 - Pigeon Pose Variation
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 60-120 seconds per side
- **Primary**: Hip flexors, glutes
- **Secondary**: External rotators
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - From all fours, bring one leg forward
  - Back leg extends behind, hip flexor on stretch
  - Square hips to floor
  - Can fold forward for more glute stretch
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hip hiking → Keep hips level
  - Knee pain → Use modification

#### ST017 - Hip Flexor Leg Swing (Dynamic)
- **Type**: Dynamic
- **Reps**: 10-15 per leg
- **Primary**: Hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Hamstrings
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same as ST004 but emphasize hip flexor
  - Swing leg with control front to back
  - Focus on the backswing for hip flexor
  - Gradual increase in range
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Arching back → Keep core tight
  - Not going back far enough → Full range

## Upper Body Stretches

### Chest

#### ST034 - Doorway Chest Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per side
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major
- **Secondary**: Anterior deltoid
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder instability
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand in doorway, arm on frame at 90 degrees
  - Step through with same-side foot
  - Rotate torso away from arm
  - Adjust arm height to target different fibers
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Shrugging shoulder → Keep down
  - Not rotating enough → Full rotation away

#### ST035 - Lying Chest Opener
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 60-120 seconds
- **Primary**: Chest, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Biceps
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder injuries
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on foam roller lengthwise (spine on roller)
  - Arms out to sides, palms up
  - Let chest open and arms drop
  - Relax and breathe
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Arching lower back → Keep core engaged
  - Forcing arms down → Let gravity work

#### ST036 - Wall Angel
- **Type**: Active/Dynamic
- **Reps**: 10-15
- **Primary**: Chest, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Upper back
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand with back against wall
  - Arms in W position against wall
  - Slide arms up to Y, keeping contact with wall
  - Return to W position
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows coming off wall → Keep contact
  - Lower back arching → Flatten against wall

#### ST037 - Behind Back Clasp Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds
- **Primary**: Chest
- **Secondary**: Biceps, front shoulder
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder injuries
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand tall, clasp hands behind back
  - Straighten arms and lift up
  - Squeeze shoulder blades together
  - Lift chest
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding shoulders → Pull back
  - Not lifting high enough → Raise arms up

#### ST038 - Floor Chest Stretch
- **Type**: Static
- **Hold**: 30-60 seconds per side
- **Primary**: Pectoralis major
- **Secondary**: Anterior deltoid
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie face down, extend one arm to side
  - Roll toward extended arm
  - Feel stretch across chest
  - Can adjust arm angle
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Going too far → Gradual rotation
  - Shoulder pain → Reduce range

## PNF Stretching Protocols

#### ST071 - PNF Hamstring (Contract-Relax)
- **Type**: PNF
- **Protocol**: 6s contract, 30s relax, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Steps**:
  1. Partner raises leg to stretch position
  2. Contract hamstring against partner (6 sec, 50% effort)
  3. Relax completely
  4. Partner increases range
  5. Repeat 3 times

#### ST072 - PNF Hip Flexor
- **Type**: PNF
- **Protocol**: 6s contract, 30s relax, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Hip flexors
- **Steps**:
  1. Kneeling hip flexor stretch position
  2. Push knee into floor (hip extension resistance)
  3. Relax, sink deeper
  4. Repeat

#### ST073 - PNF Chest
- **Type**: PNF
- **Protocol**: 6s contract, 30s relax, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Pectorals
- **Steps**:
  1. Doorway stretch position
  2. Push arm into door frame
  3. Relax, rotate further
  4. Repeat

#### ST074 - PNF Shoulder (External Rotation)
- **Type**: PNF
- **Protocol**: 6s contract, 30s relax, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Internal rotators
- **Steps**:
  1. Arm in external rotation stretch
  2. Contract internal rotators against resistance
  3. Relax, increase rotation
  4. Repeat

#### ST075 - PNF Quad
- **Type**: PNF
- **Protocol**: 6s contract, 30s relax, 3 cycles
- **Primary**: Quadriceps
- **Steps**:
  1. Prone quad stretch, partner holds ankle
  2. Push ankle against partner's hand
  3. Relax, partner increases flexion
  4. Repeat

## Common Stretching Mistakes

1. **Bouncing** - Use static holds, not ballistic movement
2. **Holding breath** - Breathe deeply into stretch
3. **Stretching cold muscles** - Light movement first
4. **Overstretching** - Mild discomfort only, never pain
5. **Inconsistency** - Regular practice required for gains
6. **Rushing** - Minimum 30 second holds for effect
7. **Stretching injured areas** - Let injuries heal first
8. **Ignoring reciprocal muscles** - Balance front and back

2. **Add resistance** - Body weight → light weights
3. **Improve range** - Progressively deeper positions
4. **Add PNF** - Contract-relax for faster gains
