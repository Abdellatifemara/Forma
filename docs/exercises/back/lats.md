# Lat Exercises (85+ Movements)

## Anatomy
The **Latissimus Dorsi** (lats) is the largest muscle of the back, spanning from the lower spine to the upper arm.

### Attachments
- **Origin**: Lower 6 thoracic vertebrae, thoracolumbar fascia, iliac crest, lower 3-4 ribs
- **Insertion**: Bicipital groove of the humerus

### Functions
- **Shoulder extension**: Pulling arm back
- **Shoulder adduction**: Pulling arm toward body
- **Internal rotation**: Rotating arm inward
- **Spinal extension**: Assists in arching back

## Lat Development Guide

### Width (V-Taper)
Best exercises:
- Wide grip pull-ups (LA002)
- Wide grip pulldown (LA019)
- Archer pull-up (LA008)

### Thickness
Best exercises:
- Close grip rows (LA059)
- Pendlay row (LA036)
- Single-arm DB row (LA045)

### Lower Lats
Best exercises:
- Close grip pulldown (LA020)
- Underhand pulldown (LA022)
- Yates row (LA037)

### Upper/Outer Lats
Best exercises:
- Wide pull-ups (LA002)
- High cable row (LA065)
- Wide pulldown (LA019)

## Key Training Principles

1. **Full stretch**: Extend arms completely at bottom of each rep
2. **Squeeze at contraction**: Pull shoulder blades together and down
3. **Elbow path**: Pull elbows toward hips for max lat activation
4. **Mind-muscle**: Focus on pulling with lats, not biceps
5. **Controlled negatives**: 2-3 second eccentric for growth

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Using momentum | Reduces lat tension | Strict form, control |
| Bicep dominant | Lats don't work | Pull with elbows, not hands |
| Short ROM | Misses stretch | Full extension each rep |
| Rounded shoulders | Injury risk | Retract scapulae |
| Shrugging | Traps take over | Depress shoulders |

| Goal | Sets/Week | Rep Range | Rest |
