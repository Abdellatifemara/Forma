# Olympic Weightlifting Exercises

## Overview

Olympic weightlifting comprises the snatch and clean & jerk. These explosive lifts develop power, coordination, mobility, and total-body strength. This database includes competition lifts, variations, and assistance exercises.

**Important**: Olympic lifts require proper coaching. Consider working with a qualified coach, especially when learning these movements.

---

## The Competition Lifts

### The Snatch
- Single movement: Floor to overhead
- Wide grip on barbell
- Catch in deep squat position
- Most technically demanding lift

### The Clean & Jerk
- Two movements: Clean (floor to shoulders), Jerk (shoulders to overhead)
- Shoulder-width grip for clean
- Various jerk styles (split, power, squat)
- Allows for heavier loads than snatch

---

## Positions & Terminology

### Starting Positions
- **Floor**: Barbell starts on ground
- **Hang (Above Knee)**: Barbell at mid-thigh
- **Hang (Below Knee)**: Barbell just below knees
- **Blocks**: Barbell elevated on blocks/risers

### Catch Positions
- **Full/Squat**: Deep squat catch
- **Power**: Catch with partial squat (thighs above parallel)
- **Muscle**: No squat, standing catch

### Key Positions
- **1st Pull**: Floor to knee
- **2nd Pull**: Knee to hip (explosive)
- **3rd Pull**: Under the bar (pulling under)
- **Catch**: Receiving position
- **Recovery**: Standing up from catch

## SNATCH EXERCISES

### OLY001: Power Snatch
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Advanced
**Primary Muscles**: Full body - posterior chain, shoulders, traps
**Secondary Muscles**: Core, legs, back

**Form Cues**:
- Wide snatch grip
- First pull: Slow, controlled to knees
- Second pull: Explosive hip extension
- Catch in partial squat (above parallel)
- Arms locked overhead

**Common Mistakes**:
- Early arm pull
- No hip contact
- Soft lockout
- Catching too deep for "power"

**Contraindications**:
- Shoulder injuries
- Wrist problems
- Lower back issues
- Inadequate mobility

**Programming**: 3-5 reps, 3-6 sets, 60-80% of snatch max

---

### OLY002: Full Snatch (Squat Snatch)
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Expert
**Primary Muscles**: Full body
**Secondary Muscles**: All major muscle groups

**Form Cues**:
- Explosive pull from floor
- Full hip and knee extension
- Aggressive pull under the bar
- Catch in deep overhead squat
- Stand with control

**Common Mistakes**:
- Incomplete extension
- Not pulling under aggressively
- Soft catch position
- Pressing out

**Contraindications**:
- All from Power Snatch plus
- Knee issues
- Ankle mobility limitations

**Programming**: 1-3 reps, 4-8 sets, competition lift

## JERK EXERCISES

### OLY026: Push Jerk
**Equipment**: Barbell
**Difficulty**: Intermediate
**Primary Muscles**: Shoulders, triceps, legs
**Secondary Muscles**: Core, traps

**Form Cues**:
- Start in front rack
- Dip and drive straight up
- Press under as you catch
- Feet move out slightly
- No split position

**Common Mistakes**:
- Dip forward
- Pressing out (no leg drive)
- Arms before legs

**Contraindications**:
- Shoulder injuries
- Wrist problems

**Programming**: 3-5 reps, 4-6 sets

---

**Primary Muscles**: Shoulders, triceps, legs
**Secondary Muscles**: Core, hip flexors

**Form Cues**:
- Dip and drive vertical
- Split into lunge position
- Front shin vertical
- Back knee bent
- Recover front foot, then back

**Common Mistakes**:
- Dip forward
- Short split
- Catching soft
- Poor recovery

**Contraindications**:
- Knee injuries
- Hip problems
- Shoulder issues

**Programming**: 2-5 reps, 4-6 sets

## PROGRAMMING RECOMMENDATIONS

### For Beginners
- Learn positions before adding weight
- Focus on mobility
- Start with hang variations
- Progress to full lifts gradually
- Work with a coach

### For Intermediate Lifters
- Technical work at 70-80%
- Strength work above competition lifts
- Complexes for training density
- Address specific weaknesses

### For Advanced/Competitive
- Periodized programming
- Peak for competition
- Heavy singles regularly
- Sport-specific accessory work
- Recovery management

### Sample Week Structure
- **Day 1**: Snatch variations + pulls
- **Day 2**: Clean & jerk variations + squats
- **Day 3**: Light technique + accessory
- **Day 4**: Competition lifts + pulls
- **Day 5**: Squats + accessory
- **Days 6-7**: Rest

---

## MOBILITY REQUIREMENTS

### Snatch
- Overhead squat depth
- Shoulder external rotation
- Thoracic extension
- Hip flexion
- Ankle dorsiflexion

### Clean
- Full front squat depth
- Wrist extension (front rack)
- Hip flexion
- Ankle dorsiflexion

### Jerk
- Split position mobility
- Overhead shoulder flexion
- Thoracic extension

---

## SAFETY CONSIDERATIONS

- Always warm up thoroughly
- Learn to bail safely
- Use appropriate flooring and bumper plates
- Don't max out without spotters/proper setup
- Address mobility before adding load
- Rest adequately between heavy sets
