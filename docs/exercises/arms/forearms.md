## Wrist Curl Variations (Flexors)

### FA001 - Barbell Wrist Curl
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Barbell |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Wrist flexors |
| **Secondary** | Finger flexors |
| **Contraindications** | Carpal tunnel syndrome, wrist tendinitis |

**Form Cues:**
- Forearms flat on bench, wrists hanging off edge
- Grip bar with palms up, slightly narrower than shoulder width
- Lower bar by extending wrists fully (let bar roll to fingers)
- Curl bar up by flexing wrists, squeeze at top
- Keep forearms stationary throughout

**Common Mistakes:**
- Moving forearms → Only wrists should move
- Going too heavy → This is an isolation exercise
- Bouncing at bottom → Control the negative

## Form Guides for Key Exercises

### Wrist Curls - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Sit at bench, forearms flat on pad, wrists over edge
2. **Grip:** Shoulder-width or slightly narrower, palms up
3. **Descent:** Lower bar by extending wrists, let it roll to fingertips
4. **Ascent:** Curl fingers first, then flex wrists
5. **Peak:** Squeeze wrist flexors hard at top
6. **Control:** 2 seconds up, 2-3 seconds down

**Tips:**
- Use a FULL range of motion
- Let bar roll to maximize finger flexor involvement
- Don't bounce at bottom
- Start very light - these are isolation exercises

### Reverse Curls - Detailed Form
1. **Setup:** Stand with feet hip-width, barbell in hands
2. **Grip:** Overhand (pronated), shoulder-width
3. **Elbows:** Pinned to sides, don't let them drift forward
4. **Curl:** Bring bar up in an arc, keeping wrists NEUTRAL
5. **Peak:** Squeeze brachioradialis at top (don't curl to shoulders)
6. **Lower:** Control the descent, don't drop

**Tips:**
- You'll use 40-60% of your regular curl weight
- Wrists stay straight - don't let them extend or flex
- Focus on the outer forearm (brachioradialis) contraction

### Farmer's Walks - Detailed Form
1. **Pickup:** Hinge at hips, flat back, grip weights
2. **Stand:** Drive through heels, stand tall
3. **Posture:** Shoulders back and down, chest up
4. **Walk:** Short, quick steps
5. **Grip:** Squeeze handles as hard as possible
6. **Breathing:** Brace core, breathe shallowly

**Tips:**
- Start with 50% bodyweight total (25% per hand)
- Progress to bodyweight total, then beyond
- Keep shoulders LEVEL - don't let one side drop
- Eyes forward, not down

## Common Mistakes & Fixes

| Mistake | Why It's Wrong | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Only training flexors | Creates imbalance, tennis elbow risk | Train extensors equally |
| Going too heavy on wrist curls | Can cause injury, poor ROM | Use lighter weight, full ROM |
| Neglecting grip training | Weak link in pulling exercises | Add farmer's walks, dead hangs |
| Training forearms before big lifts | Compromises grip for compounds | Train forearms last |
| Not using full ROM | Limits development | Let bar roll to fingertips |
| Ignoring finger extensors | Imbalance, injury risk | Use rubber band extensions |
| Skipping pronation/supination | Incomplete development | Add rotation work |

## Injury Prevention

### Tennis Elbow (Lateral Epicondylitis) Prevention
1. Balance flexor and extensor work
2. Use rubber band finger extensions daily
3. Don't overtrain forearms
4. Warm up wrists before lifting
5. Tyler Twist with FlexBar (rehab exercise)

### Wrist Pain Prevention
1. Strengthen through full ROM
2. Don't go too heavy too fast
3. Keep wrists neutral in compound lifts when possible
4. Stretch wrists regularly
5. Use wrist wraps for heavy pressing if needed

### Carpal Tunnel Prevention
1. Avoid excessive wrist flexion positions
2. Take breaks from repetitive gripping
3. Nerve glide exercises
4. Don't sleep with wrists bent
