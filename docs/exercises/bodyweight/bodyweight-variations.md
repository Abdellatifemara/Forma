# Bodyweight Exercise Variations Database

## Overview

Bodyweight training offers unparalleled accessibility and scalability, from rehabilitation to elite calisthenics. This database focuses on variations and progressions of fundamental movements, providing options for all fitness levels without equipment requirements.

## Training Principles

### Benefits of Bodyweight Training
- Zero equipment needed - train anywhere
- Improved body awareness and control
- Natural, functional movement patterns
- Infinite scalability through progressions
- Joint-friendly loading options
- Excellent for active recovery

### Progression Methods
1. **Leverage changes** - Incline/decline positions
2. **Range of motion** - Partial to full to extended
3. **Tempo manipulation** - Slow eccentrics, pauses
4. **Unilateral loading** - Single limb variations
5. **Stability challenges** - Reduced base of support
6. **Plyometric additions** - Explosive movements

## PUSH-UP VARIATIONS

### BW001 - Standard Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Core
**Form Cues:**
- Hands slightly wider than shoulders
- Body in straight line from head to heels
- Lower until chest near floor
- Push up to full arm extension
- Keep core engaged throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging or piking
- Flaring elbows to 90 degrees
- Incomplete range of motion
- Head dropping forward
**Contraindications:** Wrist pain, shoulder injury
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 10-20 reps, 2-3x/week

### BW002 - Incline Push-Up
**Equipment:** Elevated surface
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Lower Chest, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Hands on elevated surface
- Maintain straight body line
- Lower chest toward surface
- Press back to start
- Great regression for beginners
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Not lowering enough
- Surface too high or low
**Contraindications:** Wrist issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 12-20 reps, 2-3x/week

### BW003 - Decline Push-Up
**Equipment:** Elevated surface
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Upper Chest, Anterior Deltoid
**Secondary Muscles:** Triceps
**Form Cues:**
- Feet elevated on surface
- Hands on floor, shoulder-width
- Lower until nose near floor
- Press back up
- Core extra tight
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips dropping
- Looking forward (strain neck)
- Surface too high initially
**Contraindications:** High blood pressure, shoulder issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 8-15 reps, 2x/week

### BW004 - Diamond Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Inner Chest, Anterior Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Hands together, index fingers and thumbs touching

**Common Mistakes:**
- Elbows flaring out

## SQUAT VARIATIONS

### BW016 - Bodyweight Squat
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Glutes
**Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Calves
**Form Cues:**
- Feet shoulder-width apart
- Arms forward for balance
- Sit back and down
- Thighs at least parallel
- Drive through whole foot
**Common Mistakes:**
- Knees caving inward
- Rising onto toes
- Not going deep enough
- Rounding lower back
**Contraindications:** Acute knee injury
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 15-25 reps, 3x/week

## CORE EXERCISES

### BW039 - Plank
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Rectus Abdominis, Transverse Abdominis
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Glutes
**Form Cues:**
- Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders
- Body in straight line
- Engage core, squeeze glutes
- Don't let hips sag or pike
- Breathe normally
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Hips too high
- Holding breath
**Contraindications:** Shoulder injury, lower back pain
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 30-60 seconds, 3-5x/week

### BW040 - Side Plank
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Obliques
**Secondary Muscles:** Glutes (medius), Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Stack feet, forearm on floor
- Lift hips to form straight line
- Top arm up or on hip
- Don't let hips drop
- Hold position steadily
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Rolling forward or back
- Shoulder shrugging
**Contraindications:** Shoulder injury
**Programming:** 3 sets x 20-45 seconds each side, 3x/week

## Progression Tips

1. **Master basics first** before advancing
2. **Use tempo** to increase difficulty (3-1-3-1)
3. **Add pauses** at hardest positions
4. **Decrease leverage** gradually
5. **Record progress** to track improvements
6. **Train consistently** (frequency > intensity)
7. **Include mobility work** for full ROM
8. **Rest adequately** between sessions
