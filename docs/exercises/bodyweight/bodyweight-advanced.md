# Advanced & Additional Bodyweight Exercises

## Overview

This extended database provides additional no-equipment exercises for those training at home, traveling, or without gym access. These movements range from accessible beginner options to challenging advanced progressions.

## Why No-Equipment Training Matters

- **Accessibility**: Train anywhere, anytime
- **Cost-effective**: No gym membership needed
- **Functional**: Natural movement patterns
- **Injury prevention**: Body awareness development
- **Scalable**: Modify any exercise for your level
- **Time-efficient**: No travel to gym

## ADDITIONAL PUSH-UP VARIATIONS

### BWA001 - Typewriter Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Core, Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Wide push-up position
- Lower and slide toward one hand
- Stay low, slide across to other side
- Push up from that side
- Alternate directions
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rising up during slide
- Not going low enough
- Rushing movement
**Contraindications:** Shoulder instability
**Programming:** 3 sets x 4-6 each side, 2x/week

### BWA002 - Aztec Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Expert
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Core, Hip Flexors
**Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Explosive push-up
- Touch hands to toes mid-air
- Land softly in push-up position
- Requires significant power and flexibility
- Master clap push-ups first
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not enough height
- Poor landing
- Flexibility limitation
**Contraindications:** Wrist/shoulder problems
**Programming:** 2-3 sets x 3-5 reps, 1x/week

### BWA003 - Planche Push-Up (Tuck)
**Equipment:** None or parallettes
**Difficulty:** Expert
**Primary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Chest
**Secondary Muscles:** Core, Biceps (isometric)
**Form Cues:**
- Achieve tuck planche position
- Lower body slightly
- Push back to tuck planche
- Maintain forward lean
- Keep hips at hand height
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not enough lean
- Breaking tuck position
- Arms bending unevenly
**Contraindications:** Wrist issues, shoulder problems
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 3-5 reps, 2x/week

### BWA004 - Knuckle Push-Up
**Equipment:** None (soft surface recommended)
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Forearms, Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Make fists, knuckles on floor
- Wrists stay straight
- Perform standard push-up
- Harder on forearms
- Safer for wrist issues
**Common Mistakes:**
- Wrists bending
- Knuckles rolling
- Poor surface choice
**Contraindications:** Knuckle/hand injuries
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 10-15 reps, 2-3x/week

### BWA005 - Fingertip Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate-Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Finger Flexors, Forearms
**Form Cues:**
- Support on fingertips, not flat hands
- Start with all 5 fingers
- Progress to fewer fingers
- Full push-up range of motion
- Builds grip strength
**Common Mistakes:**
- Fingers collapsing
- Reducing range of motion
- Progressing too fast
**Contraindications:** Finger injuries
**Programming:** 3 sets x 8-12 reps, 2x/week

### BWA006 - Superman Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Expert
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Shoulders
**Secondary Muscles:** Core
**Form Cues:**
- Explosive push from bottom
- Extend arms forward mid-air (like flying)
- Return to push-up position
- Land with soft elbows
- Requires significant power
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not enough height
- Arms not extending
- Hard landing
**Contraindications:** Shoulder/wrist issues
**Programming:** 2-3 sets x 3-5 reps, 1x/week

### BWA007 - Tiger Bend Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps, Chest
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Lower to elbows (sphinx position)
- Push back up to hands
- Keep body rigid throughout
- Arms stay close to body
- Control the transition
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Using momentum
- Elbows flaring
**Contraindications:** Elbow problems
**Programming:** 3 sets x 6-10 reps, 2x/week

### BWA008 - Grasshopper Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Obliques
**Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Push-up position
- Lower while swinging leg under body
- Rotate to touch opposite elbow
- Return and alternate sides
- Fluid motion
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not rotating enough
- Incomplete push-up
- Losing balance
**Contraindications:** Lower back issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 6-8 each side, 2x/week

### BWA009 - Reverse Push-Up
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps, Rear Deltoid
**Secondary Muscles:** Lats
**Form Cues:**
- Lie face up, hands by hips
- Fingers pointing toward feet
- Press down to lift upper body
- Lower with control
- Keep hips on ground
**Common Mistakes:**
- Lifting hips
- Wrists uncomfortable
- Incomplete range
**Contraindications:** Wrist issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 10-15 reps, 2x/week

### BWA010 - Push-Up with Rotation
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Obliques
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders
**Form Cues:**
- Perform push-up
- At top, rotate to side plank
- Extend top arm to ceiling
- Return and alternate
- Control throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rushing rotation
- Not stabilizing at top
- Incomplete push-up
**Contraindications:** Shoulder instability
**Programming:** 3 sets x 8-10 total, 2x/week

## ANIMAL MOVEMENTS

### BWA011 - Bear Crawl (Forward)
**Equipment:** Space
**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Core, Shoulders
**Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Quadriceps
**Form Cues:**
- Hands and feet on ground
- Knees hover 2-3 inches
- Move opposite hand/foot together
- Keep hips low and stable
- Controlled movement
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips too high
- Same side moving together
- Moving too fast
**Contraindications:** Wrist pain
**Programming:** 3 sets x 30-60 seconds, 2-3x/week

### BWA012 - Bear Crawl (Backward)
**Equipment:** Space
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Core, Shoulders
**Secondary Muscles:** Hip Flexors
**Form Cues:**
- Same position as forward bear crawl
- Move in reverse
- Requires more coordination
- Keep looking forward
- Maintain low position
**Common Mistakes:**
- Looking backward (neck strain)
- Rising up
- Poor coordination
**Contraindications:** Wrist issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 30-60 seconds, 2x/week

### BWA013 - Frog Jump
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Glutes
**Secondary Muscles:** Calves, Core
**Form Cues:**
- Deep squat, hands on floor
- Jump forward like a frog
- Land in squat position
- Repeat continuously
- Keep chest up
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not going deep enough
- Landing hard
- Poor coordination
**Contraindications:** Knee issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 10-15 jumps, 2x/week

### BWA014 - Crocodile Walk
**Equipment:** Space
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Chest, Core
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Hip Flexors
**Form Cues:**
- Low push-up position

- Chest nearly touches ground
- Elbows bent throughout
- Like crawling under barbed wire
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rising too high
- Moving too fast
- Losing form when fatigued
**Contraindications:** Shoulder problems
**Programming:** 3 sets x 20-40 seconds, 2x/week

### BWA015 - Lizard Crawl

**Primary Muscles:** Core, Hip Flexors
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Chest
**Form Cues:**

- Stay close to ground
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rising too high

- Rushing movement

### BWA016 - Duck Walk

**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Glutes
**Secondary Muscles:** Calves

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rising up
- Falling forward

**Primary Muscles:** Shoulders, Core

- Stay in squat throughout
**Common Mistakes:**

### BWA019 - Crab Reach
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Core, Shoulders

**Form Cues:**

- Hips dropping

### BWA020 - Scorpion Stretch (Dynamic)
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Chest
**Secondary Muscles:** Obliques
**Form Cues:**
- Lie face down, arms out
- Lift one leg, reach toward opposite hand
- Rotate through spine
- Return and alternate
- Controlled movement
**Common Mistakes:**
- Lifting shoulder off floor
- Forcing range
- Moving too quickly
**Contraindications:** Lower back pain
**Programming:** 2-3 sets x 8-10 each side, daily

## ISOMETRIC HOLDS

### BWA021 - Wall Sit Variations
**Equipment:** Wall
**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Glutes
**Form Cues:**
- Back flat against wall
- Progress depth gradually
- Try single-leg holds
- Add arm movements
- Breathe steadily
**Common Mistakes:**
- Leaning on thighs
- Shallow angle
- Holding breath
**Contraindications:** Knee pain
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 30-60 seconds, 3x/week

### BWA022 - Plank Shoulder Tap
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Core, Shoulders
**Secondary Muscles:** Chest
**Form Cues:**
- High plank position
- Tap opposite shoulder with hand
- Minimize hip rotation
- Alternate hands
- Core engaged throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive hip sway
- Moving too fast
- Hips piking
**Contraindications:** Shoulder issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 10-15 each side, 3x/week

### BWA023 - Superman Hold
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Erector Spinae
**Secondary Muscles:** Glutes
**Form Cues:**
- Lie face down
- Lift arms, chest, and legs
- Hold at top position
- Squeeze lower back
- Breathe normally
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hyperextending neck
- Not holding long enough
- Legs too low
**Contraindications:** Lower back injury
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 20-45 seconds, 3x/week

- Achieve superman position
- Roll back to hollow
- Arms and legs stay elevated
**Common Mistakes:**
- Using arms to roll
- Breaking position
- Legs touching ground
**Contraindications:** Lower back issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 5-10 rolls, 2-3x/week

### BWA027 - Low Squat Hold (Malasana)
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Hip Flexors, Ankles
**Secondary Muscles:** Adductors
**Form Cues:**
- Full depth squat
- Heels down if possible
- Elbows press knees out
- Chest up, spine long
- Relax and breathe
**Common Mistakes:**
- Heels lifting
- Rounding excessively
- Knees collapsing
**Contraindications:** Knee pain in position
**Programming:** Accumulate 5-10 minutes daily

### BWA028 - Single-Leg Balance with Eyes Closed
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Ankle Stabilizers
**Secondary Muscles:** Core, Glutes
**Form Cues:**
- Stand on one leg
- Close eyes
- Maintain balance
- Arms out for help if needed
- Progress duration gradually
**Common Mistakes:**
- Peeking
- Not challenging enough
- Giving up too quickly
**Contraindications:** Severe balance disorders
**Programming:** 3 sets x 20-60 seconds each leg, daily

### BWA029 - Copenhagen Plank
**Equipment:** Elevated surface
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Adductors
**Secondary Muscles:** Obliques, Core
**Form Cues:**

- Keep hips from dropping
- Switch sides
**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips sagging
- Top leg sliding
- Not engaging adductors
**Contraindications:** Groin strains
**Programming:** 3 sets x 15-30 seconds each side, 2-3x/week

### BWA030 - Arch Hold (Gymnastics)
**Equipment:** None
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Erector Spinae, Glutes
**Secondary Muscles:** Shoulders, Hamstrings
**Form Cues:**
- Lie face down, arms overhead
- Lift arms, chest, legs simultaneously
- Form shallow arch shape
- Squeeze glutes hard
- Hold position
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not lifting legs high enough

- Holding breath

## Tips for No-Equipment Training

1. **Use furniture creatively** - Chairs for dips, tables for rows
2. **Towels are your friend** - Sliders, rows, stretches
3. **Stairs add options** - Incline/decline, step-ups, calf work
4. **Backpacks add load** - Fill with books for resistance
5. **Walls are equipment** - Wall sits, handstands, push-ups
6. **Partner training** - Double the exercises, double the fun
7. **Time under tension** - Slow down movements for intensity
8. **Isometric holds** - Pause at hardest position
9. **Increase range** - Deficit push-ups, deep squats
10. **Train daily** - Bodyweight allows higher frequency
