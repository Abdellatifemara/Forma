# Complete Yoga Poses Database

## Overview

Yoga combines physical postures (asanas), breathing techniques (pranayama), and meditation for holistic fitness. This database covers poses from beginner to advanced, organized by category and body position.

### Benefits of Yoga
- Improved flexibility and mobility
- Better balance and stability
- Increased strength (especially core)
- Stress reduction and mental clarity
- Better posture and body awareness
- Enhanced breathing capacity

### Yoga Terminology
- **Asana**: Physical posture
- **Vinyasa**: Flow between poses
- **Pranayama**: Breath control
- **Drishti**: Gaze point
- **Bandha**: Energy lock (engagement)

## Standing Poses

### YG001 - Mountain Pose (Tadasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Posture, awareness
- **Secondary**: Legs, core
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand with feet together or hip-width
  - Weight even across both feet
  - Engage quads, lift kneecaps
  - Tailbone neutral, core engaged
  - Shoulders back and down
  - Arms at sides, palms forward or together
  - Crown of head reaching up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Weight on heels → Spread across whole foot
  - Locked knees → Micro-bend
  - Shoulders up → Relax down

### YG002 - Forward Fold (Uttanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, lower back
- **Secondary**: Calves
- **Contraindications**: Lower back injury (bend knees)
- **Form Cues**:
  - From standing, hinge at hips
  - Let head hang, release neck
  - Hands to floor, shins, or blocks
  - Keep weight forward in feet
  - Can bend knees if needed
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding from lower back → Hinge from hips
  - Locking knees → Soft bend OK

### YG003 - Halfway Lift (Ardha Uttanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Back extensors
- **Secondary**: Hamstrings
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - From forward fold, lift torso parallel to floor
  - Hands on shins or fingertips on floor
  - Flat back, gaze forward
  - Core engaged
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Keep flat
  - Looking up too far → Neutral neck

### YG004 - Chair Pose (Utkatasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Core, shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet together or hip-width
  - Sit back as if into a chair
  - Arms overhead, biceps by ears
  - Weight in heels
  - Keep chest lifted
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knees over toes → Sit back more
  - Lower back arching → Tuck tailbone slightly

### YG005 - Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Shoulders, core
- **Contraindications**: Hip/knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Front knee bent 90 degrees over ankle
  - Back leg straight, foot at 45 degrees
  - Hips square to front
  - Arms overhead, palms together or apart
  - Chest lifted
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips not square → Rotate back hip forward
  - Front knee caving → Track over toes

### YG006 - Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, hip abductors
- **Secondary**: Shoulders, core
- **Contraindications**: Hip/knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Front knee bent 90 degrees
  - Back leg straight, foot parallel to back of mat
  - Hips open to side
  - Arms extended, gaze over front fingers
  - Shoulders over hips
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning forward → Stay centered
  - Front knee collapsing → Push out over toes

### YG007 - Warrior III (Virabhadrasana III)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, glutes, balance
- **Secondary**: Core, back
- **Contraindications**: Balance issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg
  - Hinge forward, back leg lifts
  - Body forms T-shape
  - Arms forward, by sides, or back
  - Hips level
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hip of lifted leg rotating up → Keep level
  - Standing knee locking → Micro-bend

### YG008 - Extended Side Angle (Utthita Parsvakonasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques, hips
- **Secondary**: Shoulders, legs
- **Contraindications**: Hip issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - From Warrior II, lean toward front leg
  - Bottom hand to floor or block outside front foot
  - Top arm extends overhead, one long line
  - Gaze up at top hand
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Collapsing onto front thigh → Keep core engaged
  - Top shoulder rolling forward → Open chest

### YG009 - Triangle Pose (Trikonasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, obliques
- **Secondary**: Hips, shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Lower back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide stance, front foot forward, back foot parallel
  - Both legs straight
  - Reach forward then down, hand to shin/block/floor
  - Top arm straight up
  - Open chest to ceiling
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending front knee → Keep straight
  - Rounding toward floor → Maintain side bend

### YG010 - Revolved Triangle (Parivrtta Trikonasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, thoracic rotation
- **Secondary**: Balance, core
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Triangle setup but rotate toward front leg
  - Opposite hand to floor/block outside front foot
  - Top arm reaches up
  - Square hips as much as possible
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Back hip dropping → Keep level
  - Not rotating enough → Full thoracic twist

### YG011 - Half Moon (Ardha Chandrasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Balance, hip abductors
- **Secondary**: Core, hamstrings
- **Contraindications**: Balance issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - From Triangle, shift weight to front foot
  - Lift back leg parallel to floor
  - Bottom hand on floor/block
  - Open hips and chest to ceiling
  - Top arm reaches up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Standing leg locking → Micro-bend
  - Closing off chest → Stay open

### YG012 - Tree Pose (Vrksasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Balance
- **Secondary**: Hip abductors, core
- **Contraindications**: Ankle/knee injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg
  - Place other foot on inner thigh or calf (not knee)
  - Hands at heart or overhead
  - Find a drishti (gaze point)
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Foot on knee → Above or below knee only
  - Hip jutting out → Keep hips level

### YG013 - Eagle Pose (Garudasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Balance, hip/shoulder mobility
- **Secondary**: Focus
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg, wrap other leg around
  - Sink into standing leg
  - Wrap arms (opposite arm on top)
  - Elbows lift
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Standing up too tall → Sink deeper
  - Elbows dropping → Lift up

### YG014 - Standing Split (Urdhva Prasarita Eka Padasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, balance
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Hamstring injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - From forward fold, lift one leg high
  - Keep hips square
  - Hands on floor or holding standing ankle
  - Work toward vertical split
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Opening hip → Keep square
  - Locking standing knee → Soft bend

### YG015 - Dancer Pose (Natarajasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Balance, quad stretch
- **Secondary**: Shoulders, back
- **Contraindications**: Back/knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand on one leg
  - Grab back foot with same-side hand
  - Kick back into hand, leaning forward
  - Extend opposite arm forward
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Losing balance → Find drishti
  - Cramping hamstring → Don't kick too hard

### YG016 - Extended Hand to Big Toe (Utthita Hasta Padangusthasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, balance
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Hamstring injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand, grab big toe with hand
  - Extend leg forward (can keep bent)
  - Progress to leg out to side
  - Standing leg strong
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Stay tall
  - Forcing leg straight → Can keep bent

### YG017 - Pyramid Pose (Parsvottanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Balance, back
- **Contraindications**: Hamstring injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Staggered stance, both legs straight
  - Hips square to front
  - Fold over front leg
  - Hands on floor/blocks or reverse prayer
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Back hip opening → Square forward
  - Rounding back → Lead with chest

### YG018 - Wide-Legged Forward Fold (Prasarita Padottanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, adductors
- **Secondary**: Back
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide stance, feet parallel
  - Fold forward from hips
  - Hands on floor, head toward floor
  - Can grab ankles or clasp hands behind back
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Feet turning out → Keep parallel
  - Rounding excessively → Flat back as long as possible

### YG019 - Goddess Pose (Utkata Konasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Inner thighs, quads
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: Hip issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide stance, feet turned out 45 degrees
  - Bend knees deeply, track over toes
  - Arms in goalpost or overhead
  - Tailbone neutral
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knees caving → Push out
  - Leaning forward → Stay upright

### YG020 - Humble Warrior (Baddha Virabhadrasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, hips
- **Secondary**: Legs
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - From Warrior I, clasp hands behind back
  - Fold inside front leg
  - Head toward floor
  - Arms lift overhead behind you
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Back knee bending → Keep straight
  - Forcing shoulders → Work gradually

## Seated Poses

### YG021 - Easy Pose (Sukhasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hip opening
- **Secondary**: Posture
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues (sit on block)
- **Form Cues**:
  - Cross-legged seat
  - Sit on blanket/block if hips are tight
  - Spine tall, crown reaching up
  - Hands on knees or in lap
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Elevate hips
  - Shoulders hunching → Roll back

### YG022 - Staff Pose (Dandasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Posture, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Legs
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Sit with legs extended
  - Flex feet, engage quads
  - Spine tall, hands by hips
  - Shoulders back and down
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Engage core, sit on block
  - Knees bending → Can place rolled blanket under

### YG023 - Seated Forward Fold (Paschimottanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, back
- **Secondary**: Calves
- **Contraindications**: Back injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - From staff pose, reach forward
  - Lead with chest, not head
  - Grab feet, ankles, or use strap
  - Relax into pose over time
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding to reach feet → Lead with chest
  - Forcing → Relax and breathe

## Supine Poses (On Back)

### YG036 - Corpse Pose (Savasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Relaxation
- **Secondary**: Integration
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie flat on back
  - Legs slightly apart, feet fall open
  - Arms at sides, palms up
  - Close eyes, release all effort
  - Breathe naturally
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Remaining tense → Consciously relax each body part
  - Fidgeting → Settle and be still

### YG037 - Reclined Bound Angle (Supta Baddha Konasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Hips, chest opening
- **Secondary**: Relaxation
- **Contraindications**: Groin injury (support knees)
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, soles of feet together
  - Let knees fall open
  - Can support knees with blocks
  - Arms out or on belly
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Forcing knees down → Let gravity work
  - Lower back discomfort → Place blanket under hips

### YG038 - Happy Baby (Ananda Balasana)

- **Primary**: Hips, lower back
- **Secondary**: Inner thighs
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues

- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Head lifting → Keep on floor

### YG039 - Supine Twist (Supta Matsyendrasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Thoracic rotation, lower back
- **Secondary**: Glutes
- **Contraindications**: Spinal issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, hug one knee to chest
  - Draw knee across body to floor

- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Opposite shoulder lifting → Keep grounded

### YG040 - Legs Up the Wall (Viparita Karani)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Relaxation, circulation
- **Secondary**: Hamstrings
- **Contraindications**: Glaucoma, uncontrolled hypertension
- **Form Cues**:
  - Sit sideways at wall, swing legs up
  - Scoot buttocks to wall
  - Legs rest on wall, body on floor
  - Arms out to sides
  - Stay 5-15 minutes
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Too far from wall → Scoot closer
  - Discomfort → Place blanket under hips

### YG041 - Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)

### YG042 - Wheel Pose (Urdhva Dhanurasana)

- **Primary**: Back extensors, shoulders

## Prone Poses (On Stomach)

### YG049 - Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Back extensors
- **Secondary**: Chest, shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Back injury (stay low)
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie face down, hands under shoulders
  - Press up, keeping hips on floor
  - Elbows can stay bent (baby cobra) or straighten
  - Shoulders back and down
  - Look forward, not up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Shoulders shrugging → Roll back and down
  - Crunching neck → Keep neutral

### YG050 - Upward Facing Dog (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Back extensors, chest
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Back injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - From plank or prone, press hands and tops of feet
  - Lift thighs and hips off floor
  - Only hands and feet touch floor
  - Open chest, shoulders back
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Thighs on floor → Lift everything
  - Shoulders by ears → Roll back

### YG051 - Sphinx Pose
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Back extensors
- **Secondary**: Chest
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie prone, prop on forearms
  - Elbows under shoulders
  - Gentle backbend
  - Can stay here for yin practice
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Shoulders shrugging → Relax down
  - Collapsing → Press forearms down

### YG052 - Locust Pose (Salabhasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Back extensors, glutes
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Back injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie prone, arms at sides
  - Lift chest, arms, and legs off floor
  - Reach back through fingertips and toes
  - Keep head neutral
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Looking up too far → Keep neck neutral
  - Legs apart → Keep together

### YG053 - Bow Pose (Dhanurasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Back extensors, quads
- **Secondary**: Chest, shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Back injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie prone, bend knees, grab ankles
  - Kick feet into hands, lift chest and thighs
  - Create bow shape
  - Rock gently if desired
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knees splaying → Keep hip-width
  - Only lifting chest → Lift thighs too

### YG054 - Superman Pose
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Back extensors
- **Secondary**: Glutes, shoulders
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie prone, arms extended overhead
  - Lift arms, chest, and legs simultaneously
  - Hold or pulse
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Crunching neck → Keep neutral
  - Holding breath → Keep breathing

### YG055 - Frog Pose (Bhekasana)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Quads, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Back
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie prone, bend knees
  - Reach back and grab feet from outside
  - Press feet toward floor beside hips
  - Lift chest
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knee pain → Come out immediately
  - Forcing → Work gradually

## Arm Balances

### YG056 - Plank Pose (Phalakasana)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Arms, legs
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hands under shoulders, fingers spread
  - Body in straight line from head to heels
  - Core engaged, don't let hips sag
  - Gaze slightly forward of hands
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips sagging → Engage core
  - Hips piking → Lower into line

### YG057 - Side Plank (Vasisthasana)
