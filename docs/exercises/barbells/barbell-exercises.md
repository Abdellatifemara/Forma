# Barbell Exercises Database

## Overview

Barbell training is the cornerstone of strength development, offering unmatched loading potential and progressive overload capability. This database covers essential barbell movements for all major muscle groups and training goals.

## Training Principles

### Benefits of Barbell Training
- Maximum loading capacity for strength development
- Bilateral training for balanced development
- Precise progressive overload with small weight increments
- Compound movement patterns for functional strength
- Time-efficient training with high muscle recruitment

### Equipment Variations
- **Standard Barbell**: 20kg (45lb) Olympic bar
- **Women's Bar**: 15kg (35lb) shorter bar
- **Trap/Hex Bar**: Deadlift and shrug variations
- **EZ Curl Bar**: Reduced wrist strain for curls
- **Safety Squat Bar**: Easier shoulder positioning
- **Swiss/Football Bar**: Neutral grip pressing

## CHEST EXERCISES

### BB001 - Barbell Bench Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Flat Bench, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major (Sternal)
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Triceps
**Form Cues:**
- Retract and depress scapulae
- Arch back slightly, feet flat
- Lower bar to mid-chest level
- Press up and slightly back
- Keep wrists stacked over elbows
**Common Mistakes:**
- Bouncing bar off chest
- Flaring elbows excessively
- Lifting hips off bench
- Uneven bar path
**Contraindications:** Acute shoulder injury, shoulder impingement
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 3-8 reps, 2-3x/week

### BB002 - Incline Barbell Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Incline Bench (30-45Â°), Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major (Clavicular)
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Triceps
**Form Cues:**
- Set bench to 30-45 degree angle
- Plant feet firmly on floor
- Lower bar to upper chest
- Press in slight arc toward face
- Maintain tight upper back
**Common Mistakes:**
- Bench angle too steep (becomes shoulder press)
- Lifting hips off bench
- Bar path too vertical
**Contraindications:** Shoulder impingement, AC joint issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 6-10 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB003 - Decline Barbell Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Decline Bench, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major (Lower)
**Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Anterior Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Secure legs under pads
- Lower bar to lower chest
- Press up and slightly toward head
- Keep shoulder blades retracted
- Maintain core tension
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive decline angle
- Bouncing bar off chest
- Not securing legs properly
**Contraindications:** High blood pressure, glaucoma, recent head injury
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 6-10 reps, 1x/week

### BB004 - Close-Grip Bench Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Flat Bench, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Triceps, Pectoralis Major
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Grip shoulder-width or slightly narrower
- Keep elbows closer to body
- Lower bar to lower chest
- Focus on triceps contraction
- Lock out fully at top
**Common Mistakes:**
- Grip too narrow (wrist strain)
- Flaring elbows out wide
- Incomplete range of motion
**Contraindications:** Wrist injuries, elbow tendinitis
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 8-12 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB005 - Floor Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Rack or Blocks
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Lie on floor with knees bent or legs straight
- Lower until triceps touch floor
- Pause briefly, then press up
- Maintain shoulder blade retraction
- Keep core engaged throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Bouncing arms off floor
- Losing shoulder position
- Arching excessively
**Contraindications:** Shoulder instability
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 6-10 reps, 1x/week

### BB006 - Guillotine Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Flat Bench, Rack
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Pectoralis Major (Upper/Clavicular)
**Secondary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Triceps
**Form Cues:**
- Use lighter weight than standard bench
- Lower bar toward neck/upper chest
- Elbows flared at 90 degrees
- Control descent completely
- Press up in reverse arc
**Common Mistakes:**
- Using too heavy weight
- Losing control of bar
- Not having spotter
**Contraindications:** Shoulder problems, neck issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 8-12 reps, 1x/week (with spotter)

## BACK EXERCISES

### BB007 - Barbell Bent-Over Row
**Equipment:** Barbell
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Latissimus Dorsi, Rhomboids
**Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Rear Deltoid, Erector Spinae
**Form Cues:**
- Hinge at hips to 45-degree angle
- Keep back flat, core braced
- Pull bar to lower chest/upper abdomen
- Squeeze shoulder blades together
- Lower with control
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive body English
- Rounding lower back
- Standing too upright
- Pulling with arms only
**Contraindications:** Acute lower back injury
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 6-12 reps, 2x/week

### BB008 - Pendlay Row
**Equipment:** Barbell
**Difficulty:** Intermediate-Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Latissimus Dorsi, Rhomboids
**Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Erector Spinae
**Form Cues:**
- Start with bar on floor each rep
- Back parallel to ground
- Explosively pull bar to chest
- Lower under control to floor
- Reset position each rep
**Common Mistakes:**
- Not touching floor each rep
- Raising torso during pull
- Using momentum
**Contraindications:** Lower back injuries, disc issues
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 5-8 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB009 - T-Bar Row
**Equipment:** Barbell, Landmine or Corner
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Latissimus Dorsi, Middle Back
**Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Rear Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Straddle bar with feet wide
- Hinge at hips, neutral spine
- Pull toward chest/abdomen
- Squeeze at top position
- Control the negative
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive hip movement
- Rounding upper back
- Using arm momentum
**Contraindications:** Lower back issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 8-12 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB010 - Barbell Shrug
**Equipment:** Barbell
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Primary Muscles:** Upper Trapezius
**Secondary Muscles:** Levator Scapulae
**Form Cues:**
- Hold bar at hip level, arms straight
- Elevate shoulders straight up
- Hold contraction at top
- Lower slowly with control
- Keep chin neutral
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rolling shoulders (unnecessary)
- Using momentum
- Shrugging too fast
**Contraindications:** Neck injuries, cervical issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 10-15 reps, 2x/week

### BB011 - Seal Row
**Equipment:** Barbell, Elevated Bench
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Latissimus Dorsi, Rhomboids
**Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Rear Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Lie face down on elevated bench
- Arms hanging straight down
- Pull bar to bench/chest
- Squeeze shoulder blades together
- Lower with full control
**Common Mistakes:**
- Bench too low
- Using body momentum
- Incomplete range of motion
**Contraindications:** Rib injuries
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 8-12 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB012 - Meadows Row
**Equipment:** Barbell, Landmine
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Latissimus Dorsi, Teres Major
**Secondary Muscles:** Biceps, Rear Deltoid
**Form Cues:**
- Stand perpendicular to landmine
- Staggered stance, hinge forward
- Pull end of bar toward hip
- Drive elbow back and up
- Control eccentric portion
**Common Mistakes:**
- Standing too far from bar
- Not hinging enough
- Rotating torso excessively
**Contraindications:** Lower back injuries
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 8-12 reps each side, 1x/week

## SHOULDER EXERCISES

### BB013 - Standing Overhead Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Medial Deltoid
**Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Upper Chest, Core
**Form Cues:**
- Start bar at clavicle level
- Brace core, squeeze glutes
- Press bar overhead in straight line
- Move head through at top
- Lower with control to shoulders
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive back arch
- Bar drifting forward
- Not bracing core
- Incomplete lockout
**Contraindications:** Shoulder impingement, lower back issues
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 5-10 reps, 2x/week

### BB014 - Push Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Deltoids, Triceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Core, Legs (assist)
**Form Cues:**
- Start in front rack position
- Dip knees slightly (quarter squat)
- Explosively drive through legs
- Press bar overhead
- Catch with locked arms
**Common Mistakes:**
- Dip too deep
- Not using leg drive
- Pressing before drive completes
**Contraindications:** Knee issues, shoulder problems
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 3-6 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB015 - Seated Military Press
**Equipment:** Barbell, Upright Bench, Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Anterior Deltoid, Medial Deltoid
**Secondary Muscles:** Triceps, Upper Trapezius
**Form Cues:**
- Sit with back supported
- Unrack bar to shoulders
- Press straight up overhead
- Lower to chin level
- Keep core engaged throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Arching back excessively
- Pressing behind neck (risky)
- Using excessive weight
**Contraindications:** Shoulder impingement, neck issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 6-10 reps, 1-2x/week

## LEG EXERCISES

### BB030 - Back Squat
**Equipment:** Barbell, Squat Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Glutes
**Secondary Muscles:** Hamstrings, Adductors, Core
**Form Cues:**
- Bar on upper traps (high bar) or rear delts (low bar)
- Feet shoulder-width or wider
- Break at hips and knees together
- Descend to parallel or below
- Drive through whole foot
**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive forward lean
- Knees caving inward
- Rising onto toes
- Rounding lower back
**Contraindications:** Acute knee injury, severe back problems
**Programming:** 3-5 sets x 3-10 reps, 2-3x/week

### BB031 - Front Squat
**Equipment:** Barbell, Squat Rack
**Difficulty:** Intermediate-Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Glutes, Core
**Form Cues:**
- Bar on front delts, elbows high
- Clean grip or cross-arm grip
- Keep torso upright
- Descend between legs
- Drive through midfoot
**Common Mistakes:**
- Elbows dropping
- Forward lean
- Not staying upright
**Contraindications:** Wrist limitations, knee issues
**Programming:** 3-4 sets x 3-8 reps, 1-2x/week

### BB032 - Zercher Squat
**Equipment:** Barbell, Squat Rack or Floor
**Difficulty:** Advanced
**Primary Muscles:** Quadriceps, Core
**Secondary Muscles:** Glutes, Biceps (isometric)
**Form Cues:**
- Bar held in elbow crooks
- Arms crossed for support
- Keep torso very upright
- Squat between arms
- Maintain balance throughout
**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding upper back
- Bar slipping from elbows
- Not staying upright
**Contraindications:** Elbow injuries, bicep issues
**Programming:** 3 sets x 5-8 reps, 1x/week

### BB033 - Box Squat
**Equipment:** Barbell, Squat Rack, Box/Bench
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Primary Muscles:** Glutes, Hamstrings, Quadriceps
**Secondary Muscles:** Core, Adductors

- Bouncing off box
- Not pausing

## Safety Tips

1. **Always use collars** to secure weights
2. **Use a spotter** for heavy bench and squat
3. **Set safety pins** appropriately in rack
4. **Warm up progressively** before heavy work
5. **Master technique** before adding weight
6. **Use lifting belt** for near-maximal lifts only
7. **Keep bar path efficient** close to body
8. **Brace core** before every heavy rep
