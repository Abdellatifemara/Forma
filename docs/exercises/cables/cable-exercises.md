# Cable Exercises Database

## Overview

Cable machines provide constant tension throughout the entire range of motion, making them excellent for muscle building, rehabilitation, and functional training. The versatile angles and attachments allow endless exercise variations.

---

## Cable Training Benefits

### Advantages
- **Constant Tension**: Unlike free weights, resistance doesn't vary with leverage
- **Multi-Angle Training**: Adjust pulley height for different angles
- **Unilateral Work**: Easy to train one side at a time
- **Safety**: Easier to bail out than free weights
- **Variety**: Multiple attachments for different exercises
- **Joint-Friendly**: Smooth, controlled resistance

### Cable Attachments
- Straight bar
- EZ curl bar
- V-bar / triangle
- Rope
- Single handle (D-handle)
- Ankle strap
- Wide lat bar
- Rotating handles

## CHEST EXERCISES

### CBL001: Cable Chest Press (Mid)
**Pulley Position**: Mid/shoulder height
**Attachment**: D-handles
**Difficulty**: Beginner
**Primary Muscles**: Pectoralis major
**Secondary Muscles**: Triceps, front deltoids

**Form Cues**:
- Stand with split stance for stability
- Press handles forward at chest height
- Squeeze chest at full extension
- Control the return
- Maintain slight forward lean

**Common Mistakes**:
- Elbows flaring too wide
- Using momentum
- Not squeezing at peak contraction

**Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement

**Programming**: 10-15 reps, 3-4 sets

## PROGRAMMING RECOMMENDATIONS

### For Muscle Building
- Include cable work for constant tension
- Use after heavy compound movements
- Higher rep ranges (12-15)
- Focus on mind-muscle connection
- Great for drop sets

### For Strength
- Supplement free weight work
- Use for weak point training
- Lower rep ranges (8-10)
- Progressive overload

### For Rehabilitation
- Controlled environment
- Easy to adjust resistance
- Specific angle training
- Work with PT guidance

### For Beginners
- Learn movement patterns
- Safe to train close to failure
- Focus on form over weight
- Full range of motion

## CABLE TRAINING TIPS

1. **Adjust pulley height** for optimal angle
2. **Control both phases** - never let weight slam
3. **Mind-muscle connection** - focus on target muscle
4. **Use full range** of motion
5. **Vary attachments** for different stimulus
6. **Maintain posture** - don't compensate with body
7. **Progress gradually** - add weight when form is solid
8. **Mix with free weights** for complete development
