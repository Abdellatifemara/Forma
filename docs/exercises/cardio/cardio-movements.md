# Cardio & Conditioning Movements

## Overview

Cardiovascular exercise improves heart health, endurance, and metabolic function. This database covers low-impact to high-intensity movements for all fitness levels.

---

## Cardio Training Principles

### Energy Systems
- **Aerobic**: Uses oxygen, sustainable for long durations (walking, jogging)
- **Anaerobic Glycolytic**: 30 seconds to 2 minutes of intense work (sprints)
- **Phosphagen/ATP-CP**: Explosive efforts under 10 seconds (max sprints)

### Heart Rate Zones
| Zone | Intensity | % Max HR | Purpose |
|------|-----------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Very Light | 50-60% | Recovery, warm-up |
| 2 | Light | 60-70% | Fat burning, endurance base |
| 3 | Moderate | 70-80% | Aerobic capacity |
| 4 | Hard | 80-90% | Lactate threshold |
| 5 | Maximum | 90-100% | VO2 max, speed |

**Max HR Estimate**: 220 - age (or 208 - 0.7 Ã— age for better accuracy)

## RUNNING & WALKING MOVEMENTS

### CD001: Steady-State Jog
**Equipment**: None (track/treadmill optional)
**Difficulty**: Beginner
**Primary Systems**: Aerobic, cardiovascular
**Muscles**: Quadriceps, hamstrings, calves, hip flexors, glutes

**Form Cues**:
- Land midfoot, not heel
- Keep shoulders relaxed, arms at 90 degrees
- Lean slightly forward from ankles
- Breathe rhythmically (2:2 or 3:3 pattern)
- Short, quick strides rather than overstriding

**Common Mistakes**:
- Heel striking with extended leg (increases impact)
- Holding breath or shallow breathing
- Tensing shoulders up to ears
- Arms crossing body midline

**Contraindications**:
- Acute knee/ankle injuries
- Severe shin splints
- Stress fractures (active)
- Uncontrolled hypertension

**Programming**: 20-60 minutes, Zone 2-3, 2-4x/week

### CD002: High Knees
**Equipment**: None
**Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
**Primary Systems**: Anaerobic, neuromuscular
**Muscles**: Hip flexors, quadriceps, calves, core

**Form Cues**:
- Drive knees to hip height
- Stay on balls of feet
- Pump arms opposite to legs
- Keep torso upright
- Quick ground contact

**Common Mistakes**:
- Leaning back excessively
- Knees not reaching hip height
- Landing flat-footed
- Arms not coordinating with legs

**Contraindications**:
- Hip flexor strains
- Knee injuries
- Lower back pain

**Programming**: 20-30 seconds work, 3-5 sets, part of warm-up or HIIT

### CD003: Butt Kicks
**Equipment**: None
**Difficulty**: Beginner
**Primary Systems**: Aerobic, neuromuscular
**Muscles**: Hamstrings, calves, quadriceps (eccentric)

**Form Cues**:
- Kick heels toward glutes
- Stay on balls of feet
- Keep thighs relatively vertical
- Quick, light foot contacts
- Arms pump naturally

**Common Mistakes**:
- Leaning too far forward
- Kicking out behind instead of up
- Slow, heavy foot strikes
- Not reaching full heel-to-glute range

**Contraindications**:
- Hamstring strains
- Knee injuries (acute)

**Programming**: 20-30 seconds, 3-5 sets, warm-up or conditioning

### CD004: Sprint Intervals
**Equipment**: Track/field/treadmill
**Difficulty**: Advanced
**Primary Systems**: Anaerobic, phosphagen
**Muscles**: Quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, calves, hip flexors

**Form Cues**:
- Powerful arm drive (cheek to pocket)
- High knee lift
- Full hip extension at push-off
- Forward lean from ankles
- Stay relaxed despite intensity

**Common Mistakes**:
- Tensing face and shoulders
- Arms crossing midline
- Not achieving full hip extension
- Slowing down before finish line

**Contraindications**:
- Hamstring injuries (high risk exercise)
- Cardiac conditions without clearance
- Joint injuries
- Inadequate warm-up

**Programming**: 10-30 seconds max effort, 1:3-1:5 work:rest, 6-10 reps

### CD005: Hill Sprints
**Equipment**: Hill or incline treadmill
**Difficulty**: Advanced
**Primary Systems**: Anaerobic, muscular
**Muscles**: Glutes, hamstrings, quadriceps, calves

**Form Cues**:
- Shorter stride than flat sprinting
- Drive knees higher
- Pump arms aggressively
- Lean into hill from ankles
- Push through full foot

**Common Mistakes**:
- Looking down at feet
- Overstriding on incline
- Insufficient arm drive
- Giving up before top

**Contraindications**:
- Achilles issues (higher stress)
- Calf strains
- Cardiac conditions
- Poor sprint mechanics

**Programming**: 10-20 seconds uphill, walk down recovery, 6-12 reps

**Primary Systems**: Muscular endurance, core
**Muscles**: Shoulders, core, hip flexors, quadriceps

## HIGH-INTENSITY MOVEMENTS

### CD026: Burpees
**Equipment**: None
**Difficulty**: Advanced
**Primary Systems**: Anaerobic, full body
**Muscles**: Chest, triceps, hip flexors, quadriceps, shoulders, core

**Form Cues**:
- Squat down, hands to floor
- Jump feet back to plank
- Perform push-up (optional)
- Jump feet to hands
- Explode up with arms overhead

**Common Mistakes**:
- Worm-like push-up
- Not reaching full extension on jump
- Skipping the push-up when required
- Landing hard

**Contraindications**:
- Wrist injuries
- Shoulder problems
- Knee injuries
- Lower back pain
- Cardiac conditions (high intensity)

**Programming**: 10-15 reps, 3-4 sets, or timed intervals

## PROGRAMMING RECOMMENDATIONS

### For Fat Loss
- Mix Zone 2 steady state (60-70% HR) with HIIT
- 3-5 cardio sessions per week
- Include variety to prevent adaptation
- Example week:
  - Day 1: 30 min steady jog
  - Day 2: 20 min HIIT (burpees, mountain climbers)
  - Day 3: Rest
  - Day 4: 30 min row or bike
  - Day 5: Combat cardio 20 min
  - Day 6: Active recovery (water jogging)
  - Day 7: Rest

### For Conditioning (Athletic Performance)
- Focus on sport-specific movements
- Interval training 2-3x per week
- Maintain aerobic base
- Example:
  - Day 1: Hill sprints
  - Day 2: Steady state 30 min
  - Day 3: Agility work (lateral shuffle, carioca)
  - Day 4: Rest
  - Day 5: Battle ropes + sled work
  - Day 6: Easy jog or swim
  - Day 7: Rest

### For Heart Health
- Prioritize Zone 2 training
- 150+ minutes moderate intensity per week
- Low-impact options for joint preservation
- Example:
  - 5x 30 min sessions: walking, cycling, swimming, rowing, stair climber

### For Beginners
- Start with low-impact options
- Build to 20-30 minutes continuous
- Progress intensity gradually
- Weeks 1-4: Walking, marching, step touch
- Weeks 5-8: Add low-impact jacks, boxer shuffle
- Weeks 9+: Introduce higher intensity as tolerated

## COMMON MISTAKES IN CARDIO TRAINING

1. **Too much too soon** - Progress gradually
2. **Ignoring form** - Fatigue shouldn't compromise technique
3. **Same routine forever** - Vary modalities and intensities
4. **Neglecting recovery** - Rest days are essential
5. **All high-intensity** - Balance with Zone 2 work
6. **Skipping warm-up** - 5-10 minutes minimum
7. **Poor breathing** - Learn to breathe rhythmically
8. **Ignoring pain** - Differentiate discomfort from injury

## CONTRAINDICATIONS BY CONDITION

| Condition | Avoid | Consider Instead |
|-----------|-------|------------------|
| Knee injuries | Jumping, running, deep squats | Water exercises, cycling, upper body |
| Back pain | Burpees, tire flips, heavy rotation | Walking, swimming, recumbent bike |
| Shoulder injuries | Battle ropes, rowing, overhead movements | Lower body focus, water jogging |
| Cardiac conditions | Max effort sprints (without clearance) | Zone 2 work, water exercise |
| Pregnancy | High impact, supine after 1st trimester | Walking, swimming, prenatal classes |
