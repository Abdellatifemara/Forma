# Complete TRX/Suspension Training Database

## Overview

Suspension training uses bodyweight with adjustable straps to create instability, challenging strength, balance, and core stability simultaneously.

### Benefits of Suspension Training
- Full body engagement on every exercise
- Scalable difficulty (adjust body angle)
- Develops core stability
- Improves balance and proprioception
- Portable and versatile
- Joint-friendly

### Difficulty Adjustment
- **Easier**: Stand more upright, move feet away from anchor
- **Harder**: Lean more, move feet closer to anchor
- **Hardest**: Single leg variations

### Equipment
- TRX Suspension Trainer (most common)
- Gymnastic rings
- Other suspension systems (Jungle Gym, etc.)

## Upper Body - Push

### TRX001 - TRX Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Secondary**: Core, shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet in straps, hands on floor
  - Perform push-up with unstable legs
  - Core tight throughout
  - Control the movement
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips sagging → Engage core
  - Swinging → Control motion

### TRX002 - TRX Chest Press
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away from anchor, hold handles
  - Lean forward, arms extended
  - Lower chest toward hands
  - Press back up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Body breaking at hips → Stay rigid
  - Elbows flaring → Keep 45-degree angle

### TRX003 - TRX Chest Fly
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest
- **Secondary**: Core, shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away, arms out to sides
  - Open arms wide (eccentric)
  - Bring hands together (concentric)
  - Slight elbow bend throughout
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending elbows too much → Maintain angle

### TRX004 - TRX Atomic Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Chest, core
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Push-up position, feet in straps
  - Push-up, then tuck knees to chest
  - Extend legs, repeat
  - Combines push-up and tuck
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips rising on tuck → Keep controlled

### TRX005 - TRX Pike Push-Up
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, chest
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet in straps, pike hips up
  - Lower head toward floor (push-up)
  - Press back to pike
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Sagging in pike → Keep hips high

## Upper Body - Pull

### TRX006 - TRX Row (Basic)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Lats, upper back
- **Secondary**: Biceps, core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, lean back, arms extended
  - Pull chest to handles
  - Squeeze shoulder blades
  - Control descent
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Body breaking → Stay rigid as plank

### TRX007 - TRX Row (Feet Elevated)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats, upper back
- **Secondary**: Biceps, core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same as basic row but feet on box
  - More horizontal body = harder
  - Full range of motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Partial reps → Full extension to full contraction

### TRX008 - TRX Single Arm Row
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats, core (anti-rotation)
- **Secondary**: Biceps
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold one handle, other arm extended
  - Pull with one arm
  - Resist rotation with core
  - Switch sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rotating → Fight to stay square

### TRX009 - TRX Face Pull
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Rear delts, upper back
- **Secondary**: External rotators
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, lean back
  - Pull handles to face
  - Elbows high, external rotate at end
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows dropping → Keep high

### TRX010 - TRX Y-Fly
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Upper back, rear delts
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lean back, pull arms up into Y
  - Straight arms, thumbs up
  - Squeeze upper back
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending arms → Keep straight

### TRX011 - TRX T-Fly
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Rear delts, rhomboids
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Pull arms out to T position
  - Squeeze shoulder blades together
  - Control return
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Shrugging → Shoulders down

### TRX012 - TRX W-Fly
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Rotator cuff, upper back
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Pull into W position (elbows bent)
  - External rotation at top
  - Squeeze between shoulder blades
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - No external rotation → Full W shape

### TRX013 - TRX Bicep Curl
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Biceps
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, lean back, palms up
  - Curl handles to shoulders
  - Keep elbows high and fixed
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows dropping → Keep high

## Upper Body - Shoulders

### TRX014 - TRX Deltoid Fly
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Side delts
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, lean back
  - Arms out to sides (T shape)
  - Raise body by pulling arms together overhead
  - Control descent
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Using momentum → Controlled movement

### TRX015 - TRX I-Raise
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders
- **Secondary**: Core, upper back
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lean back, arms overhead in I position
  - Raise body by pulling down
  - Straight arms throughout
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending elbows → Keep straight

### TRX016 - TRX Pike
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank position, feet in straps
  - Pike hips up toward ceiling
  - Keep legs straight
  - Control return to plank
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending knees → Keep straight

## Upper Body - Triceps

### TRX017 - TRX Tricep Press
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Triceps
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away from anchor
  - Start with bent elbows, hands by forehead
  - Extend arms forward (like skull crusher)
  - Control return
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Moving elbows → Keep fixed position

### TRX018 - TRX Tricep Extension
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Triceps
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away, arms extended overhead
  - Lower body by bending elbows
  - Extend to return
  - Body stays rigid
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Breaking at hips → Plank position

## Lower Body

### TRX019 - TRX Squat
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, hold handles
  - Sit back into squat
  - Use straps for balance assistance
  - Full depth
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pulling on straps → Light assistance only

### TRX020 - TRX Single Leg Squat (Pistol)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold straps, one leg extended
  - Squat on single leg
  - Straps assist balance
  - Progress to less assistance
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Too much pulling → Minimize assistance

### TRX021 - TRX Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, hold straps
  - Step back into reverse lunge
  - Drive through front heel
  - Use straps for balance
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning forward → Stay upright

### TRX022 - TRX Bulgarian Split Squat
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - Back foot in strap
  - Front foot forward
  - Lunge down, keeping torso upright
  - Drive through front heel
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning forward → Stay tall

### TRX023 - TRX Curtsy Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, quads
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold straps, back leg crosses behind
  - Lunge down in curtsy pattern
  - Return and repeat
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knee caving → Track straight

### TRX024 - TRX Hamstring Curl
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Glutes, core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, heels in straps
  - Lift hips (bridge)
  - Curl heels toward glutes
  - Extend and repeat
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips dropping → Keep lifted

### TRX025 - TRX Single Leg Hamstring Curl
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Core, balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - One foot in strap
  - Bridge up, curl heel in
  - One leg works at a time
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rotating → Stay level

### TRX026 - TRX Hip Press
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Glutes
- **Secondary**: Hamstrings
- **Form Cues**:
  - Heels in straps, lying on back
  - Press hips up into bridge
  - Squeeze glutes at top
  - Lower controlled
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Not full extension → Full hip extension

### TRX027 - TRX Calf Raise
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Calves
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, light strap hold
  - Rise onto toes
  - Full range of motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Partial reps → Full plantarflexion

### TRX028 - TRX Side Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Adductors, quads
- **Secondary**: Glutes
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, hold straps
  - Step to side, shift weight
  - Squat on one side
  - Return and alternate
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Not going deep → Full depth

## Core

### TRX029 - TRX Plank
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet in straps, hands on floor
  - Hold plank position
  - Body straight from head to heels
  - Don't let hips sag or pike
- **Target**: 30-60 seconds

### TRX030 - TRX Body Saw
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank with feet in straps
  - Rock body forward and back
  - Maintain rigid plank throughout
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips moving → Stay rigid

### TRX031 - TRX Mountain Climber
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank position, feet in straps
  - Drive knees to chest alternating
  - Keep hips level
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips bouncing → Controlled movement

### TRX032 - TRX Knee Tuck
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core (especially lower abs)
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank, feet in straps
  - Tuck both knees to chest
  - Extend back to plank
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips rising too high → Controlled tuck

### TRX033 - TRX Pike
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank with feet in straps
  - Pike hips to ceiling, legs straight
  - Return to plank
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending knees → Straight legs

### TRX034 - TRX Oblique Tuck
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank position
  - Tuck knees toward one elbow
  - Return, then other side
  - Targets obliques
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rotating torso → Knees go to side

### TRX035 - TRX Side Plank
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Side plank, feet in straps
  - Hold position, hips high
  - Switch sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips dropping → Keep lifted

### TRX036 - TRX Crunch
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Abs
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, heels in straps
  - Crunch up, reaching for feet
  - Control descent
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Using momentum → Slow, controlled

### TRX037 - TRX Pendulum
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques, core
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank, feet in straps
  - Swing legs side to side
  - Keep upper body still
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Upper body moving → Isolate legs

### TRX038 - TRX Fallout
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core (anti-extension)
- **Secondary**: Lats, shoulders
- **Form Cues**:
  - Kneeling or standing, hold handles
  - Arms extended, lean forward
  - Roll out into extended position
  - Pull back using core
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Lower back arching → Keep neutral

## Full Body/Compound

### TRX039 - TRX Burpee
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Conditioning
- **Form Cues**:
  - Feet in straps
  - Jump/step to squat
  - Jump/step to plank
  - Push-up
  - Jump feet back to squat
  - Stand
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Sloppy form → Each phase controlled

### TRX040 - TRX Clock Press
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Chest, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Chest press position
  - Press while rotating (like clock hands)
  - Return to center, rotate other way
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Losing core → Stay rigid

### TRX041 - TRX Sprinter Start
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Explosive power
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away, leaning forward
  - Drive one knee up explosively
  - Like sprinter starting
  - Alternate legs
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Not explosive → Maximum drive

### TRX042 - TRX Crossing Balance Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, balance
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold straps, perform crossing lunge
  - Back leg crosses behind front
  - Balance challenge
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Losing balance → Light strap assistance

### TRX043 - TRX Power Pull
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Back, core rotation
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Form Cues**:
  - Single arm row setup
  - Rotate and reach with free arm
  - Pull and rotate back
  - Explosive movement
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - No rotation → Full torso rotation

## Stretching/Mobility

### TRX044 - TRX Chest Stretch
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face away, hold straps
  - Step forward, let arms go back
  - Lean into stretch
- **Purpose**: Chest and shoulder opening

### TRX045 - TRX Lat Stretch
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Form Cues**:
  - Face anchor, hold straps
  - Sit back, letting arms stretch
  - Push hips back for more stretch
- **Purpose**: Lat and back stretch

### TRX046 - TRX Hip Flexor Stretch
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Form Cues**:
  - Back foot in strap
  - Lunge position
  - Sink hips for hip flexor stretch
- **Purpose**: Hip flexor opening

### TRX047 - TRX Hamstring Stretch
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Form Cues**:
  - Heel in strap, leg extended
  - Pull strap to stretch hamstring
- **Purpose**: Hamstring flexibility

### TRX048 - TRX Torso Rotation Stretch

  - Face anchor, hold straps

## Advanced Skills

### TRX049 - TRX Muscle-Up
- **Difficulty**: Elite
- **Primary**: Full upper body
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start in row position
  - Explosive pull to transition
  - Press to full dip position
  - Lower with control
- **Prerequisite**: TRX Rows 3x15, TRX Dips strong

### TRX050 - TRX L-Sit
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Core, hip flexors
- **Secondary**: Triceps
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold handles, lift body
  - Extend legs to L position
  - Hold for time
- **Prerequisites**: Strong core, hip flexor strength

### TRX051 - TRX Front Lever (Tuck)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hang from straps
  - Pull to horizontal, knees tucked
  - Hold position
- **Prerequisites**: Strong pulling base

### TRX052 - TRX Skin the Cat
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hang, tuck and rotate backward
  - Pass through to German hang
  - Reverse back
- **Prerequisites**: Strong shoulders, mobility
