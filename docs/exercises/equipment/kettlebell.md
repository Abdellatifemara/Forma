# Complete Kettlebell Exercise Database

## Overview

Kettlebells offer unique training benefits due to their offset center of gravity. They're especially effective for building grip strength, conditioning, hip power, and full-body coordination.

### Benefits of Kettlebell Training
- Develops hip power and explosiveness
- Builds grip strength
- Improves cardiovascular conditioning
- Enhances full-body coordination
- Time-efficient workouts
- Functional movement patterns

### Kettlebell Sizes (Recommendations)

**Men**:
- Beginner: 12-16 kg
- Intermediate: 20-24 kg
- Advanced: 28-32 kg

**Women**:
- Beginner: 8-12 kg
- Intermediate: 14-16 kg
- Advanced: 20-24 kg

### Safety Notes
- Always check handle condition before use
- Clear space around you for swings
- Learn proper hip hinge before ballistic movements
- Start lighter than you think necessary

## Foundational Kettlebell Exercises

### KB001 - Kettlebell Deadlift
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Glutes, hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Lower back, core
- **Contraindications**: Lower back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Stand over kettlebell, feet shoulder-width
  - Hinge at hips, grip handle with both hands
  - Flat back, chest up, shoulder blades engaged
  - Drive through heels to stand
  - Squeeze glutes at top
  - Control descent
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Keep chest up, flat back
  - Using arms to lift → Drive through legs/hips
  - Not hinging → Push hips back first

### KB002 - Kettlebell Goblet Squat
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Core, upper back
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest, elbows under bell
  - Feet slightly wider than shoulder-width
  - Squat deep, elbows inside knees
  - Keep torso upright
  - Drive through heels to stand
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows flaring out → Keep tight to body
  - Forward lean → Stay upright
  - Heels lifting → Shift weight back

### KB003 - Kettlebell Swing (Two-Hand)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, hamstrings
- **Secondary**: Core, shoulders, grip
- **Contraindications**: Lower back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start with KB slightly in front, hinge to grab
  - Hike KB back between legs (like football snap)
  - Explosively drive hips forward
  - Arms stay straight, KB floats to shoulder height
  - Let KB fall, hinge as it passes hips
  - Continuous fluid motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Squatting instead of hinging → Push hips BACK
  - Using arms to lift → Power from hips only
  - Hyperextending back at top → Stop at vertical

### KB004 - Kettlebell Turkish Get-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate-Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core, full body
- **Secondary**: Stability, coordination
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, KB pressed up in one hand
  - Roll to elbow, then to hand
  - Lift hips into bridge
  - Sweep leg through to kneeling
  - Stand up while keeping KB overhead
  - Reverse all steps to return
  - KB arm stays vertical throughout
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rushing → Each step distinct
  - Losing vertical arm → KB stays above shoulder
  - Skipping steps → Follow all positions

### KB005 - Kettlebell Clean
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hips, upper back
- **Secondary**: Biceps, grip
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start like swing, hike KB back
  - As KB comes up, pull elbow back
  - Rotate arm, KB lands softly in rack position
  - KB rests on forearm, wrist straight
  - Not a curl or reverse curl motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Banging wrist → "Tame the arc" by keeping close
  - Curling the weight → It's a pull-under motion
  - Grip too tight on clean → Loosen to rotate

### KB006 - Kettlebell Press (Military Press)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, triceps
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement
- **Form Cues**:
  - Clean KB to rack position
  - Elbow tucked, wrist straight
  - Press straight up, slight lean away
  - Lockout overhead
  - Control descent back to rack
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pressing forward → Straight up path
  - Elbow flaring → Keep tucked initially
  - Wrist bending → Stay neutral

### KB007 - Kettlebell Snatch
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Shoulders, grip, conditioning
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start like swing, one arm
  - At top of swing, pull KB overhead
  - Punch hand through to catch KB overhead
  - Soft catch, KB rests on back of forearm
  - Let KB drop, absorbing at hips
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Banging forearm → Punch through, don't flip
  - Two motions → One fluid movement
  - Using arm → Hip power, arm guides

## Swing Variations

### KB008 - Single Arm Swing
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, hamstrings, obliques
- **Secondary**: Grip, anti-rotation
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same as two-hand swing, one arm
  - Free arm mirrors movement
  - Resist rotation of torso
  - Creates anti-rotation challenge
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rotating torso → Stay square
  - Free arm dead → Keep active

### KB009 - Hand-to-Hand Swing
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, hamstrings, grip
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: Grip issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start with single arm swing
  - At top, switch hands
  - Catch with other hand, continue swing
  - Smooth transitions
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rushing switch → Switch at float point
  - Losing control → Master single arm first

### KB010 - Dead Stop Swing
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hip power
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Complete one full swing
  - Set KB down between legs
  - Reset position
  - Repeat from dead stop each rep
  - Focus on explosive start
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Not fully resetting → Complete stop each rep
  - Rounding on pickup → Maintain flat back

### KB011 - Swing to Squat
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Glutes, quads
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Perform swing
  - Catch KB at top in goblet position
  - Immediately squat
  - Stand and swing KB back down
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Sloppy catch → Clean catch at chest
  - Not deep squat → Full depth

### KB012 - High Pull (from Swing)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Upper back, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Hips
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start like swing
  - At top, pull elbow high
  - KB comes to chin level, elbow above hand
  - Release back to swing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pulling too early → Wait for hip pop
  - Elbow below hand → High elbow position

### KB013 - Swing to Clean
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Conditioning
- **Form Cues**:
  - Alternate between swing and clean
  - Swing, then clean, then swing
  - Trains both patterns
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Confusion on timing → Clear distinction

### KB014 - American Swing (Overhead)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Same as two-hand swing
  - KB goes all the way overhead
  - More shoulder involvement
  - More range of motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hyperextending back → Keep core tight
  - Losing control overhead → Start lighter
- **Note**: Traditional Russian swing (shoulder height) preferred by many coaches

## Squat Variations

### KB015 - Sumo Squat
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, adductors
- **Secondary**: Glutes
- **Contraindications**: Hip pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Wide stance, toes out
  - KB hangs between legs
  - Squat straight down
  - Keep chest up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knees caving → Push out over toes
  - Forward lean → Stay upright

### KB016 - Front Squat (Single KB)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, core
- **Secondary**: Upper back
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Clean KB to rack position
  - Squat with KB at chest
  - Elbow stays up
  - Creates asymmetric load
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbow dropping → Keep high
  - Leaning to side → Stay centered

### KB017 - Double Front Squat
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, core
- **Secondary**: Upper back
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs in rack position
  - Squat maintaining upright torso
  - Elbows stay high
  - Significantly challenging
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows dropping → Fight to keep up
  - Rounding back → Stay tall

### KB018 - Pistol Squat (KB Counterbalance)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Single leg squat, other leg forward
  - KB acts as counterbalance
  - Control descent and ascent
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Falling backward → KB helps balance forward
  - Not going deep → Full range

### KB019 - Cossack Squat with KB
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Adductors, quads
- **Secondary**: Hip mobility
- **Contraindications**: Groin injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Wide stance, shift to one side
  - Squat on one leg, other leg straight
  - KB provides counterbalance
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Straight leg foot flat → Heel only, toes up
  - Not going deep → Full depth on bent leg

## Press Variations

### KB020 - Push Press
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, legs
- **Secondary**: Triceps
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in rack position
  - Quick dip with legs
  - Drive up, using momentum to press
  - Lockout overhead
  - More weight than strict press
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Dip too deep → Shallow, quick dip
  - Not using legs → Legs initiate movement

### KB021 - Jerk
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in rack, first dip like push press
  - Second dip under the weight
  - Catch KB overhead with bent knees
  - Stand to finish
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - No second dip → Must drive under weight
  - Pressing instead of jerking → Fast motion

### KB022 - Floor Press
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Chest, triceps
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, KB in one hand
  - Upper arm rests on floor
  - Press KB straight up
  - Lower until elbow touches
  - Limited range = tricep focus
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bouncing elbow → Controlled touch
  - Flaring elbow → Keep 45-degree angle

### KB023 - See-Saw Press (Alternating)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Anti-rotation
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs in rack position
  - Press one while lowering other
  - Create see-saw motion
  - Core works to stabilize
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Both stopping in middle → Continuous motion
  - Leaning side to side → Stay stable

### KB024 - Bottoms-Up Press
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, grip, stability
- **Secondary**: Rotator cuff
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB upside down (bottom up)
  - Press while keeping KB balanced
  - Requires extreme grip and stability
  - Start very light
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - KB too heavy → Start light
  - Losing balance → Focus on stability

### KB025 - Half-Kneeling Press
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Hip stability
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Half-kneeling, KB on same side as back knee
  - Press overhead
  - Core must stabilize asymmetric position
  - Great for shoulder stability
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning away → Stay vertical
  - Hip shifting → Keep stable

## Pulling Movements

### KB026 - Bent Over Row (Single)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Lats, upper back
- **Secondary**: Biceps, core
- **Contraindications**: Lower back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Staggered stance, hinge forward
  - Row KB to hip
  - Keep elbow close to body
  - Squeeze shoulder blade at top
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Standing too upright → Hinge more
  - Elbow flaring → Keep close

### KB027 - Renegade Row
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Lats, core
- **Secondary**: Anti-rotation
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Push-up position on two KBs
  - Row one KB while stabilizing on other
  - Keep hips completely still
  - Alternate sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips rotating → Fight to stay level
  - Wide feet → Narrow for more challenge

### KB028 - Upright Row
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulders, traps
- **Secondary**: Biceps
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB with both hands
  - Pull up toward chin
  - Elbows lead the movement
  - Don't go above shoulder height
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pulling too high → Stop at shoulders
  - No elbow lead → Elbows above hands

### KB029 - Gorilla Row
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Lats, upper back
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs on floor between feet
  - Hinge over, alternating rows
  - Quick switches between sides
  - Stay hinged throughout
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Standing up → Maintain hinge
  - Twisting → Stay square

### KB030 - Face Pull (with KB)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Rear delts, upper back
- **Secondary**: Rotator cuff
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB by horns
  - Pull toward face
  - Elbows high and wide
  - Squeeze shoulder blades
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbows dropping → Keep high
  - Not pulling far enough → Full contraction

## Lunge and Single Leg Movements

### KB031 - Goblet Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Step forward into lunge
  - Back knee toward floor
  - Push off front foot to return
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knee over toe → Keep 90-degree angle
  - Torso leaning → Stay upright

### KB032 - Reverse Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Contraindications**: Knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB at chest or by side
  - Step backward into lunge
  - Easier on knees than forward lunge
  - Drive through front heel to stand
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Short step → Full step back
  - Leaning forward → Stay upright

### KB033 - Rack Position Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, core
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB cleaned to rack position
  - Perform lunges
  - Asymmetric load challenges core
  - Switch sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning toward KB → Fight to stay straight
  - Elbow dropping → Keep high

### KB034 - Overhead Lunge
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Shoulder stability, core
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB pressed overhead
  - Perform lunges while maintaining arm
  - Extreme core demand
  - Arm must stay vertical
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Arm drifting → Keep locked vertical
  - Rushing → Controlled movement

### KB035 - Lateral Lunge with KB
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Adductors, glutes
- **Secondary**: Quads
- **Contraindications**: Groin injury
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Step to side, hinge and squat on that leg
  - Other leg stays straight
  - Push back to center
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Knee caving → Track over toe
  - Not going deep → Full depth

### KB036 - Step-Up with KB
- **Difficulty**: Beginner-Intermediate
- **Primary**: Quads, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance
- **Contraindications**: Knee pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB at chest or sides
  - Step onto box/bench
  - Drive through top leg
  - Control step down
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pushing off bottom leg → Top leg does work
  - Box too high → Start moderate height

### KB037 - Single Leg Deadlift with KB
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hamstrings, glutes
- **Secondary**: Balance, core
- **Contraindications**: Balance issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in one or both hands
  - Hinge on one leg, other leg goes back
  - Flat back throughout
  - Return to standing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rounding back → Keep flat
  - Rotating hips → Stay square

## Core Exercises

### KB038 - Windmill
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques, hips
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB pressed overhead
  - Turn feet 45 degrees away from KB arm
  - Hinge toward front foot, reaching down
  - Keep KB arm vertical throughout
  - Return to standing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bending KB arm → Keep locked straight
  - Not turning feet → Must angle away
  - Rounding back → Maintain flat back

### KB039 - Russian Twist
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Obliques
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: Back pain
- **Form Cues**:
  - Sit, lean back slightly, feet can be up or down
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Rotate side to side, tapping KB on floor
  - Keep chest up
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Only moving arms → Rotate full torso
  - Rounding back → Stay lifted

### KB040 - Turkish Sit-Up
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - First portion of Turkish Get-Up
  - Lie down with KB overhead
  - Roll to elbow, then to hand
  - Reverse back down
  - Focuses on sit-up portion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Using momentum → Control throughout
  - KB arm bending → Stay straight

### KB041 - Figure 8
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core, grip
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Squat stance, pass KB between legs in figure 8
  - Hand to hand exchanges
  - Keep chest up
  - Develops coordination
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Standing too upright → Stay in squat
  - Dropping KB → Controlled passes

### KB042 - Around the World
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Core, grip
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Pass KB around body at waist level
  - Hand to hand behind back
  - Both directions
  - Keep body still
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Swaying with KB → Body stays stable
  - Going too fast → Controlled movement

### KB043 - Halo
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Upper back
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder impingement
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB upside down at chest
  - Circle KB around head
  - Keep tight to head
  - Both directions
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Wide circles → Stay close to head
  - Moving head → Head stays still

### KB044 - Drag (Plank KB Pull)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core, lats
- **Secondary**: Anti-rotation
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Plank position, KB beside you
  - Reach under body, drag KB to other side
  - Keep hips completely still
  - Alternate hands
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Hips rotating → Fight rotation
  - Lifting hips → Stay level

### KB045 - Dead Bug with KB
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core
- **Secondary**: Hip flexors
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on back, hold KB over chest
  - Standard dead bug movement
  - KB adds load to maintain position
  - Press back into floor
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Back arching → Keep pressed down
  - KB drifting → Keep stable

## Conditioning/Complex Exercises

### KB046 - KB Thruster
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Conditioning
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder/knee issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in rack position (or two KBs)
  - Squat deep
  - Stand explosively and press overhead
  - One fluid motion
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Two separate movements → One continuous motion
  - Shallow squat → Full depth

### KB047 - Man Maker (KB version)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Conditioning
- **Contraindications**: Various
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start standing with two KBs
  - Squat down, KBs on floor
  - Kick out to push-up
  - Row each KB
  - Jump feet in
  - Clean and press both
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Skipping steps → Complete all movements
  - Losing form → Quality over speed

### KB048 - KB Swing to Squat to Press
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Conditioning
- **Form Cues**:
  - Swing KB up
  - Catch in goblet position
  - Squat
  - Stand and press overhead
  - Return to swing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Sloppy transitions → Clean each movement
  - Rushing → Controlled pace

### KB049 - Long Cycle (Clean and Jerk)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Competition lift
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Clean KB to rack
  - Jerk overhead
  - Drop to rack
  - Drop to swing
  - Repeat continuously
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pressing instead of jerking → Quick dip and drive
  - Losing rhythm → Find sustainable pace

### KB050 - Snatch Test
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body, conditioning
- **Secondary**: Competition test
- **Protocol**:
  - 100 snatches in 5 minutes
  - Switch hands as needed
  - Standard kettlebell certification test
- **Notes**: Requires excellent technique and conditioning

## Carry Variations

### KB051 - Farmer's Walk (Two KB)
- **Difficulty**: Beginner
- **Primary**: Grip, core
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Contraindications**: Grip injuries
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in each hand at sides
  - Walk with upright posture
  - Don't lean or sway
  - Cover set distance
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning forward → Stay upright
  - Short steps → Normal walking stride

### KB052 - Suitcase Carry
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Obliques, grip
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB in one hand only
  - Walk without leaning
  - Core works overtime to prevent side bend
  - Switch sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning away → Stay perfectly vertical
  - Rushing → Controlled walk

### KB053 - Rack Carry
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core, shoulders
- **Secondary**: Grip
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - One or two KBs in rack position
  - Walk maintaining rack
  - Elbow stays tucked
  - Challenges core and breathing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Elbow dropping → Keep high
  - Leaning back → Stay upright

### KB054 - Overhead Carry
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Shoulders, core
- **Secondary**: Full body stability
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - KB pressed overhead
  - Walk while maintaining lockout
  - Keep ribs down, don't flare
  - Extremely challenging
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Rib flare → Keep core tight
  - Arm drifting → Stay vertical

### KB055 - Mixed Carry (Cross Body)
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Full body, core
- **Secondary**: Anti-rotation/anti-lateral flexion
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - One KB overhead, other at side
  - Walk maintaining both positions
  - Maximum core demand
  - Switch sides
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Leaning to any side → Perfect vertical posture
  - Losing positions → Control both KBs

## Explosive/Ballistic Variations

### KB060 - Double Swing
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hips, glutes
- **Secondary**: Grip, core
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs, same swing mechanics
  - Significantly more demanding
  - Keep KBs from hitting together
  - Power from hips
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - KBs colliding → Wider grip
  - Losing hip drive → Focus on pop

### KB061 - Double Clean
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Full body
- **Secondary**: Grip
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs, swing and clean simultaneously
  - Land in rack position
  - Both KBs at same time
  - Challenging coordination
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - KBs banging → Pull in tight
  - Asymmetric catch → Equal timing

### KB062 - Swing to High Pull
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Upper back, hips
- **Secondary**: Shoulders
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Start with regular swing
  - At top, pull to chin level
  - Elbow leads, goes high
  - Return to swing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Pulling too early → Wait for hip pop
  - Elbow below hand → High elbow

### KB063 - Alternating Swing
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hips, coordination
- **Secondary**: Grip
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Two KBs, swing one at a time
  - As one descends, other swings up
  - Continuous alternating motion
  - Requires coordination
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Losing rhythm → Find timing
  - Both swinging together → Alternate

## Specialty Exercises

### KB064 - Bottom-Up Clean
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Grip, stability
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Clean KB to bottom-up position
  - KB balanced upside down
  - Hold briefly
  - Return to swing
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Trying to catch unstable → Smooth transition
  - Using too heavy KB → Start light

### KB065 - Deck Squat with KB
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Hip mobility, explosiveness
- **Secondary**: Core
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB at chest
  - Roll backward onto back
  - Roll forward explosively into squat, then stand
  - KB provides momentum assistance
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Not using momentum → Roll forward explosively
  - Landing off balance → Controlled roll

### KB066 - Lateral Swing
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Hips, obliques
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: Back issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Swing KB side to side
  - Hips rotate to power movement
  - Different plane of motion
  - Controls rotational power
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Only arms moving → Hip rotation drives it
  - Too aggressive → Control the movement

### KB067 - Figure 8 to Hold
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Core, grip
- **Secondary**: Coordination
- **Contraindications**: None
- **Form Cues**:
  - Figure 8 pattern
  - On signal or count, hold KB in front
  - Hold position before continuing
  - Adds isometric challenge
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Sloppy hold → Clean, stable hold
  - Losing squat position → Stay low

### KB068 - Swing to Catch
- **Difficulty**: Advanced
- **Primary**: Coordination, explosiveness
- **Secondary**: Full body
- **Contraindications**: Grip issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Swing KB, release at top
  - Catch handle on descent
  - Immediately continue swing
  - Advanced coordination drill
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Bad timing → Practice release point
  - KB flying away → Control the release

### KB069 - Bottoms-Up Hold
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Grip, stability
- **Secondary**: Shoulder stabilizers
- **Contraindications**: Wrist issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Hold KB upside down
  - Maintain balance for time
  - Start at chest, progress overhead
  - Maximum grip and stability challenge
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - Too heavy → Start very light
  - Losing balance → Focus and squeeze

### KB070 - Arm Bar
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Primary**: Shoulder stability
- **Secondary**: Core, thoracic
- **Contraindications**: Shoulder issues
- **Form Cues**:
  - Lie on side, KB pressed up
  - Roll onto back, keeping KB vertical
  - Let opposite leg swing over
  - Opens thoracic while stabilizing shoulder
- **Common Mistakes**:
  - KB drifting → Keep vertical
  - Rushing → Slow controlled movement
