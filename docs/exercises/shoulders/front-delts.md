# Front Deltoid Exercises (60+ Movements)

## Anatomy

### Anterior Deltoid
The front portion of the three-headed deltoid muscle.

**Attachments:**
- **Origin**: Lateral third of clavicle
- **Insertion**: Deltoid tuberosity of humerus

**Functions:**
- **Shoulder flexion**: Raising arm forward
- **Horizontal adduction**: Moving arm across body
- **Internal rotation**: Rotating arm inward

### Important Note
Front delts receive significant indirect stimulation from ALL pressing movements:
- Bench press (all angles)
- Push-ups
- Dips
- Any overhead press

**Many lifters overdevelop front delts relative to side and rear delts, leading to postural issues and shoulder problems.**

## OVERHEAD PRESS VARIATIONS (16)

### FD001 - Standing Barbell Overhead Press
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Barbell |
| **Difficulty** | Intermediate |
| **Primary** | Anterior deltoid |
| **Secondary** | Triceps, upper chest, core |
| **Contraindications** | Shoulder impingement, lower back issues |

**Form Cues:**
- Feet shoulder-width, slight knee bend
- Grip just outside shoulders
- Bar starts at collar bone level
- Brace core, squeeze glutes
- Press straight up, move head back then forward
- Lock out overhead, biceps by ears

**Common Mistakes:**
- Excessive lower back arch → Brace core harder
- Pressing in front of face → Move head back, bar goes straight up
- Flaring elbows excessively → 45° elbow angle

## Front Delt Training Philosophy

### The Overtraining Problem
Most people OVERTRAIN their front delts:
- Every bench press hits front delts
- Every incline press hits front delts
- Every push-up hits front delts
- Every dip hits front delts

This creates imbalance → rounded shoulders → injury risk

### Recommendations
1. **Prioritize side and rear delts** over front
2. **Limit direct front delt work** to 2-4 sets/week max
3. **Count pressing sets** as front delt work
4. **Focus on overhead pressing** rather than front raises

## Key Training Principles

1. **Press first, isolate second**: Heavy pressing > isolation
2. **Don't overtrain**: Front delts get enough from pressing
3. **Pain = stop**: Any shoulder pain during pressing needs attention
4. **Balance pushing and pulling**: Match with rear delt work

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Too much front delt work | Imbalance, injury | Reduce direct work |
| Excessive arching on OHP | Lower back strain | Brace core |
| Front raises above shoulder | Impingement | Stop at shoulder level |
| Ignoring mobility | Limited ROM | Daily shoulder stretches |

## Programming Recommendations
| Goal | Direct Sets/Week | Rep Range | Rest |
|------|------------------|-----------|------|
| Strength | 6-10 (pressing) | 4-6 | 3 min |
| Hypertrophy | 3-6 (includes pressing) | 8-12 | 60-90s |
| Maintenance | 0-2 | 12-15 | 60s |

**Remember:** You likely get 15-20+ indirect sets per week from all pressing movements.
