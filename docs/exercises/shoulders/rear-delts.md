# Rear Deltoid Exercises (70+ Movements)

## Anatomy

### Posterior Deltoid
The rear portion of the three-headed deltoid muscle.

**Attachments:**
- **Origin**: Spine of scapula
- **Insertion**: Deltoid tuberosity of humerus

**Functions:**
- **Shoulder extension**: Pulling arm backward
- **Shoulder horizontal abduction**: Pulling arm out to the side (rear fly motion)
- **External rotation**: Rotating arm outward

### Why Rear Delts Are Critical
1. **Posture**: Counter rounded shoulders from desk work
2. **Shoulder health**: Balance pushing muscles
3. **Injury prevention**: Strong rear delts protect the shoulder joint
4. **Aesthetics**: Complete the 3D shoulder look
5. **Performance**: Essential for pulling movements

**Most people severely undertrain their rear delts!**

## REAR DELT FLY VARIATIONS (20)

### RD001 - Bent-Over Dumbbell Reverse Fly
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | Dumbbells |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Rear deltoid |
| **Secondary** | Rhomboids, mid traps |
| **Contraindications** | Lower back issues (seated version better) |

**Form Cues:**
- Hinge at hips, torso nearly parallel to floor
- Arms hang down, slight elbow bend
- Raise arms to sides, squeeze shoulder blades
- Lead with elbows, not hands
- Control the descent

**Common Mistakes:**
- Using too much weight → Lighter, feel the squeeze
- Not bending over enough → Torso parallel to floor
- Swinging → Controlled movement
- Arms going too far back → Stop at shoulder level

## FACE PULL VARIATIONS (14)

### RD021 - Face Pull - Rope
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Equipment** | High Cable + Rope |
| **Difficulty** | Beginner |
| **Primary** | Rear deltoid, rhomboids |
| **Secondary** | Mid/lower traps, rotator cuff |
| **Contraindications** | None |

**Form Cues:**
- Set cable at face height or slightly above
- Grip rope with thumbs toward you
- Pull toward face, elbows high and out
- Externally rotate at end (thumbs point back)
- Squeeze shoulder blades, pause
- Control return

**Why Face Pulls Are Essential:**
- Train rear delts AND external rotation
- Counter internal rotation from daily life
- Improve posture
- Protect shoulder health
- Jeff Cavaliere says do them every workout!

## The Rear Delt Priority Principle

### Why Prioritize Rear Delts?
1. **Most undertrained** delt head
2. **Get no indirect work** from pressing
3. **Critical for posture** (everyone sits too much)
4. **Shoulder health** depends on rear delt strength
5. **Creates balanced physique**

### How Much Volume?
| Training Level | Sets/Week |
|----------------|-----------|
| Beginner | 8-12 |
| Intermediate | 12-18 |
| Advanced | 15-25 |
| Posture correction | 20-30+ |

## Daily Rear Delt Protocol

Do this every day for shoulder health:
1. **Band Pull-Aparts**: 3x20
2. **Face Pulls**: 2x15
3. **Wall Slides**: 2x10

**Total time:** 5 minutes
**Benefits:** Better posture, healthier shoulders, more gains

## Key Training Principles

1. **Volume is key**: Rear delts can handle lots of work
2. **Daily work is fine**: Band pull-aparts and face pulls daily
3. **Control over weight**: Light weight, squeeze hard
4. **External rotation**: Include it for shoulder health
5. **Multiple angles**: Y, T, W, face pulls all hit differently

## Common Mistakes
| Mistake | Problem | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Too heavy | Traps/rhomboids take over | Lighter, feel rear delt |
| Not enough volume | Rear delts stay weak | 15-25 sets/week |
| No external rotation | Incomplete rear delt work | Include face pulls |
| Skipping rear delts | Posture problems, injury | Every workout |
| Momentum/swinging | Reduces rear delt tension | Slow, controlled |

## Programming Recommendations
| Goal | Sets/Week | Rep Range | Rest |
|------|-----------|-----------|------|
| Hypertrophy | 15-25 | 12-20 | 45-60s |
| Shoulder health | 10-15 | 15-25 | 30-60s |
| Posture correction | 20-30 | 15-30 | 30-45s |

**Remember:** You can train rear delts every day if needed for posture correction!
