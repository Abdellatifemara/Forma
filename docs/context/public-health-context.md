## WHO Physical Activity Guidelines Summary

### Adults (18-64 years) - Key Points for Messaging

**Minimum Recommendations:**
- At least 150-300 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity per week
- OR 75-150 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic activity per week
- OR equivalent combination

**Strength Training:**
- Muscle-strengthening activities at moderate or greater intensity
- All major muscle groups
- 2 or more days per week

**For Additional Benefits:**
- More than 300 minutes moderate-intensity
- OR more than 150 minutes vigorous-intensity

**Key Messaging Points:**
1. "Some physical activity is better than none"
2. "More is generally better, up to a point"
3. "All types of activity count"
4. "Breaking up sitting time has benefits"

### What This Means for FitApp

| WHO Says | FitApp Translates To |
|----------|----------------------|
| 150 min/week moderate | ~20-30 min most days |
| Muscle-strengthening 2x/week | Two resistance sessions |
| Some > None | Start small, consistency matters |
| Break sitting | Movement reminders |

### FitApp Philosophy Alignment

FitApp targets **20-30 minutes per session** which aligns with:
- WHO minimum (achievable for beginners)
- Sustainable habit formation
- "Minimum effective dose" concept
- Time-poverty reality

## Egypt Physical Activity Statistics

### Source Context
Data from WHO Eastern Mediterranean Regional Office (EMRO), World Bank, and published studies.

### Key Statistics (for awareness, not diagnosis)

**Physical Inactivity Prevalence:**
- Egypt has among the highest physical inactivity rates in the Eastern Mediterranean region
- Estimated 30-40% of adults do not meet minimum physical activity guidelines
- Urban areas show higher inactivity rates than rural
- Women report higher inactivity than men

**Contributing Factors (Egyptian Context):**
- Hot climate reducing outdoor activity
- Urban density and limited green spaces
- Car-centric city design
- Cultural factors (varying by region)
- Limited awareness of exercise benefits
- Time pressures from work/commute

### Why This Matters for FitApp

| Challenge | FitApp Solution |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Hot climate | Home workout options |
| Limited space | Bodyweight/no equipment programs |
| Time pressure | 20-30 minute efficient sessions |
| Limited awareness | Educational content, gradual onboarding |
| Cultural factors | Local language, relevant content |

## Sedentary Behavior Guidelines

### What is Sedentary Behavior?

Prolonged sitting or reclining with low energy expenditure. This is SEPARATE from not exercising - someone can exercise daily but still be sedentary if they sit 10+ hours.

### Key Findings for Messaging

**Research suggests:**
- Prolonged uninterrupted sitting associated with health risks
- Breaking up sitting time has benefits
- Light activity (walking, standing) helps
- Risk reduction is independent of exercise

### FitApp Application

**Movement Reminders Philosophy:**
- Not about intense exercise
- About breaking sitting patterns
- Light movement counts
- 2-5 minute breaks make difference

**Suggested Break Activities:**
- Walk to get water
- Stand while on phone
- Light stretching
- Mobility drills
- Walking meetings

**Messaging Tone:**
- Encouraging, not alarming
- "Movement break" not "you're being unhealthy"
- Options, not demands
- Respect user autonomy

## Evidence-Based Messaging Guidelines

### DO Use These Messages

**Positive Framing:**
- "Movement improves how you feel"
- "Exercise supports your energy levels"
- "Strength training helps maintain muscle as we age"
- "Regular activity supports sleep quality"
- "20 minutes can make a difference"

**Inclusive Language:**
- "All movement counts"
- "Start where you are"
- "Progress at your own pace"
- "Any activity is better than none"
- "Find what works for YOUR life"

### DO NOT Use These Messages

**Avoid:**
- "Exercise will cure/treat/fix [condition]"
- "You need to lose weight to be healthy"
- Fear-based health messaging
- Guarantees about health outcomes
- Disease-specific claims
- Body shaming or guilt
- "You should" language excessively

## App Messaging Templates

### Onboarding Messages

**Welcome:**
"FitApp is designed to help you build sustainable exercise habits. Whether you have 20 minutes or 60, there's a path forward."

**Setting Expectations:**
"Health organizations recommend around 150 minutes of activity per week. That's about 20-30 minutes most days. We'll help you build up to that at your own pace."

**First Steps:**
"The most important step is the first one. Even a short workout is a win."

### Motivation Messages

**Low Activity Period:**
"Haven't worked out in a while? That's okay. Today is a new opportunity. Even 10 minutes counts."

**Consistency:**
"You've been showing up. Consistency is the foundation of progress."

**Achievement:**
"Another workout completed. Your body adapts through consistent effort."

### Movement Reminders

**Gentle:**
"Been sitting a while? A quick stretch or short walk can help you feel refreshed."

**Educational:**
"Breaking up sitting time throughout the day has benefits separate from your workouts."

**Optional:**
"Movement break suggestion - take it if it works for you, skip if you're in flow."

## Handling Sensitive Topics

### Weight and Body Composition

**FitApp Approach:**
- Focus on strength, energy, capability
- Don't make weight loss the primary goal
- Acknowledge body diversity
- No before/after body comparison features
- Celebrate performance improvements

**If User Mentions Weight Goals:**
- Acknowledge their goal respectfully
- Redirect to sustainable habits
- Emphasize overall health markers
- Don't promise specific results

### Medical Conditions

**FitApp Approach:**
- Do NOT provide disease-specific advice
- Recommend consulting healthcare provider
- Offer general fitness guidance only
- Have clear disclaimers

**Standard Disclaimer:**
"FitApp provides general fitness information and is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Consult a healthcare provider before starting any exercise program, especially if you have existing health conditions."
