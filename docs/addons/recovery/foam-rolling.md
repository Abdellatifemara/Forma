# Foam Rolling Guide

## Foam Rolling Basics

### How It Works
- Self-myofascial release (SMR)
- Releases muscle tension
- Improves blood flow
- Reduces muscle soreness
- Increases ROM temporarily

### Guidelines
| Guideline | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Pressure | Moderate (4-7/10 discomfort) |
| Duration | 30-60 seconds per area |
| Speed | Slow, controlled rolls |
| Frequency | Daily or pre/post workout |
| Avoid | Bones, joints, lower back |

### Foam Roller Types
| Type | Density | Best For |
|------|---------|----------|
| Soft (white) | Low | Beginners, sensitive |
| Medium (blue) | Medium | Most people |
| Firm (black) | High | Experienced, dense tissue |
| Textured | Varies | Deeper massage |
| Vibrating | Medium | Enhanced recovery |

## Upper Body Foam Rolling

### Upper Back (Thoracic Spine)
**How to:**
1. Roller under upper back
2. Hands behind head
3. Lift hips off ground
4. Roll from mid-back to shoulders
5. Pause on tight spots
6. 60 seconds

**Do:** Cross arms for deeper pressure
**Don't:** Roll lower back

### Lats
**How to:**
1. Lie on side, roller under armpit
2. Bottom arm extended overhead
3. Roll from armpit to mid-ribcage
4. Rotate body to hit different angles
5. 30-45 seconds each side

### Chest/Pecs
**How to:**
1. Lie face down
2. Roller angled under chest
3. Arm extended out
4. Roll across pec muscle
5. 30 seconds each side

**Alternative:** Use lacrosse ball against wall

## Lower Body Foam Rolling

### Quads
**How to:**
1. Forearm plank position
2. Roller under thighs
3. Roll from hip to just above knee
4. Turn to hit inner/outer quad
5. 60 seconds

**Tip:** Do one leg at a time for more pressure

### IT Band
**How to:**
1. Side position, roller under outer thigh
2. Bottom leg straight, top leg in front
3. Roll from hip to just above knee
4. 45-60 seconds each side

### Hamstrings
**How to:**
1. Sit on roller, legs extended
2. Hands behind for support
3. Roll from glutes to behind knee
4. Rotate leg in/out to hit all areas
5. 45 seconds each leg

**Tip:** Cross one leg over for more pressure

### Glutes
**How to:**
1. Sit on roller
2. Cross one ankle over opposite knee
3. Lean toward crossed leg side
4. Roll around glute muscle
5. 45 seconds each side

**Alternative:** Lacrosse ball for deeper work

### Calves
**How to:**
1. Sit on floor, roller under calves
2. Hands behind for support
3. Lift hips, roll from ankle to knee
4. Rotate foot in/out
5. 45 seconds each leg

### Hip Flexors
**How to:**
1. Lie face down
2. Roller under front of hip
3. Support on forearms
4. Roll small movements
5. 30-45 seconds each side

**Caution:** Sensitive area, lighter pressure

## Pre-Workout Rolling (5 min)
Focus on areas you'll train:
- **Push day:** Upper back, chest, lats
- **Pull day:** Upper back, lats
- **Leg day:** Quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves
- **Full body:** Quick pass on all major areas

## Post-Workout Rolling (5-10 min)
Focus on muscles worked:
- Roll each trained muscle 45-60 seconds
- Focus on tender spots
- Follow with static stretching

## Lacrosse Ball Work

### Best For
- Glutes (deeper than foam roller)
- Upper back (between shoulder blades)
- Pecs (against wall)
- Feet (plantar fascia)
- Traps and neck

### Glute Release
1. Sit on ball
2. Cross leg over knee
3. Roll around glute
4. Hold on trigger points
5. 60-90 seconds each side

### Upper Back Release
1. Ball between spine and shoulder blade
2. Lean against wall
3. Roll around to find tight spots
4. Hold tender areas 20-30 seconds
