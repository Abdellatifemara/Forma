# Yoga Poses - Complete Reference

Comprehensive guide to yoga poses (asanas) with Sanskrit names, technique, contraindications, and modifications.

---

## STANDING POSES

### Key Principles
- Root through all four corners of feet (big toe mound, little toe mound, inner heel, outer heel)
- Engage quadriceps to lift kneecaps and protect knees
- Maintain neutral pelvis
- Never hold your breath

---

### Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 2 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Core stabilizers, quadriceps, calves |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Gluteus medius, spinal erectors |

**Technique:**
1. Stand feet hip-width or together, weight evenly distributed
2. Spread toes, ground through all four corners of each foot
3. Engage quadriceps, lifting kneecaps without hyperextending
4. Tuck tailbone slightly, engage lower abdominals
5. Roll shoulders back and down, opening chest
6. Arms hang at sides, palms forward
7. Lengthen through crown of head, chin parallel to floor

**Alignment Cues:**
- Ears over shoulders, shoulders over hips, hips over ankles
- Feel string pulling crown toward ceiling

**Common Mistakes:**
- Locking/hyperextending knees
- Excessive lumbar curve
- Shoulders rounded forward
- Weight too far forward or back

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Back against wall for feedback
- *Easier:* Feet hip-width for balance
- *Props:* Block between thighs for adductor engagement

**Contraindications:**
- Severe balance issues → use wall support
- Low blood pressure → don't hold too long

### Virabhadrasana I (Warrior I)

**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes, hip flexors (stretched), deltoids |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hamstrings, calves, core, trapezius |

**Technique:**
1. From Tadasana, step left foot back 3.5-4 feet
2. Turn back foot out 45-60°, grounding outer edge
3. Align front heel with back heel (or wider for stability)
4. Bend front knee to 90°, tracking over ankle
5. Square hips toward front
6. Raise arms overhead, palms facing or touching
7. Draw shoulders down from ears
8. Slight backbend, lift chest and gaze up

**Alignment Cues:**
- Front thigh parallel to floor (work toward)
- Back leg straight and strong
- Hips squared forward (the challenge)
- Tailbone lengthens down

**Common Mistakes:**
- Front knee collapsing inward
- Back heel lifting
- Hips not squared (rotating open)
- Overarching lower back
- Shoulders hiking

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Shorten stance, hands on hips
- *Easier:* Back knee slightly bent
- *Props:* Back heel against wall

**Contraindications:**
- High blood pressure → arms at heart center
- Shoulder injuries → hands on hips
- Neck problems → don't look up
- Knee injuries → reduce lunge depth

### Virabhadrasana II (Warrior II)

**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes, hip abductors, deltoids |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hamstrings, core, trapezius |

**Technique:**
1. From Tadasana, step feet wide (4-5 feet)
2. Turn right foot out 90°, left foot in 15-30°
3. Align front heel with back arch
4. Bend right knee to 90°, tracking over second toe
5. Extend arms parallel to floor
6. Stack shoulders over hips
7. Turn head, gaze over front middle finger
8. Press outer edge of back foot down

**Alignment Cues:**
- Front thigh works toward parallel
- Back leg straight, kneecap lifted
- Torso centered (not leaning forward)
- Arms active, pulled opposite directions

**Common Mistakes:**
- Front knee collapsing inward
- Torso leaning over front leg
- Back foot rolling in
- Arms drooping

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Shorten stance, reduce knee bend
- *Props:* Back against wall

**Contraindications:**
- Neck problems → gaze forward
- High blood pressure → don't hold long
- Knee injuries → reduce depth

---

### Virabhadrasana III (Warrior III)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate-Advanced | **Hold:** 15-30 sec per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hamstrings, glutes, erector spinae, core |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Quadriceps (standing leg), deltoids |

**Technique:**
1. From Warrior I, shift weight onto front foot
2. Hinge forward at hips, lifting back leg
3. Extend through raised leg, flex foot (toes down)
4. Arms forward alongside ears, or at heart center
5. Create one line from fingertips to back heel
6. Hips remain squared to floor
7. Gaze at floor 4 feet ahead

**Alignment Cues:**
- Standing leg straight or micro-bend
- Raised leg hip tends to open - internally rotate to level
- Torso parallel to floor
- Core engaged strongly

**Common Mistakes:**
- Raised leg hip opening
- Standing leg hyperextended
- Lower back overarching
- Collapsing chest

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Hands on blocks, wall, or chair
- *Easier:* Lower the lifted leg
- *Props:* Wall for support

**Contraindications:**
- Balance disorders → use wall
- Lower back injuries → limit forward hinge
- High blood pressure

### Trikonasana (Triangle Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hamstrings, hip abductors, obliques |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glute med, erectors |

**Technique:**
1. From Warrior II setup, straighten front leg
2. Reach forward through front arm, lengthening torso
3. Hinge at hip (not waist), tilt over front leg
4. Lower front hand to shin, ankle, block, or floor
5. Extend top arm toward ceiling
6. Stack shoulders vertically
7. Turn head to gaze at top hand
8. Both legs straight and engaged

**Alignment Cues:**
- Imagine between two panes of glass
- Front thigh externally rotates
- Weight even between both feet
- Don't collapse bottom side

**Common Mistakes:**
- Bending front knee
- Collapsing torso toward floor
- Rounding spine
- Reaching too far down

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Hand on shin or block
- *Easier:* Gaze forward or down
- *Props:* Block under bottom hand

**Contraindications:**
- Low blood pressure → come up slowly
- Neck injuries → gaze forward/down
- Headaches/migraines
- Diarrhea

### Utkatasana (Chair Pose)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes, core, deltoids |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Calves, hip flexors, erectors |

**Technique:**
1. From Tadasana, bend knees deeply (sitting in chair)
2. Shift weight back into heels
3. Keep knees tracking over toes
4. Tuck tailbone slightly, engage lower abs
5. Raise arms overhead, biceps by ears
6. Keep shoulders down from ears
7. Gaze forward or slightly up

**Alignment Cues:**
- Weight in heels (can lift toes)
- Knees don't pass toes
- Inner thighs spiral slightly inward
- Lower ribs draw in

**Common Mistakes:**
- Weight forward to toes
- Knees collapsing inward
- Excessive lumbar curve
- Shoulders hunching

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Don't bend as deep
- *Easier:* Arms forward at shoulder height
- *Props:* Block between thighs

**Contraindications:**
- Knee injuries
- Low blood pressure
- Headaches
- Insomnia

### Uttanasana (Standing Forward Bend)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 2 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hamstrings, gastrocnemius, spinal erectors |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Glutes, hip flexors, calves |

**Technique:**
1. From Tadasana, hinge forward at hips (not waist)
2. Keep spine long, lead with chest
3. Place hands on floor, blocks, shins, or grab elbows
4. Let head hang heavy
5. Shift weight slightly toward balls of feet
6. Engage quadriceps to release hamstrings

**Alignment Cues:**
- Hinge from hip crease
- Microbend knees if hamstrings tight
- Sit bones lift toward ceiling
- Shoulders release from ears

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding lower back excessively
- Locking knees
- Forcing depth
- Holding tension in neck

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Generous knee bend
- *Easier:* Hands on blocks or shins
- *Deeper:* Belly toward thighs

**Contraindications:**
- Lower back injuries → keep knees bent
- Hamstring injuries
- High blood pressure → don't hold long
- Glaucoma or detached retina

---

### Vrksasana (Tree Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hip abductors, core, ankle stabilizers |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Quadriceps, glutes |

**Technique:**
1. Stand in Tadasana, shift weight to left foot
2. Place right foot on inner left thigh or calf (NOT knee)
3. Press foot into thigh, thigh into foot
4. Hands at heart center or overhead
5. Find a focal point (drishti) for balance
6. Keep hips level

**Alignment Cues:**
- Standing leg straight, not hyperextended
- Hip of lifted leg opens (external rotation)
- Pelvis stays level
- Spine tall

**Common Mistakes:**
- Foot on knee (dangerous)
- Standing hip popping out
- Holding breath
- Looking around

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Foot on calf or kickstand (toes on floor)
- *Easier:* Hand on wall
- *Props:* Wall for support

**Contraindications:**
- Balance disorders → use wall
- Ankle/knee injuries on standing leg

## SEATED POSES

### Dandasana (Staff Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Core, hip flexors, quadriceps |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Erectors, wrist extensors |

**Technique:**
1. Sit with legs extended straight forward
2. Flex feet, toes pointing up
3. Place hands beside hips, fingers forward
4. Press palms into floor, lift chest
5. Lengthen spine toward ceiling
6. Engage quadriceps, press backs of knees down
7. Shoulders back and down

**Alignment Cues:**
- 90° angle at hips
- Spine vertical
- Chest lifted
- Shoulders over hips

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding lower back
- Collapsing chest
- Knees bending
- Shoulders hunching

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Sit on folded blanket
- *Easier:* Slight knee bend
- *Props:* Blanket under hips

**Contraindications:**
- Wrist injuries → fists or fingertips
- Lower back injuries → sit on blanket

---

### Paschimottanasana (Seated Forward Bend)

**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate | **Hold:** 1-3 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hamstrings, erector spinae, gastrocnemius |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Glutes, hip flexors |

**Technique:**
1. From Dandasana, inhale and lengthen spine
2. Exhale, hinge forward from hips
3. Lead with chest, not head
4. Reach for feet, ankles, shins, or use strap
5. Keep spine as long as possible
6. Relax head and neck
7. Breathe into the stretch

**Alignment Cues:**
- Fold from hips, not waist
- Keep spine long (even if not going deep)
- Feet flexed
- Shoulders relaxed

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding entire spine
- Pulling with arms (straining)
- Holding breath
- Forcing depth

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Bend knees generously
- *Easier:* Strap around feet
- *Props:* Blanket under hips, strap around feet

**Contraindications:**
- Herniated disc
- Sciatica (modify with bent knees)
- Hamstring injuries
- Pregnancy (wide legs variation)

---

### Baddha Konasana (Bound Angle / Butterfly)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 1-5 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hip adductors, hip external rotators |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Pelvic floor, lower back |

**Technique:**
1. Sit with soles of feet together
2. Draw heels toward pelvis (comfortable distance)
3. Hold ankles or feet
4. Let knees fall open toward floor
5. Lengthen spine, lift chest
6. Option: fold forward with flat back

**Alignment Cues:**
- Sit bones grounded
- Spine tall before folding
- Knees release with gravity (don't force)
- Shoulders relaxed

**Common Mistakes:**
- Forcing knees down
- Rounding spine
- Gripping feet too tightly
- Holding breath

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Sit on blanket
- *Easier:* Blocks under knees
- *Props:* Support knees with blocks

**Contraindications:**
- Groin injuries
- Knee injuries
- Sciatica

---

### Ardha Matsyendrasana (Half Lord of Fishes / Seated Twist)

**Difficulty:** Beginner-Intermediate | **Hold:** 30-60 sec per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Spinal rotators, obliques |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Glutes, erectors |

**Technique:**
1. Sit with legs extended
2. Bend right knee, cross foot over left leg
3. Place right foot flat outside left thigh
4. Option: bend left knee, heel near right hip
5. Inhale, lengthen spine
6. Exhale, twist to right
7. Left elbow outside right knee or hug knee
8. Right hand behind for support
9. Look over right shoulder

**Alignment Cues:**
- Sit bones stay grounded
- Spine lengthens BEFORE twisting
- Twist from thoracic, not lumbar
- Both shoulders level

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding spine while twisting
- Forcing with arms
- One hip lifting
- Collapsing chest

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Keep bottom leg straight
- *Easier:* Don't cross foot over
- *Props:* Sit on blanket

**Contraindications:**
- Spinal disc injuries
- SI joint dysfunction
- Pregnancy → gentle only
- Recent abdominal surgery

## FLOOR POSES

### Balasana (Child's Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 1-5 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Spinal erectors, lats (stretched) |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Glutes, hip flexors |

**Technique:**
1. Kneel on floor, big toes together
2. Knees together or wide apart
3. Sit hips back toward heels
4. Fold forward, forehead to floor
5. Arms extended forward or alongside body
6. Relax completely

**Alignment Cues:**
- Hips sink toward heels
- Forehead rests on floor (or prop)
- Shoulders release away from ears
- Breathe into lower back

**Common Mistakes:**
- Hips lifting away from heels
- Holding tension in shoulders
- Shallow breathing

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Cushion between hips and heels
- *Easier:* Blanket under knees
- *Props:* Block under forehead

**Contraindications:**
- Knee injuries → cushion behind knees
- Pregnancy → wide knees, avoid belly pressure
- High blood pressure → head supported

---

### Marjaryasana-Bitilasana (Cat-Cow)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Reps:** 10-20 cycles

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Spinal flexors/extensors, core |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Neck muscles, hip flexors |

**Technique:**

**Starting Position:**
1. Hands and knees (tabletop)
2. Wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
3. Spine neutral

**Cow (Bitilasana) - Inhale:**
1. Drop belly toward floor
2. Lift chest and tailbone
3. Gaze forward or slightly up

**Cat (Marjaryasana) - Exhale:**
1. Round spine toward ceiling
2. Tuck tailbone, drop head
3. Draw belly button to spine

**Alignment Cues:**
- Move with breath
- Initiate from pelvis
- Movement flows through entire spine

**Common Mistakes:**
- Moving too fast
- Only moving middle back
- Holding breath
- Collapsing into wrists

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Smaller range of motion
- *Wrist issues:* Fists or forearms down
- *Props:* Blanket under knees

**Contraindications:**
- Neck injuries → keep head neutral
- Wrist injuries → use fists or forearms

### Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward-Facing Dog)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 2 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Hamstrings, calves, deltoids, lats |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Core, triceps, forearms |

**Technique:**
1. Start on hands and knees
2. Hands shoulder-width, fingers spread wide
3. Tuck toes, lift hips up and back
4. Press heels toward floor (don't need to touch)
5. Arms straight, biceps rotate toward ears
6. Head between upper arms, neck relaxed
7. Create inverted V shape

**Alignment Cues:**
- Hands press evenly (don't dump into wrists)
- Shoulders away from ears
- Spine long, may have slight bend in knees
- Weight distributed between hands and feet

**Common Mistakes:**
- Rounding upper back
- Shoulders by ears
- Locking knees with rounded back
- Weight forward into hands

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Bend knees generously
- *Easier:* Hands on blocks
- *Props:* Blocks under hands

**Contraindications:**
- Wrist injuries → use fists or forearms (dolphin)
- High blood pressure → limit time
- Late pregnancy
- Detached retina

### Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 15-30 sec

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Erector spinae, glutes |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Triceps, anterior deltoids |

**Technique:**
1. Lie face down, legs together
2. Place palms under shoulders, elbows close to body
3. Press tops of feet into floor
4. Inhale, lift chest using back muscles
5. Keep elbows slightly bent
6. Shoulders down from ears
7. Gaze forward or slightly up

**Alignment Cues:**
- Lift with back muscles (not arm push)
- Pubic bone stays on floor
- Legs engaged, not clenched
- Elongate spine, don't crunch lower back

**Common Mistakes:**
- Pushing up with arms only
- Crunching lower back
- Shoulders at ears
- Splaying elbows

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Baby Cobra (very low lift)
- *Easier:* Forearms down (Sphinx)
- *Deeper:* Straighten arms more

**Contraindications:**
- Pregnancy
- Back injuries
- Carpal tunnel → use fists
- Recent abdominal surgery

### Setu Bandhasana (Bridge Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 30 sec - 1 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Glutes, hamstrings, erector spinae |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Quadriceps, hip flexors (stretched) |

**Technique:**
1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat
2. Feet hip-width, heels near sitting bones
3. Arms alongside body, palms down
4. Press feet and arms into floor
5. Lift hips toward ceiling
6. Roll shoulders under, clasp hands (optional)
7. Keep thighs parallel

**Alignment Cues:**
- Knees over ankles
- Thighs parallel (don't let knees splay)
- Weight in feet, not neck
- Chest moves toward chin

**Common Mistakes:**
- Knees splaying outward
- Weight on neck
- Clenching glutes excessively
- Feet too far from hips

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Lower lift
- *Easier:* Arms by sides
- *Props:* Block under sacrum (supported bridge)

**Contraindications:**
- Neck injuries → keep head still
- Shoulder injuries → don't clasp hands
- Late pregnancy

### Eka Pada Rajakapotasana (Pigeon Pose)

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Hold:** 1-3 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Piriformis, glutes, hip external rotators |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Hip flexors (back leg), psoas |

**Technique:**
1. From downward dog, bring right knee forward
2. Place right knee behind right wrist
3. Right ankle toward left wrist (angle depends on flexibility)
4. Extend left leg straight back, top of foot down
5. Square hips to front as much as possible
6. Walk hands forward, fold over front leg
7. Rest forehead on hands, block, or floor

**Alignment Cues:**
- Front shin angle varies with flexibility
- Hips level and squared
- Back leg straight, not rotated
- Relax into the pose

**Common Mistakes:**
- Front hip lifting
- Back leg rotating outward
- Forcing depth
- Knee pain (modify immediately)

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Block under front hip
- *Easier:* Reclined pigeon (lying down)
- *Easier:* Keep torso upright
- *Props:* Blanket under front hip

**Contraindications:**
- Knee injuries → use reclined version
- Sacroiliac issues
- Ankle injuries

### Supta Matsyendrasana (Supine Twist)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 1-3 min per side

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Muscles** | Spinal rotators, obliques |
| **Secondary Muscles** | Glutes, chest (stretched) |

**Technique:**
1. Lie on back, arms out in T
2. Draw right knee to chest
3. Guide right knee across body to left
4. Keep right shoulder grounded
5. Turn head to right (optional)
6. Let gravity do the work
7. Repeat other side

**Alignment Cues:**
- Both shoulders stay on floor
- Knee doesn't need to touch floor
- Breathe into the twist
- Relax completely

**Common Mistakes:**
- Forcing knee to floor
- Lifting opposite shoulder
- Holding breath
- Rushing out of pose

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Pillow under knee
- *Easier:* Both knees bent, drop to side
- *Props:* Block or blanket under knee

**Contraindications:**
- Spinal disc injuries
- SI joint dysfunction
- Pregnancy → gentle, supported only

### Savasana (Corpse Pose)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 5-15 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Purpose** | Complete relaxation, integration |

**Technique:**
1. Lie flat on back
2. Legs extended, feet fall open
3. Arms at sides, palms up
4. Close eyes
5. Release all muscular effort
6. Let breath be natural
7. Scan body, release tension

**Alignment Cues:**
- Symmetrical body position
- Space between arms and body
- Shoulders release from ears
- Jaw unclenches

**Common Mistakes:**
- Fidgeting
- Falling asleep (sometimes okay)
- Mind wandering without returning
- Skipping it

**Modifications:**
- *Comfort:* Bolster under knees
- *Comfort:* Blanket for warmth
- *Lower back:* Knees bent, feet flat

**Contraindications:**
- Pregnancy after first trimester → lie on left side
- GERD → slight incline
- Lower back pain → bolster under knees

## INVERSIONS

### Viparita Karani (Legs Up the Wall)

**Difficulty:** Beginner | **Hold:** 5-15 min

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Primary Benefits** | Circulation, relaxation, leg relief |

**Technique:**
1. Sit sideways against wall
2. Swing legs up wall as you lie back
3. Scoot hips close to wall (or few inches away)
4. Arms out to sides or on belly
5. Close eyes, relax completely

**Alignment Cues:**
- Hips can touch wall or be few inches away
- Lower back comfortable
- Shoulders relaxed on floor

**Modifications:**
- *Easier:* Hips further from wall
- *Props:* Bolster under hips

**Contraindications:**
- Glaucoma
- Uncontrolled high blood pressure
- Menstruation (some traditions)
- Hiatal hernia

## SAFETY GUIDELINES

### General Rules
1. **Never force** - yoga should not cause pain
2. **Breathe** - if you can't breathe smoothly, back off
3. **Warm up** - don't attempt advanced poses cold
4. **Listen to your body** - modify as needed
5. **Progress gradually** - flexibility takes time

### When to STOP
- Sharp pain
- Numbness or tingling
- Dizziness
- Difficulty breathing
- Joint pain (vs. muscle stretch)

### High-Risk Populations
| Population | Considerations |
|------------|----------------|
| Pregnancy | Avoid deep twists, prone poses, inversions |
| High blood pressure | Avoid inversions, long holds |
| Glaucoma | Avoid inversions, forward folds |
| Osteoporosis | Avoid deep forward folds, twists |
| Recent surgery | Get medical clearance |

### Props Guide
| Prop | Uses |
|------|------|
| **Blocks** | Bring floor closer, support, alignment |
| **Straps** | Extend reach, deepen stretches safely |
| **Blankets** | Cushion knees, support hips, warmth |
| **Bolsters** | Restorative support, comfort |
| **Wall** | Balance, alignment feedback |
